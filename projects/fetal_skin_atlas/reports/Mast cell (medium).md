**Justification:** The 'Mast cell (medium)' cluster represents an intermediate maturation state in the fetal skin mast cell series, between the earliest progenitor-like stage and the most mature chymase-positive state. CL:0000097 'mast cell' is the parent term encompassing all mast cell stages including immature forms. A broad match is appropriate because the intermediate state does not precisely correspond to any single sub-term in the CL mast cell hierarchy, and the granule content and effector molecule expression are intermediate rather than fully mature.

## Summary

Mast cell (medium) is an intermediate transcriptional state within the mast cell maturation series in fetal human skin (7–17 PCW) identified by the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas. This cluster represents mast cells that have begun granule biosynthesis but have not yet acquired the full effector phenotype of mature connective tissue mast cells.

Fetal skin mast cells progressively mature throughout gestation, with all stages retaining immature granule characteristics until late in the second trimester:

> "Mast cells have been detected in fetal skin as early as 8 PCW, 41 and continue to mature and expand throughout the second trimester."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

The intermediate stage corresponds to the window during which CD117+ cells are detectable but tryptase/toluidine blue staining is still negative or weakly positive:

> "Mast cells in first-and second-trimester fetal skin contain immature granules, 39,62 which is reflected in the delayed detection of positive tryptase or toluidine blue staining in the second trimester, 8,39 despite tryptase transcripts being detected from 8 PCW 41 and the presence of a distinct ª 2020 The Authors. Immunology published by John Wiley & Sons Ltd., Immunology, 160, 149-156 population of CD117 + mast cells being detected from 10 to 12 PCW."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

The transition toward mature mast cell status is a prolonged process in fetal skin, with chymase expression appearing only at the end of gestation:

> "Mature chymase + mast cells are not present until the end of the second trimester, and even then they are scarce in comparison to adult skin in which approximately 50% of mast cells are chymase + ."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

## Markers

Intermediate-stage mast cells in fetal skin are expected to express:

- **KIT (CD117)** — retained from progenitor stage; high expression
- **TPSAB1 / TPSB2 (tryptase)** — transcripts present; protein staining begins during second trimester
- **FcεRI** — expressed at intermediate levels
- **GATA2** — master transcription factor for mast cell identity
- **Histamine** — synthesis may commence at this stage
- **CPA3** — carboxypeptidase A3; mast cell-specific marker

Specific differentially expressed markers distinguishing the medium from the earliest and most mature mast cell states are provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

## Location

Mast cell (medium) cells are located in the dermal immune compartment of fetal skin during prenatal development (7–17 PCW). They were isolated as CD45+ immune cells by FACS prior to scRNA-seq. Like adult mast cells, fetal skin mast cells are distributed near blood vessels and hair follicle appendages, consistent with perivascular and perifollicular niches.

## Function

At the medium maturation stage, fetal skin mast cells are acquiring effector capabilities but remain functionally immature compared to adult counterparts. Key features:

- Tryptase biosynthesis is underway, contributing to extracellular matrix remodelling
- FcεRI expression allows initial IgE-dependent signalling, though functional IgE levels are low in utero
- The mast cells at this stage may contribute to immune surveillance and wound healing responses in the developing dermis
- Stem cell factor (SCF) from the skin microenvironment continues to support survival and maturation

## Structure / Morphology

Medium-stage fetal skin mast cells have cytoplasm with developing granules that may stain weakly positive or be negative for toluidine blue metachromasia. Electron microscopy of fetal skin mast cells at this stage would show partially filled secretory granules. Chymase protein is absent. KIT expression is detectable by immunohistochemistry.
