# Myocytes (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [skeletal muscle cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0008002) (CL:0008002, broad match)

## Summary

Myocytes are differentiated skeletal muscle cells identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas as a fine-grained annotation within the broad "Skeletal muscle" lineage in the fetal (7-17 PCW) dataset. Unlike the "Early myocytes" annotation which likely captures cells at the onset of differentiation, this "Myocytes" cluster represents more mature, terminally differentiated muscle cells that express the full complement of contractile machinery. In fetal skin, these cells constitute the functional units of developing arrector pili muscles and potentially the panniculus carnosus. The atlas also identifies myocytes in the skin organoid (SkO) model (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

Myocytes express mature skeletal muscle markers including MYH3 (embryonic myosin heavy chain), ACTA1, TTN (titin), TNNC2, TNNT3, and MYL1. They also express DES (desmin) and CKM (creatine kinase, muscle). Specific markers for this cluster are defined in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

Myocytes are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW) within the dermal/subcutaneous compartment:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

In skin, mature myocytes form the contractile elements of arrector pili muscles.

## Function

Myocytes are the functional contractile cells of skeletal muscle. In skin, their primary role is within the arrector pili muscles, which contract to erect hair shafts in response to cold or sympathetic stimulation. During fetal development, these cells represent the differentiated endpoint of the myogenic program, having undergone fusion and begun expressing mature contractile proteins.
