# Melanoblast (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [melanoblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000541) (CL:0000541, exact match)

## Summary

Melanoblasts are neural crest-derived pigment cell precursors identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020) and prenatal skin. In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, melanocyte lineage cells including melanoblasts and melanocytes are part of the ectoderm-derived neural compartment. Melanocytes in the SkO showed accelerated differentiation, with alignment to adult cell states as early as 4 weeks of culture. Melanoblasts represent the precursor stage before terminal differentiation into melanin-producing melanocytes.

## Markers

Melanoblasts are characterised by expression of melanocyte lineage transcription factors (MITF, SOX10) and neural crest markers, while lacking full expression of melanin biosynthesis enzymes (TYR, TYRP1, DCT) that define mature melanocytes.

## Location

In prenatal skin, melanoblasts migrate from the neural crest through the dermis to the epidermis and developing hair follicles. In the SkO, they derive from neural crest differentiation during organoid culture.

## Function

Melanoblasts serve as the progenitor pool for melanocytes. They proliferate, migrate and ultimately differentiate into melanin-producing melanocytes that populate the epidermis and hair follicles, providing pigmentation and UV protection.
