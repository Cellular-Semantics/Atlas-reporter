# Myofibroblasts (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [myofibroblast cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000186) (CL:0000186, exact match)

## Summary

Myofibroblasts are contractile mesenchymal cells that co-express fibroblastic and smooth muscle properties, most notably alpha-smooth muscle actin (αSMA). In the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, myofibroblasts are identified as a fine-grained annotation within the broad dermal stroma lineage in fetal skin (7–17 PCW). Fetal myofibroblasts in early gestation express genes associated with tissue regeneration rather than scarring, consistent with the well-documented capacity of early prenatal skin to heal without fibrosis. The atlas reveals that early-gestation dermal cells downregulate genes involved in extracellular matrix deposition and inflammation relative to adult counterparts, whereas late-gestation prenatal skin progressively acquires a more pro-fibrotic and pro-inflammatory gene expression profile. The scarless healing capacity of early fetal skin is lost after approximately 24 PCW (Larson et al., 2010). Mural and mesenchymal cells including myofibroblasts had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states even after 19 weeks of SkO culture.

## Markers

- **ACTA2 (αSMA, alpha-smooth muscle actin)**: The canonical marker of myofibroblast identity; distinguishes myofibroblasts from fibroblasts and defines the contractile phenotype. Myofibroblasts are alpha-SMA-positive, CD34-negative, CD45-negative (CL:0000186 definition).

- **POSTN (periostin)**: Expressed in late-gestation prenatal fibroblasts and at pathogenic levels in fibrotic skin disorders; marks the transition toward a pro-fibrotic mesenchymal phenotype.

  > "Notably, WNT2+ and PEAR1+ prenatal fibroblasts expressed several genes involved in cellular senescence (CDKN1A), cytokine pathways (for example, IL1R1) and collagen deposition (for example, POSTN) (Fig. 3a,b), which are highly expressed in pathogenic fibroblasts of fibrotic skin disorders"
  >
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **COL1A1**: Marker of pro-fibrotic collagen deposition; upregulated in late-gestation WNT2+ fibroblasts and relevant to myofibroblast-driven scar formation.

  > "Late gestation WNT2+ fibroblasts had upregulated genes related to extracellular matrix and collagen deposition (for example, COL1A1), whereas early WNT2+ fibroblasts had DEGs involved in cellular growth and differentiation (for example, SFRP1)"
  >
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, myofibroblasts are identified in fetal skin (7–17 PCW) as a fine-grained annotation within the dermal stroma compartment, based on DEGs listed in Supplementary Table 3. Mesoderm-derived stromal cells including myofibroblasts are present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

In early gestation, fetal skin fibroblasts and myofibroblasts downregulate pro-inflammatory and pro-fibrotic genes:

> "Prenatal skin fibroblasts had upregulated genes involved in immune suppression (CD200), regulation of inflammation (for example, RAMP2) and tissue regeneration (MDK)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Wound Healing and Scarless Repair

Myofibroblasts are central mediators of wound closure and fibrosis through αSMA-dependent contraction and ECM deposition. In early fetal skin, the pro-regenerative environment suppresses myofibroblast-driven scarring:

> "Prenatal human skin is able to heal without scarring but loses this capacity after 24 PCW"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024), citing Larson et al. (2010)

> "A combination of fibroblast and macrophage-associated molecular features potentially contribute to the ability of prenatal skin to heal without scarring, including the presence of fibroblast progenitors, a downregulated immune milieu and reduced collagen expression."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

The atlas demonstrates progressive acquisition of scar-promoting gene expression through gestation:

> "These results provide further support for our finding of progressive acquisition of scar-promoting genes in later gestation, consistent with the clinical observation of scarring in third trimester skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Comparison with Pro-regenerative Fibroblasts

> "Early-gestation human skin fibroblasts had a higher probability of correspondence to pro-regenerative reindeer fibroblasts, whereas in later gestation, the probability of matching to pro-fibrotic fibroblasts was higher"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Organoid Comparison

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
