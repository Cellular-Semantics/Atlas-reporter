# Pro B cell
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [pro-B cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000826) (CL:0000826, exact match)

## Summary
Pro-B cells are B cell progenitors at an intermediate developmental stage, identified in the fetal skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024) as part of the B cell developmental trajectory in prenatal skin. Pro-B cells are characterized by immunoglobulin heavy chain VDJ gene rearrangement and increasing expression of B cell transcripts including MS4A1 (CD20), CD79B, DNTT (terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase) and HLA-DRA (Popescu et al., 2019). In fetal haematopoiesis, pro-B cells are present in both the fetal liver and non-lymphoid tissues (NLT) including skin, indicating their ability to seed peripheral tissues during development.

## Markers

Pro-B cells in fetal skin are defined by the following markers:

- **MS4A1 (CD20)**: B cell lineage marker with increasing expression from the pro-B stage onwards (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **CD79B**: B cell receptor signalling component, expressed from the pro-B stage (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **DNTT (Terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase)**: An enzyme responsible for N-region additions in immunoglobulin gene rearrangements, expressed in pro-B cells (Popescu et al., 2019; Li and Xia, 2024).
- **HLA-DRA**: MHC class II molecule with increasing expression during B cell differentiation (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "primitive 'pre pro-B' clusters expressing CD34, SPINK2, and IGLL1 to 'pro-B' and 'pre-B' clusters with increasing expression of B cell transcripts MS4A1, CD79B, DNTT and HLA-DRA and reducing expression of JCHAIN and LTB"
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

## Location

### Fetal Skin

- Pro-B cells are identified in fetal skin as part of the B cell developmental trajectory (Supplementary Tables 3-4, Gopee et al., 2024).
- In fetal liver, pro-B cells are detected from 7-8 PCW and are present in NLT including skin (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "Comparing liver with yolk sac and NLT, pro-B, pre-B and B cells are present in NLT but HSC/MPPs and pre pro-B cells are absent."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

### Fetal Liver and Omentum

- In the human fetal omentum and liver, the pro/pre-B cell compartment is detected as early as 8 weeks of gestation, with a pro/pre-B:B cell ratio of 4:1 during the 10-12 week period (Solvason and Kearney, 1992).

## Function

### Immunoglobulin Gene Rearrangement

Pro-B cells undergo VDJ rearrangement of the immunoglobulin heavy chain locus. A proper VDJ rearrangement is necessary for pro-B cells to differentiate into pre-B cells; pro-B cells without functional VDJ rearrangement undergo apoptosis or are phagocytized by bone marrow macrophages (Li and Xia, 2024).

> "A process of gene recombination occurs through the rearrangement of genes coding for segments V (Variable), D (Diversity), and J (Joining) of the heavy chain."
>
> -- Li and Xia (2024)

### Transcriptional Regulation

Differentially regulated genes in the HSC/MPP to B cell transition include transcription factors SPIB, SP100 and CTSS, which play roles in B cell lineage commitment and differentiation (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "'Pro/pre-B' cell clusters have high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio, immature chromatin and nucleoli."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

## Structure / Morphology

- Pro-B cells are characterized by a high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio, immature chromatin and nucleoli, typical of actively rearranging lymphoid precursors (Popescu et al., 2019).
