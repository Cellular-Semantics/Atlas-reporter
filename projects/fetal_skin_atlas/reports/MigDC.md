# Migratory Dendritic Cell (MigDC) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [dendritic cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000451) (CL:0000451, broad match — no exact CL term for the skin migratory DC state as defined by Reynolds et al.)

## Summary

MigDC (migratory dendritic cell) represents a convergent mature DC state identified by Reynolds et al. (2021) at the transcriptional intersection of monocyte-derived DC (moDC), Langerhans cell (LC), and conventional DC lineages. Rather than representing a fixed DC subset, MigDC is a shared maturation programme adopted by multiple DC types upon activation and licensing for lymph node migration. The defining markers are CCR7 (migration chemokine receptor), LAMP3 (DC maturation), CD83, CD40, and CD86 (maturation and co-stimulation), together with immunoregulatory genes (CD274/PD-L1, IDO1, SOCS1) and cytoskeletal/migratory machinery (FSCN1). Reynolds et al. demonstrate that this programme is conserved across species, present in multiple human inflammatory disease states, and shares features with fetal thymic medullary DCs — indicating a developmentally conserved DC maturation programme co-opted in adult homeostasis and augmented in disease.

## Markers

> "The clusters at the convergence of these
> branches (moDC3, LC4 and migratory DC (Mig. DC)) express transcripts associated with
> DC maturation (CD83, CCR7, LAMP3, CD40, CD86), immunoregulation (CD274, IDO1,
> CD200, PDCD1LG2, SOCS1) and migration (FSCN1, PLGRKT,
> TRAF1, BCL2A1, CFLAR and REL)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

DC maturation markers:
- **CCR7** — chemokine receptor driving DC migration to lymph nodes via CCL19/CCL21 gradients
- **LAMP3 (CD208)** — lysosomal membrane protein; DC maturation marker
- **CD83** — DC maturation surface marker
- **CD40** — co-stimulatory molecule; required for T cell activation
- **CD86** — co-stimulatory molecule (B7-2); T cell activation ligand

Immunoregulatory markers:
- **CD274 (PD-L1)** — programmed death-ligand 1; T cell inhibitory checkpoint
- **IDO1** — indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase 1; tryptophan catabolism; immune tolerance
- **CD200** — immunomodulatory ligand
- **PDCD1LG2 (PD-L2)** — programmed death-ligand 2; T cell inhibitory checkpoint
- **SOCS1** — suppressor of cytokine signalling 1; immunoregulatory

Migration-associated markers:
- **FSCN1** — fascin; actin-bundling protein required for DC dendrite formation and migration
- **PLGRKT, TRAF1, BCL2A1, CFLAR, REL** — survival and migratory signalling

## Location

MigDC cells are in transit between the skin and the draining lymph nodes. In the skin, they are found in the dermis during the process of maturation and CCR7-driven egress. Reynolds et al. note that in the steady state, skin DCs undergo continuous homeostatic maturation:

> "In the steady state, skin DCs undergo a continual process of homeostatic maturation
> that is required to induce tolerance to innocuous"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Upon maturation, MigDC upregulates CCR7 and FSCN1 and loses markers conferring DC subset identity before migrating to the paracortex of skin-draining lymph nodes where they present antigen to naïve T cells.

## Function

### 1. Antigen presentation and adaptive immune activation

MigDC are professional antigen-presenting cells that carry processed antigen from the skin to regional lymph nodes. Their expression of CD40, CD83, and CD86 provides the co-stimulatory signals required to activate naïve T cells. The simultaneous expression of PD-L1, PD-L2, and IDO1 equips MigDC to induce regulatory T cell responses and peripheral tolerance, giving them a dual role in immunity and tolerance.

### 2. Convergent DC maturation programme

Reynolds et al. demonstrate that the MigDC transcriptional programme is acquired by multiple DC lineages — moDC, LC, and DC2 — upon maturation, with concomitant loss of lineage-defining genes:

> "Acquisition of this common gene signature is associated with loss
> of genes conferring subset identity"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

This convergence reflects a shared terminal maturation state rather than a fixed lineage.

### 3. Developmentally conserved programme

The MigDC signature is shared with fetal thymic medullary DCs, suggesting it represents a developmental DC gene programme utilised in adult tissue homeostasis:

> "We
> previously reported the expression of migratory genes in fetal thymic medullary DCs,
> suggesting that developmental gene programs are utilized in adult tissue homeostasis and
> augmented in disease"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

## Disease relevance

The migratory DC signature is present across multiple human inflammatory and malignant conditions:

> "Surprisingly, the migratory DC signature is also present across disease states including
> tonsillitis, ascites, lung cancer and rheumatoid arthritis"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

The conservation of this programme across disease contexts — including autoimmunity, infection, and cancer — indicates that the MigDC maturation state is a broadly deployed response to immune activation, augmented beyond its homeostatic baseline in disease. Cross-species conservation (murine dermal DC2 and splenic DC1) further confirms that MigDC represents a fundamental DC maturation programme.
