# Langerhans Cell 3 (LC_3)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Langerhans cell 3 (LC_3), annotated as "Langerhans cell 3" in the prenatal and adult human skin atlas, represents a specific cluster of epidermal Langerhans cells identified within both prenatal and adult skin contexts (Atlas Supplementary Information). Langerhans cells (LCs) are specialized, tissue-resident immune cells comprising a minor but significant fraction of epidermal nucleated cells. Hallmark marker expression for LCs includes CD207 (Langerin) and CD1a, both critical for cellular identification and immunological function. Morphologically, LCs are dendritic "stellate" cells distributed predominantly within the stratum spinosum and basal layers of the epidermis, where they play a sentinel role in skin immune surveillance, antigen presentation, and immune regulation.

## Markers

Extensive evidence supports several defining markers for Langerhans cells, including:

- **CD207 (Langerin):** Expression of CD207 is definitive for LCs, as noted by its use in distinguishing these cells in skin single-cell analysis.
  
  > "Among these, Langerhans cells (cluster 10) were identifiable based on their expression of CD207."
  >
  > — Dhariwala et al. (2020)
  
  Functionally, Langerin (CD207) is a C-type lectin receptor that mediates antigen capture and is involved in forming Birbeck granules, organelles unique to LCs.

  > "LCs express the C-type lectin Langerin (CD207), which is involved in the formation of Birbeck granules, endosomal organelles that are typical for LCs."
  >
  > — Deckers et al. (2018)

- **CD1a:** CD1a is a highly characteristic marker of LCs, essential for the presentation of lipid antigens and is commonly used in both diagnostic and research settings to specifically detect LCs in tissue.

  > "The expression of CD1a molecules on the LC surface is a characteristic feature of this cell line. Monoclonal Anti-Human HLA-DR antibodies and Anti-Human CD1a antibodies can be considered a good marker of Langerhans cells [15,16]."
  >
  > — Zegarska et al. (2017)
  
  > "Langerhans cell histiocytosis (LCH) is a disease characterized by the proliferation of Langerhans cells that express an immunophenotype positive for S100 protein, CD1 (CD1a), and Langerin (CD207) and contain cytoplasmic Birbeck granules [1]."
  >
  > — Yang et al. (2023)

- **HLA-DR:** This MHC class II molecule is also used as a marker for LCs, reflecting their role as antigen-presenting cells.

  > "Monoclonal Anti-Human HLA-DR antibodies and Anti-Human CD1a antibodies can be considered a good marker of Langerhans cells [15,16]."
  >
  > — Zegarska et al. (2017)

- **S100 protein:** S100 positivity is a recognized feature in the context of disease but also serves as a diagnostic marker for LCs.

  > "Langerhans cell histiocytosis (LCH) is a disease characterized by the proliferation of Langerhans cells that express an immunophenotype positive for S100 protein, CD1 (CD1a), and Langerin (CD207) and contain cytoplasmic Birbeck granules [1]."
  >
  > — Yang et al. (2023)

## Location

### Epidermal Distribution

LC_3, as with other Langerhans cells, is situated within the epidermis of human skin. Their spatial specificity and distribution are supported by several lines of evidence:

- **Proportion and Layer Localization:**
  
  > "Langerhans cells make up 3-5% of all nucleated cells in the epidermis and occupy the stratum spinosum, a suprabasal epidermal layer"
  >
  > — Deckers et al. (2018)
  
  > "Langerhans cells (LC) constitute approximately 3-8% of all the cells in the epidermis."
  >
  > — Zegarska et al. (2017)
  
  > "They are the most abundant in the basal layer and the spinous layer of the epidermis."
  >
  > — Zegarska et al. (2017)

### Network Formation

- **Cellular Network:**

  > "Langerhans cells (LC) are a unique population of tissue-resident macrophages with dendritic cell (DC) functionality that form a network of cells across the epidermis of the skin."
  >
  > — Zhou et al. (2022)

## Function

### Immune Surveillance and Sentinel Activity

LC_3/Langerhans cells are immune sentinels at the skin surface, capable of probing the microenvironment and initiating immune responses.

> "Their location at the skin barrier suggests an important role for LC as immune sentinels at the skin surface."
>
> — Zhou et al. (2022)

### Antigen Presentation

Langerhans cells are critical antigen-presenting cells (APCs), capable of processing and presenting antigens to T cells and thus bridging innate and adaptive immunity.

> "LC function as DC-like cells that prime T cell immunity."
>
> — Zhou et al. (2022)

> "LCs are stellate cells that protrude their dendrites via tight junctions toward the stratum corneum and as such can probe for antigens across several layers of the epidermis without disrupting the permeability barrier."
>
> — Deckers et al. (2018)

### Immunoregulation

Beyond immune activation, LCs also play roles in maintaining immune tolerance and regulating immune responses, particularly in response to environmental signals.

> "recent evidence also supports an immunoregulatory role of LC at steady state and during specific inflammatory conditions, highlighting the impact of cutaneous environment in shaping LC functionality."
>
> — Zhou et al. (2022)

## Structure / Morphology

Langerhans cells exhibit distinctive morphology:

- **Stellate/Dendritic Appearance:**

  > "LCs are stellate cells that protrude their dendrites via tight junctions toward the stratum corneum and as such can probe for antigens across several layers of the epidermis without disrupting the permeability barrier."
  >
  > — Deckers et al. (2018)

- **Ultrastructure:**

  Cytoplasmic Birbeck granules are unique to LCs and are associated with Langerin expression.

  > "Langerhans cell histiocytosis (LCH) is a disease characterized by the proliferation of Langerhans cells that express an immunophenotype positive for S100 protein, CD1 (CD1a), and Langerin (CD207) and contain cytoplasmic Birbeck granules [1]."
  >
  > — Yang et al. (2023)
