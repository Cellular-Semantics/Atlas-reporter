# Capillary Arterioles in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid
Cell Ontology: [endothelial cell of arteriole](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_1000412) (CL:1000412, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)

## Summary

Capillary arterioles are a vascular endothelial cell population identified in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model, though with markedly reduced heterogeneity and quantity compared to prenatal skin (Gopee et al., 2024). In prenatal skin, early endothelial cells differentiate into an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles, and arterioles) or a venular pathway. In the SkO, capillary arterioles are present but exhibit a critical differentiation arrest: unlike their prenatal counterparts, SkO capillary arteriole cells cannot further differentiate into arterioles. This limitation is attributed to the absence of immune cells, particularly macrophages, which provide essential growth factors and signalling cues for vascular maturation. The SkO capillary arterioles display strong expression of the sprouting angiogenesis gene signature but are unable to guide stalk cells for new blood vessel formation.

## Markers

- **GJA5**: Arteriolar marker gene, characteristic of cells along the arteriolar differentiation pathway (Gopee et al., 2024).
- **GATA2**: Key transcription factor for angiogenesis; its regulon is downregulated in SkO capillary arterioles compared to prenatal skin (Extended Data Fig. 11d,e).
- **NFATC1**: Regulon downregulated in SkO capillary arterioles (Extended Data Fig. 11d,e).
- **KDR, FLT1**: VEGF receptors, downregulated in SkO capillary arterioles despite elevated VEGFA and VEGFB in SkO cells (Extended Data Fig. 11b,c).

## Location

### In the SkO model

Capillary arterioles are present within the SkO but are markedly reduced in number.

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### In prenatal skin

In prenatal skin, capillary arterioles form part of the arteriolar differentiation pathway from early endothelial cells, with expression of characteristic arteriolar genes such as GJA5 (Fig. 4d, Extended Data Fig. 10a-c).

## Function

### Arrested vascular differentiation

The key finding for SkO capillary arterioles is their inability to complete the arteriolar differentiation programme.

> "Unlike SkO capillary arteriole cells, prenatal skin capillary arteriole cells could further differentiate into arterioles"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Sprouting angiogenesis potential without completion

Despite the differentiation arrest, SkO capillary arterioles retain the molecular machinery for sprouting angiogenesis.

> "despite strong expression of the sprouting angiogenesis gene signature, SkO capillary arteriole cells are unable to guide stalk cells for new blood vessel formation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Macrophage-dependent regulation

The downregulation of GATA2 and its target genes in SkO capillary arterioles is linked to the absence of macrophage-derived signals. NicheNet analysis identified macrophage-expressed VEGFA as a top upstream ligand regulating GATA2 expression differences between prenatal skin and SkO endothelial cells (Gopee et al., 2024). Transfer of autologous macrophages into SkO cultures enhanced vascular network remodelling.

> "GATA2 and related regulons (for example, NFATC1) were downregulated in SkO capillary arterioles"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
