# Proliferating Melanoblast (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [melanoblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000541) (CL:0000541, broad match -- proliferating subtype not separately represented)

## Summary

Proliferating melanoblasts are a mitotically active subpopulation of melanocyte precursors identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). These cells represent melanoblasts in active cell division, expanding the pigment cell pool during skin development. In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, melanocyte lineage cells showed accelerated differentiation in the SkO, with alignment to adult states from 4 weeks of culture. Proliferating melanoblasts are an intermediate state between quiescent melanoblasts and differentiated melanocytes.

## Markers

Proliferating melanoblasts express melanocyte lineage markers (MITF, SOX10) combined with proliferation markers (MKI67, TOP2A) indicating active cell cycling.

## Atlas Context

> "accelerated differentiation was observed in keratinocytes and melanocytes, with alignment to adult cell states seen as early as 4 weeks of SkO culture"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In the SkO, proliferating melanoblasts are found in the developing epidermis and hair follicle compartments, representing actively dividing pigment cell precursors during expansion of the melanocyte lineage.

## Function

Proliferating melanoblasts expand the melanocyte progenitor pool to ensure adequate pigmentation of the epidermis and hair follicles. Their proliferative capacity is essential for populating the growing skin surface during development.
