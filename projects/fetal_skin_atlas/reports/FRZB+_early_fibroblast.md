# FRZB+ Early Fibroblasts in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000057) (CL:0000057, broad match — no exact CL term)

## Summary

FRZB+ early fibroblasts are a rare early-gestation dermal fibroblast population identified in prenatal human skin by Gopee et al. (2024). This subset is defined by expression of FRZB (frizzled-related protein), a secreted WNT antagonist. FRZB+ fibroblasts were primarily observed in one sample from the earliest gestation stage (7 PCW) and were excluded from trajectory analysis due to their rarity. Although uncommon in prenatal skin, FRZB-expressing fibroblasts were present in several other developing organs, suggesting a conserved early developmental role across tissues (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

The defining marker for this subset is **FRZB** (frizzled-related protein), a secreted antagonist of WNT signalling. The full list of differentially expressed genes is provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

## Location

### In prenatal skin

FRZB+ early fibroblasts are found in the dermal compartment of prenatal skin, primarily at the earliest gestation stage examined (7 PCW). Their rarity at this time point and absence at later stages suggest they represent a transient early developmental population.

### Across developing organs

FRZB-expressing fibroblasts were detected in several other developing organs beyond skin, indicating that this cell state is not unique to the skin but may represent a broadly conserved early mesenchymal population (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Function

### 1. Early mesenchymal progenitor state

FRZB+ early fibroblasts appear to represent an early, potentially transient state in the dermal fibroblast lineage. Their expression of FRZB, a WNT pathway antagonist, suggests a role in modulating WNT signalling during the earliest stages of dermal development. FRZB+ fibroblasts were excluded from the trajectory analysis that placed HOXC5+ early fibroblasts as the common progenitor for both hair follicle-specialized and dermal fibroblast lineages (Gopee et al., 2024), indicating they may represent a distinct or antecedent developmental state.
