# Neuroendocrine (Merkel) Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [Merkel cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000592) (CL:0000592, exact match)

## Summary

The Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas identifies a neuroendocrine cell population within the epidermal compartment of human fetal skin (7–17 post-conception weeks, PCW), annotated as Merkel cells based on differentially expressed marker genes (Supplementary Table 6). Merkel cells are specialised mechanosensory cells of epidermal origin that detect gentle touch via the mechanosensitive ion channel PIEZO2, forming the Merkel cell–neurite complex with innervating Aβ sensory afferents. In human fetal back skin, these cells express canonical markers cytokeratin 20 (CK20), CK8, synaptophysin, chromogranin A, and PIEZO2. Developmental studies demonstrate that Merkel cell specification depends on the transcription factor ATOH1, acting within the epidermal rather than neural crest lineage. Their differentiation is closely linked to hair follicle morphogenesis, consistent with their fetal appearance from around 56 days of gestation in glabrous skin and later in haired skin.

## Markers

Merkel cells in human fetal skin carry a dual epithelial–neuroendocrine marker profile. Cytokeratin 20 is the most specific epithelial marker, and in fetal skin CK8 additionally identifies Merkel cells uniquely within the epidermis:

> "Human skin contains specialized neuroendocrine Merkel cells responsible for fine touch sensation. In the present study, we performed in-depth analysis of Merkel cells in human fetal back skin. We revealed that these Merkel cells expressed cytokeratin 20 (CK20), were positive for the neuroendocrine markers synaptophysin and chromogranin A, and the mechanosensitive ion channel Piezo2."

> "in fetal skin and in skin substitutes, only Merkel cells were positive for CK8, while in culture, also non-Merkel cells showed positivity for CK8"

CK20 is the most reliable immunohistochemical marker across species, co-localising with PIEZO2 and classical neuroendocrine proteins:

> "In the skin, cytokeratin (CK) 20 is specifically expressed in Merkel cells, thus used as the most reliable immunohistochemical marker for Merkel cell in human and animals."

> "PIEZO2 co-localized with cytokeratin 20, chromogranin A, synaptophysin and neurofilament."

Additional neuroendocrine markers include CD56 (NCAM), neuron-specific enolase (NSE), and PGP9.5:

> "Neuroendocrine markers such as chromogranin, synaptophysin, CD56 (NCAM), neuron-specific enolase, and PGP9.5 are expressed in Merkel cells."

## Location

Merkel cells occupy the basal layer of the epidermis at the dermal-epidermal junction, in close spatial association with innervating sensory afferents:

> "Responsible for light touch sensation, Merkel cells are multimodal epidermal sensory cells that appear at around week 18 of gestation in human skin. Located in the basal layer and the lower epidermis at the dermal-epidermal junction, Merkel cells are closely associated with afferent neurons."

In the human fetus, CK20-positive Merkel cells are detectable from as early as 56 days (approximately 8 PCW) of gestation in glabrous skin, preceding their appearance in haired skin:

> "In the human fetus, CK20-positive Merkel cells were identified in the epidermis of glabrous skin as early as 56 days of gestation."

Their emergence is coupled to hair follicle development:

> "Merkel cell differentiation is strongly linked to HF morphogenesis."

## Function

Merkel cells are the sensory transducer component of the Merkel cell–neurite complex, a slowly adapting type I (SAI) mechanoreceptor that encodes fine spatial detail of touched objects. Their mechanosensory function depends entirely on the mechanically activated cation channel PIEZO2:

> "The Merkel cell–neurite complex is a gentle touch receptor in the skin that mediates slowly adapting responses of Aβ sensory fibres to encode fine details of objects."

> "Piezo2, a mechanically activated cation channel, is expressed in Merkel cells. We engineered mice deficient in Piezo2 in the skin, but not in sensory neurons, and show that Merkel-cell mechanosensitivity completely depends on Piezo2."

> "Our results indicate that Piezo2 is the Merkel-cell mechanotransduction channel and provide the first line of evidence that Piezo channels have a physiological role in mechanosensation in mammals."

PIEZO2 is characteristically expressed by human skin Merkel cells as an essential mechano-gated ion channel:

> "As an essential component of mechano-gated ion channels, critically required for mechanotransduction in mammalian cells, PIEZO2 is known to be characteristically expressed by Merkel cells in human skin."

## Development

Merkel cells arise from epidermal progenitors under the control of ATOH1. Lineage tracing and conditional knockout experiments have resolved the long-standing debate over neural crest versus epidermal origin:

> "In both studies it was shown that deletion of Atoh1 in epidermal progenitors resulted in a complete absence of MCs. Additionally, Morrison and colleagues demonstrated that Atoh1 deletion in the neural crest lineage did not affect the MC population."

Human fetal skin xenograft experiments independently confirm an epidermal origin:

> "xenograft of human fetal skin free of neural crest progenitors in immunocompromised mice led to the development of human Merkel cell suggesting an epidermal origin of this population"

Their dual epithelial–neuroendocrine phenotype reflects this origin:

> "Their origin is debatable, with some theories suggesting neural crest cells as the source and others proposing an epithelial/epidermal lineage based on the expression of epithelial keratins 8/18/20."
