# Neuroendocrine Cell (Merkel Cell) in Fetal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Source dataset: Gopee et al. (2024), prenatal skin, 7–17 PCW
Cell Ontology: [Merkel cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_4052058) (CL:4052058, exact match)

## Summary

The Neuroendocrine cluster in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas corresponds to Merkel cells — specialised neuroendocrine mechanoreceptor cells of the fetal epidermis. Merkel cells are identified by expression of simple epithelial keratins KRT8, KRT18, and KRT20, the neuroendocrine markers synaptophysin (SYP) and chromogranin A (CHGA), the mechanosensitive ion channel PIEZO2, and the transcription factor ATOH1 (also known as MATH1), which is required for Merkel cell specification. These cells occupy the basal layer of the fetal epidermis and are functionally distinct from keratinocytes by their neuroendocrine character and their role as slowly adapting type I (SAI) mechanoreceptors that sense sustained touch and texture. The cluster is annotated as a fine-grained cell type in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024).

## Markers

### Defining keratins: KRT8, KRT18, KRT20

Merkel cells express the simple epithelial keratins KRT8, KRT18, and KRT20, which distinguish them from stratified-epithelium keratinocytes that express KRT5 and KRT14. KRT20 in particular is a widely used immunohistochemical marker for Merkel cells.

### Neuroendocrine markers: synaptophysin and chromogranin A

Expression of synaptophysin (SYP) and chromogranin A (CHGA) reflects the neuroendocrine identity of Merkel cells, consistent with their vesicular release of neuropeptides and neurotransmitters at synaptic contacts with afferent nerve fibres.

### Mechanosensory ion channel: PIEZO2

PIEZO2 is a mechanosensitive ion channel essential for Merkel cell function as low-threshold mechanoreceptors. Its expression is required for the electrophysiological responses that underlie fine touch discrimination.

### Specification transcription factor: ATOH1

The bHLH transcription factor ATOH1 (MATH1) is required for Merkel cell fate specification from epidermal progenitors. Loss of ATOH1 abolishes Merkel cell formation. Its expression in this cluster supports the identification as Merkel cells rather than another neuroendocrine population.

## Location

### Basal layer of the fetal epidermis, 7–17 PCW

Merkel cells are annotated as a fine-grained fetal cell type in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024), present throughout the 7–17 PCW sampling window. They reside in the basal layer of the interfollicular epidermis and in the outer root sheath of hair follicles, particularly concentrated in regions of high tactile acuity such as touch domes.

## Function

### Neuroendocrine mechanoreception

Merkel cells function as slowly adapting type I (SAI) mechanoreceptors, responding to sustained indentation of the skin with sustained discharge patterns. They form synaptic contacts with Aβ afferent nerve fibres, and their mechanosensory function depends on PIEZO2-mediated ion channel activity. In fetal skin, Merkel cells are among the neural cell types present from early gestation, consistent with the early establishment of sensory innervation.
