# Venules (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal

## Summary

Venules represent a fine-grained blood vascular endothelial cell population in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), annotated under the broad "Vascular endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). They are the most differentiated state on the venular trajectory from early endothelial cells, with postcapillary venules as the intermediate step. Their defining venular marker is PLVAP:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis showed that early endothelial cells in prenatal skin differentiated into either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles and arterioles) or venular pathway (postcapillary venules and venules), with expression of characteristic genes (for example, GJA5 for arteriolar and PLVAP for venular)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Venule endothelial cells participate in macrophage-endothelial crosstalk and are present throughout gestation.

## Markers

Marker genes are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. The defining venular marker shared with postcapillary venules is **PLVAP**. GATA2/NFATC1 target genes are expressed across the venular trajectory pseudotime, including **VWF**:

> "Several target genes of GATA2 and NFATC1 (for example, VWF), which were expressed across the venular trajectory pseudotime and are involved in endothelial cell differentiation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

In adult skin, the venule cluster is characterised by ACKR1, ICAM1, SELP, EGR2, and LRG1; these markers are expected to be expressed at higher levels in venules than in postcapillary venules.

## Location

Venule endothelial cells form the collecting venular segments of the dermal microvasculature, downstream of postcapillary venules. They are present throughout the 7–17 PCW gestational window:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Terminal venular differentiation

Venules are the fully differentiated endpoint of the venular trajectory in fetal skin. Their maturation depends on immune cell signals:

> "immune cells are required to fully recapitulate in vivo endothelial cell development"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

In organoids lacking macrophages:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### GATA2/NFATC1 regulon activity

The venular trajectory is shaped by GATA2 and NFATC1 transcriptional programmes, with their target genes expressed across venular pseudotime. Macrophage-derived VEGFA is identified as an upstream regulator of GATA2 activity in endothelial cells.

### Macrophage communication

Macrophage-venule endothelial cell interactions are predicted through ligand-receptor crosstalk:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
