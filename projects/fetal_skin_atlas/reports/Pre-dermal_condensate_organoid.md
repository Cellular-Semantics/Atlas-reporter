# Pre-dermal Condensate Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [hair follicle cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002559) (CL:0002559, broad match -- no specific CL term for pre-dermal condensate)

## Summary

Pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells are fibroblast progenitors identified in both prenatal skin and the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, pre-Dc cells originate from HOXC5+ early fibroblasts and migrate toward the epidermis, expressing genes involved in collective migration (CLDN11, CXCL12, ADAMTS1). They interact with epidermal basal cells through CXCL12-ACKR3 signalling and aggregate to form the dermal condensate (Dc), which subsequently matures into the dermal papilla (Dp). Pre-Dc cells co-locate with dendritic and lymphoid cells in prenatal skin.

## Location

Pre-Dc cells are located in the dermis, migrating toward the epidermal-dermal junction where hair follicle formation is initiated. In the SkO, they occupy the same sub-epidermal position.

## Function

Pre-Dc cells are the critical mesenchymal signalling centres that initiate hair follicle formation through crosstalk with the overlying epidermis. Their collective migration, driven by CXCL12 signalling, leads to aggregation into the dermal condensate. In prenatal skin, immune cells (ILC3, LTi) co-locate with pre-Dc cells and may regulate their adhesion and migration through CXCL12-CXCR4 interactions.
