# Neuron Progenitors (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match)

## Summary

Neuron progenitors are neural crest-derived progenitor cells identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas as a fine-grained annotation within the fetal (7-17 PCW) dataset. These cells belong to the broad "Neuronal cells" lineage and represent precursors that give rise to peripheral neurons innervating the skin. In the atlas, neural cells are enriched in early gestation as part of the ectoderm-derived compartment, and co-locate with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments. The atlas also identifies these cells in the skin organoid (SkO) model, confirming their developmental relevance. Their neural crest origin means they share a common progenitor pool with melanocytes and Schwann cells, all arising from multipotent migratory neural crest cells during embryonic development.

## Markers

Neuron progenitors in skin are expected to express neural progenitor markers including SOX10 (early), SOX2, STMN2 (neuronal commitment), DCX (doublecortin), and neurogenin family transcription factors. Specific marker genes distinguishing this cluster from other neural populations are defined in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

Neuron progenitors are identified in fetal skin from 7-17 PCW. Neural cells are enriched in early gestation:

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm, which constitutes the first skin permeability barrier 2 , were enriched in early gestation, whereas suprabasal epidermal and HF cells were mainly observed in later gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

They reside within the dermal compartment, associated with the developing peripheral nervous system of the skin.

## Function

Neuron progenitors serve as the precursor population for cutaneous sensory and autonomic neurons. In prenatal skin, macrophages support neural development:

> "TML macrophages were predicted to interact with Schwann cells, contributing to synapse formation and axon guidance (VEGFA-NRP1, VEGFA-NRP2, SEMA3C-NRP2 and SEMA3E-PLXND1)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "These findings suggest that prenatal skin macrophages may support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system during early gestation, as previously reported in mouse skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
