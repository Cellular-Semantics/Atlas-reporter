# WNT2+ Adipocytes in Fetal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Source dataset: Gopee et al. (2024), prenatal skin, 7–17 PCW
Cell Ontology: [preadipocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002334) (CL:0002334, broad match)

## Summary

The WNT2+ Adipocytes cluster in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas is annotated within the dermal stromal compartment and likely represents preadipocyte or immature adipocyte cells that co-express WNT2 alongside lipogenic or adipogenic markers. In fetal skin, the dermal white adipose tissue (dWAT) layer is actively developing during the second trimester. The WNT2+ label, shared with the WNT2+ fibroblast cluster, suggests these cells derive from the same HOXC5+ early fibroblast lineage as WNT2+ fibroblasts and may represent a branch toward adipogenesis within the late-gestation dermal stromal compartment. The cluster appears as a fine-grained annotation in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024), and the dermal fibroblast trajectory analysis in the paper identifies WNT2+ cells as a major late-gestation stromal component:

> "the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts (abundant after 11 PCW)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Markers

### WNT2

WNT2 expression links this population to the broader WNT2+ dermal stromal lineage in fetal skin. WNT signalling is a known regulator of adipogenesis and preadipocyte commitment.

### Adipogenic markers

Based on the annotation as adipocytes and position in the dermal stromal compartment, this cluster likely expresses lipid droplet markers and adipogenic transcription factors such as PPARG and CEBPA, and/or lipid storage markers such as PLIN1. The precise marker set is characterised in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024).

### Temporal profile: late gestation

Like WNT2+ fibroblasts, WNT2+ adipocytes are expected to be enriched in later gestation, consistent with the second-trimester development of dWAT in human fetal skin:

> "Late gestation WNT2 + fibroblasts had upregulated genes related to extracellular matrix and collagen deposition (for example, COL1A1), whereas early WNT2 + fibroblasts had DEGs involved in cellular growth and differentiation (for example, SFRP1)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### Fetal dermis / developing dermal white adipose tissue layer, abundant from mid-gestation

WNT2+ adipocytes are a fine-grained annotation in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024), localised to the fetal dermis in the context of developing subcutaneous and dermal adipose tissue.

## Function

### Dermal adipogenesis and lipid storage

The primary function inferred for this cluster is participation in the establishment of the dermal white adipose tissue layer during fetal skin development. Preadipocyte-to-adipocyte differentiation in fetal skin contributes to the structural padding of the dermis and to paracrine signalling that regulates hair follicle cycling in postnatal skin. The WNT2 co-expression suggests these cells may occupy a transitional state between WNT2+ fibroblasts and committed adipocytes.
