# Suprabasal Interfollicular Epidermal Keratinocyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match -- suprabasal IFE not specifically represented in CL)

## Summary

Suprabasal IFE (interfollicular epidermal) keratinocytes are mature differentiating keratinocytes identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, mature suprabasal IFE cells increased after 11 PCW, replacing the immature suprabasal population. These cells have committed to terminal differentiation, expressing late differentiation markers as they move through the spinous and granular layers toward cornification. Keratinocyte differentiation in the SkO was notably accelerated, aligning with adult cell states from 4 weeks of culture.

## Markers

Suprabasal IFE keratinocytes express differentiation keratins (KRT1, KRT10), involucrin (IVL), filaggrin (FLG), and cornifin (CNFN), marking their commitment to terminal differentiation.

## Location

Suprabasal IFE keratinocytes are located in the spinous and granular layers of the interfollicular epidermis, above the basal layer. In the SkO, they occupy the same suprabasal position.

## Function

Suprabasal IFE keratinocytes undergo terminal differentiation to form the cornified envelope, the skin's essential barrier against water loss and environmental insults. Their accelerated maturation in the SkO demonstrates that the organoid efficiently generates a functional stratified epithelium.
