# Inflammatory Differentiated Keratinocyte (c8) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [suprabasal keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_4033013) (CL:4033013, broad match; new term needed)

## Summary

The c8 cluster represents inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes ("Differentiated KC*") in adult human skin, identified by Reynolds et al. (2021) as a distinct keratinocyte state separate from standard differentiated keratinocytes (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7). These cells are characterized by co-expression of lower levels of both basal (TP63, ITGA6) and suprabasal (KRT1, KRT10) markers alongside inflammatory mediators ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20, as well as genes associated with inflammatory ichthyoses (NIPAL4, SPINK5). This population is expanded in psoriasis lesional skin and functionally resembles CCL20-expressing keratinocytes in murine IL-17/TNF-alpha-driven inflammatory models. The adult skin annotations including c8 were integrated into the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas for cross-developmental comparisons.

## Markers

### Inflammatory mediators: ICAM1, TNF, CCL20

The defining feature distinguishing c8 from other differentiated keratinocytes is the expression of inflammatory genes. Reynolds et al. (2021) reported:

> "Inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes co-express lower levels of undifferentiated (TP63, ITGA6) and differentiated (KRT1, KRT10) transcripts but additionally express ICAM1, TNF and CCL20"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

CCL20 is particularly significant functionally. Liu et al. (2022) confirmed:

> "Reynolds et al. defined a new subpopulation of celled inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes, as suggested by coexpressing lower levels of undifferentiated and differentiated markers but additionally expressing intercellular adhesion molecule1 (ICAM1), tumor necrosis factor (TNF), and CCL20"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

CCL20 is the sole high-affinity ligand for CCR6, a chemokine receptor expressed on IL-17A-producing T cells and dendritic cells, creating a positive inflammatory feedback loop in skin disease (Liu et al., 2022).

### Ichthyosis-associated genes: NIPAL4, SPINK5

A distinctive molecular feature of cluster 8 is the elevated expression of genes linked to inherited inflammatory skin disorders. Reynolds et al. (2021) noted:

> "Inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes express higher levels of genes associated with inflammatory ichthyoses and severe atopy such as NIPAL4 and SPINK5 (Fig. 4E, cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

NIPAL4 mutations cause autosomal recessive congenital ichthyosis, while SPINK5 mutations cause Netherton syndrome, characterized by ichthyosiform erythroderma and severe atopy. The expression of these genes in inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes suggests molecular overlap between this acquired inflammatory state and genetic barrier disorders.

### Intermediate differentiation markers: KRT1, KRT10 (lower), TP63, ITGA6 (lower)

Unlike standard differentiated keratinocytes that express high KRT1/KRT10, and unlike undifferentiated basal keratinocytes that express high TP63/ITGA6, cluster 8 shows reduced levels of both marker sets. This positions it as a transcriptionally intermediate state that has partially lost both basal and suprabasal identity while acquiring an inflammatory program.

## Location

### Interfollicular epidermis

Inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes reside in the suprabasal epidermal layers of interfollicular human skin. Their spatial arrangement is consistent with the general epidermal architecture described by Reynolds et al. (2021), where differentiated keratinocytes occupy layers above the basal compartment. The intermediate expression of both basal and suprabasal markers suggests these cells may be distributed across the lower spinous layer rather than strictly localizing to upper differentiation-committed strata.

## Function

### Immune cell recruitment via CCL20-CCR6 axis

The primary functional role of inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes is chemokine-mediated recruitment of immune cells into the skin. CCL20 produced by these cells attracts CCR6-expressing Th17 cells, dendritic cells, and other IL-17-producing lymphocytes (Liu et al., 2022). This creates a feed-forward inflammatory loop: recruited Th17 cells produce IL-17 and TNF-alpha, which further stimulate keratinocyte CCL20 production, amplifying immune cell infiltration.

### Expansion in psoriasis

Reynolds et al. (2021) observed that inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes are expanded in psoriasis lesional skin and resemble CCL20-expressing keratinocytes induced by subcutaneous IL-17 and TNF-alpha injection in murine models. This links the c8 state to active psoriatic inflammation and suggests it represents a disease-amplifying keratinocyte phenotype rather than a homeostatic cell state.

### Potential barrier dysfunction

The co-expression of NIPAL4 and SPINK5 (genes whose loss-of-function causes severe barrier defects) at higher levels in this inflammatory state suggests possible dysregulation of barrier homeostasis. Whether these genes are protective (compensatory response to barrier stress) or pathogenic (aberrant expression disrupting normal cornification) in the context of inflammatory differentiated keratinocytes remains to be determined.
