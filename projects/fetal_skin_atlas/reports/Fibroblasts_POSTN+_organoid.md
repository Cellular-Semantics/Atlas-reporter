# Fibroblasts POSTN+ in Skin Organoid

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Tissue context: hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO)
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast of dermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002551) (CL:0002551, broad match)

## Summary

The Fibroblasts POSTN+ population represents a dermal fibroblast state in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) characterised by expression of periostin (POSTN), a secreted extracellular matrix protein and integrin ligand. This population is identified in the SkO dermal compartment (Extended Data Fig. 2b; Gopee et al., 2024) and was catalogued using Supplementary Table 3 marker gene data. Periostin is associated with fibroblast activation and connective tissue remodelling, and POSTN-expressing fibroblast subsets have been described in developing and injured tissues across multiple organs. The SkO recapitulates the major components of prenatal skin including the dermal fibroblast compartment, though immune cells are absent and endothelial cells are markedly reduced:

> "Notably, the SkO model recapitulated the different components of prenatal skin HF, interfollicular epidermis, neural cells and dermal fibroblasts, but immune cells were not represented and endothelial cells were markedly reduced."

— Gopee et al. (2024)

Across the SkO dermal fibroblast compartment, cells retain a prenatal-like rather than adult identity even after extended culture:

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states"

— Gopee et al. (2024)

## Markers

### Defining marker: POSTN

POSTN (periostin) is the defining marker for this fibroblast population (Supplementary Table 3; Gopee et al., 2024). Periostin is an extracellular matrix protein that binds integrins and promotes cell adhesion, migration, and collagen fibrillogenesis. In skin, POSTN-expressing fibroblasts have been associated with the reticular dermis and with activated fibroblast states in inflammatory and developmental contexts. Reynolds et al. (2021) identified POSTN-expressing fibroblast subsets in their adult skin atlas that are co-opted in inflammatory skin disease, providing a cross-developmental comparison point for the SkO POSTN+ population.

## Location

POSTN+ fibroblasts are present in the dermal compartment of the SkO, as shown in Extended Data Fig. 2b of Gopee et al. (2024). Within the SkO, the dermal compartment contains multiple fibroblast subtypes that recapitulate the diversity seen in prenatal skin, including hair follicle-associated mesenchymal cells and interfollicular dermal fibroblasts. POSTN+ fibroblasts likely correspond to an activated or matrix-producing subset within this compartment, consistent with the known biology of periostin-expressing cells in developing connective tissue.

## Functional Significance

Periostin has established roles in connective tissue development, wound healing, and epithelial-mesenchymal interactions. In the context of hair follicle morphogenesis, POSTN has been detected in the dermal papilla and surrounding connective tissue sheath (Heitman et al., 2022), suggesting that POSTN+ fibroblasts in the SkO may contribute to the structural matrix environment supporting hair follicle development. The expression of POSTN in this fibroblast subset, rather than in the broader fibroblast pool, implies a degree of functional specialisation toward matrix deposition and tissue organisation within the organoid dermis.

The broader context of distinct fibroblast lineages in determining dermal architecture has been established in mouse models (Driskell et al., 2013), and the presence of a molecularly distinct POSTN+ subset in the SkO dermal compartment is consistent with this lineage heterogeneity being recapitulated in the organoid system.

## Relationship to Inflammatory Skin Disease

Reynolds et al. (2021) identified that developmental cell programs in fibroblasts, including POSTN-associated signatures, are co-opted in inflammatory skin disease such as psoriasis and atopic dermatitis. The identification of a POSTN+ fibroblast subset in the prenatal skin organoid provides a developmental baseline against which disease-associated fibroblast activation states can be compared.

No CL term exists for a POSTN-expressing or organoid-specific dermal fibroblast. CL:0002551 (fibroblast of dermis) is the most appropriate available term. CL:0000186 (myofibroblast cell) was considered but rejected: the atlas does not designate this population as myofibroblasts, and POSTN expression alone does not constitute a myofibroblast identity in the absence of reported alpha-smooth muscle actin expression or contractile characteristics. A new CL term for a POSTN+ activated dermal fibroblast may be warranted with further cross-dataset evidence.
