# Immature Basal Keratinocyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [basal cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002187) (CL:0002187, broad match -- immature state not separately represented in CL)

## Summary

Immature basal keratinocytes are an early embryonic epidermal cell state identified in both prenatal human skin and the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model derived from human pluripotent stem cells (Lee et al., 2020). In the prenatal skin atlas of Gopee et al. (2024), immature IFE cells, including immature basal cells, were present from 7 post-conception weeks (PCW) and decreased after 11 PCW during the transition from embryonic to fetal skin. In the SkO, these cells recapitulate the earliest epidermal progenitor state, preceding the emergence of mature DPYSL2+ basal and POSTN+ basal keratinocytes that appear later in development. Immature basal cells interact with early fibroblasts through mesenchymal-epithelial signalling during hair follicle initiation.

## Markers

### Basal keratin expression: KRT5, KRT14

Immature basal keratinocytes express the canonical basal keratin pair KRT5 and KRT14, consistent with their identity as basal epidermal progenitors. These markers are shared with mature basal keratinocytes.

### Distinguishing from mature basal cells

Unlike mature basal cells, immature basal keratinocytes lack the elevated expression of DPYSL2 and POSTN that characterise later developmental states. The transition from immature to mature basal phenotype occurs around 11 PCW in prenatal skin.

## Location

Immature basal keratinocytes reside in the basal layer of the embryonic interfollicular epidermis, where they form a single layer overlying the dermal stroma in early gestation (7-11 PCW). In the SkO, they occupy a corresponding basal position within the organoid epithelium.

## Function

Immature basal keratinocytes serve as the earliest epidermal progenitor pool in embryonic skin. They participate in mesenchymal-epithelial interactions that initiate hair follicle formation, acting as the epithelial partner in early signalling exchanges with dermal fibroblasts. Their transition to mature basal states (DPYSL2+ and POSTN+) marks the onset of hair follicle competence and interfollicular epidermal stratification.
