# ILC3 (Group 3 Innate Lymphoid Cells) in Prenatal Human Skin
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: prenatal (7-17 PCW)  
CL mapping: [CL:0001071](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0001071) — group 3 innate lymphoid cell (exact match, high confidence)

## Summary

Group 3 innate lymphoid cells (ILC3s) are RORgammat-dependent innate lymphocytes identified in prenatal human skin from early gestation. In this atlas, ILC3 cells were predicted to co-locate and interact with pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells through CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4 ligand-receptor signals, implicating them in the regulation of hair follicle morphogenesis (Gopee et al., 2024). ILC3s are among the first immune cells to colonize tissues during embryonic development and include both lymphoid tissue inducer (LTi)-like cells and non-LTi subsets, which play diverse roles in lymphoid organogenesis, barrier immunity, and tissue repair. In the prenatal skin context, their spatial association with pre-dermal condensate cells suggests a non-canonical developmental function beyond conventional immunity.

## Markers

ILC3s are defined by expression of the lineage transcription factor RORgammat (encoded by RORC) and the capacity to produce IL-17 and/or IL-22. Key markers include:

- **RORC (RORgammat):** The master transcription factor for ILC3 identity. Expression of RORgammat is essential for the development and maturation of all ILC3 family members, including fetal LTi cells, adult LTi-like ILC3s, and NKp46+ ILC3s.

- **IL7R (CD127):** A shared ILC lineage marker critical for ILC3 development and maintenance. IL-7 signaling is necessary for ILC3 survival.

- **CXCR4:** Expressed by prenatal ILC3 in this atlas, where CXCL12-CXCR4 interactions mediate co-location with pre-dermal condensate cells (Gopee et al., 2024).

In fetal tissues, ILC3 subsets display additional markers that distinguish LTi-like from non-LTi populations:

> "ILC3_b and ILC3_c formed the LTi-related cluster, with increased expression of LTi characteristic genes such as LTA and LTB. NRP1, expressed by LTi-like group 3 ILCs in smokers' lungs and associated with ectopic lymphoid aggregates, was also enriched in ILC3_b and ILC3_c"
>
> -- Liu et al. (2021)

In adult skin, ILC3 subsets are further distinguished by NKp44 expression:

> "human NKp44 + ILC3s are enriched in barrier tissues such as colon and skin"
>
> -- Song et al. (2020)

In healthy human skin, ILC3s are identifiable by RORc and AHR expression:

> "ILCs are present in the upper dermis of healthy skin, with the predominant subsets being ILC1 (T-bet + ) and ILC3 (RORc + / AHR + )."
>
> -- Wei et al. (2025)

## Location

### Prenatal Skin -- Co-location with Pre-Dermal Condensate

The most distinctive spatial finding for ILC3 in this atlas is their predicted co-location with pre-dermal condensate cells:

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "lymphoid tissue inducer and ILC3 cells were also predicted to co-locate and interact with pre-Dc cells through ligand-receptor signals implicated in the regulation of cellular adhesion and migration (CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

This spatial relationship places ILC3 in a defined microanatomical niche where they may contribute to hair follicle morphogenesis through immune-stromal crosstalk.

### Developmental Timing

ILC3s, like other ILCs, are present from early gestation:

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Across developing human organs, ILC3 are detectable from the first trimester:

> "human CRTH2-CD117+ ILC3 among Lin-CD34-CD45+ cells can be detected in liver, intestine, spleen, skin, lung, and thymus at 12 PCW displaying LTi-related as well as non-LTi gene expression patterns"
>
> -- Hernandez-Torres & Stehle (2022)

### Broader Tissue Distribution

In the Suo et al. (2022) cross-organ developmental atlas, ILC3 co-localize with B cell progenitors across multiple organs:

> "Spatial transcriptomic data identified cells colocalizing with B cell progenitors across multiple organs, including ILC3, LYVE1 hi macrophages, NK cells, type 1 innate T cells, and LMPP_MLP"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

The supplementary data from the prenatal skin atlas also notes the previously established supportive role of ILC3 in B-cell lymphopoiesis in developing organs (Gopee et al., 2024, supplementary information).

## Function

### Hair Follicle Morphogenesis

The atlas predicts that ILC3 and LTi cells interact with pre-dermal condensate cells through CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4 signaling axes (Gopee et al., 2024). The dermal condensate is the precursor to the dermal papilla, a signaling center essential for hair follicle induction and cycling. This co-location suggests ILC3 may have a morphogenetic role during hair follicle development, consistent with the broader atlas finding that innate immune cells perform non-immune functions through crosstalk with non-immune cells.

In postnatal skin, ILC3 contribute to hair follicle regeneration:

> "ILC3 also contribute to hair follicle regeneration (hair follicle neogenesis). They regulate macrophage and stem cell activity through secreted factors like CCL3 and influence the function of hair follicle stem cells by modulating BMP signaling"
>
> -- Wei et al. (2025)

### Wound Repair and Barrier Immunity

ILC3s produce IL-17 and IL-22, which are critical for epithelial barrier maintenance and repair:

> "ILC3 express the transcription factor RORgt and primarily secrete cytokines such as IL-17 and IL-22, which have diverse functions during skin development. After skin injury, activation of epidermal Notch1 signaling recruits ILC3 to the wound site. ILC3 rapidly produce IL-17F and IL-22, promoting macrophage recruitment and keratinocyte proliferation, thereby accelerating wound closure and reepithelialization"
>
> -- Wei et al. (2025)

> "ILC3 produce IL-17A, however IL-17F expression is the dominant wound response in skin. The importance of ILC3 in skin healing was demonstrated by impaired healing in RORgt deficient animals that lack ILC3 and IL-17 production"
>
> -- Cox et al. (2021)

> "Activated ILC3 produce CCL3 which recruits monocytes (Mon). ILC3 may also produce GM-CSF which promotes reepithelialisation and vascularization, and regulates collagen deposition."
>
> -- Cox et al. (2021)

### Lymphoid Organogenesis

Fetal ILC3 (LTi cells) are essential for the formation of secondary lymphoid organs:

> "ILC3 are one of the first immune cells to colonize the intestine during the embryonic period and are critical for the formation of SLOs"
>
> -- Domingues & Hepworth (2020)

> "Fetal ILC3s at this stage express CXCR5, CCR7, and alpha4beta7; homing markers that are important for fetal ILC3 recruitment and which were shown to mediate migration toward LTo-derived chemokines and adhesion molecules"
>
> -- Domingues & Hepworth (2020)

### B-cell Lymphopoiesis Support

ILC3 have a supportive role in B-cell development within developing organs, co-localizing with B cell progenitors across multiple tissues (Suo et al., 2022). This function may also be relevant in prenatal skin, where the atlas supplementary data notes similar supportive roles.
