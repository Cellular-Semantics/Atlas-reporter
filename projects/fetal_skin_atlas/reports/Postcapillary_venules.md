# Postcapillary Venules (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [endothelial cell of venule](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_1000414) (CL:1000414, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)

## CL Mapping

**CL:1000414** — endothelial cell of venule
- Match type: broad
- Rationale: Postcapillary venules in the fetal skin atlas are annotated as the first step on the venular differentiation trajectory, between early endothelial cells and venules, characterised by PLVAP expression. CL:1000414 is defined as "an endothelial cell that is part of the venule," which is the best available CL term. The more specific CL:0008040 (squamous endothelial cell of venule) or CL:0002652 (endothelial cell of high endothelial venule) are less appropriate. No postcapillary venule-specific endothelial cell term exists in CL.

## Summary

Postcapillary venules represent a fine-grained blood vascular endothelial cell population in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), annotated under the broad "Vascular endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). They occupy the first differentiated position on the venular trajectory from early endothelial cells, characterised by expression of PLVAP:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis showed that early endothelial cells in prenatal skin differentiated into either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles and arterioles) or venular pathway (postcapillary venules and venules), with expression of characteristic genes (for example, GJA5 for arteriolar and PLVAP for venular)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Postcapillary venule endothelial cells participate in macrophage-endothelial crosstalk and are present throughout gestation.

## Markers

Marker genes are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. The defining venular marker is **PLVAP** (plasmalemma vesicle-associated protein), shared with venules. Additional target genes of the GATA2/NFATC1 regulon expressed across the venular trajectory include **VWF**:

> "Several target genes of GATA2 and NFATC1 (for example, VWF), which were expressed across the venular trajectory pseudotime and are involved in endothelial cell differentiation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

In adult skin, the postcapillary venule (PCV) cluster is marked by ACKR1, ICAM1, and SELP; these markers may also be relevant to the fetal postcapillary venule population.

## Location

Postcapillary venule endothelial cells are part of the dermal microvasculature, forming the venular side immediately downstream of capillaries. They are present throughout gestation as part of the mesoderm-derived endothelial compartment:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Venular differentiation and PLVAP expression

Postcapillary venules represent the initial venular differentiation state from early endothelial cells, acquiring PLVAP expression as a defining feature of the venular programme. Full differentiation into venules requires immune cell signals:

> "immune cells are required to fully recapitulate in vivo endothelial cell development"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Absence of macrophages results in:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### Macrophage communication

Macrophage-endothelial crosstalk encompasses postcapillary venule cells through predicted ligand-receptor interactions:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
