# ILC1 (Innate Lymphoid Cell Type 1)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Innate lymphoid cell type 1 (ILC1) is a subset of innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) that is distinguished by its expression of the transcription factor T-bet and its secretion of type 1 cytokines, principally interferon gamma (IFN-γ). ILC1s are found in barrier tissues such as the skin, where they contribute to immune responses and tissue homeostasis. While their phenotypic definition often overlaps with that of natural killer (NK) cells, unique markers like IL7R (CD127), lack of Eomes, and differential expression of NK-associated proteins help distinguish them. ILC1s play critical roles in type 1 immunity, tissue defense, and disease, including anti-tumor responses and inflammatory conditions such as alopecia areata.

## Markers

ILC1s are defined by the expression of certain transcription factors, cytokines, and cell surface markers that distinguish them from other ILC subsets and from NK cells:

- **T-bet (TBX21):** The presence of the T-bet transcription factor is a defining feature of ILC1s and drives their type 1 functional program, including IFN-γ production.
  
  > "ILC1 are grouped within group 1 ILCs due to their expression of the transcription factor (TF) T-bet and secretion of IFNg (Bernink et al., 2013;Spits et al., 2013)"
  >
  > — Bennstein et al. (2020)
  
- **IFN-γ production:** ILC1s characteristically produce IFN-γ, a cytokine central to their antiviral and anti-tumor roles.
  
  > "group 1 ILCs (ILC1) are T-bet dependent and produce IFNγ"
  >
  > — Tay et al. (2013)
  
- **CD127 (IL7R):** CD127 is frequently expressed on human non-NK ILCs, including ILC1s, and is used to help differentiate ILC1s from NK cells. IL7R's presence is also corroborated as a distinguishing marker in single-cell studies.
  
  > "IL7R, LTB, and RGS1 differentiate ILC1s from NK cells in mice and humans."
  >
  > — Lopes et al. (2022)
  
- **CD161:** Identified as being expressed on ILC1s, though this is not entirely specific as it is shared with some NK cell subsets.
  
  > "ILC1 were defined as lin -CD127 + CD117 -CRTH2 -CD161 + cells (Bernink et al., 2013)"
  >
  > — Bennstein et al. (2020)
  
- **NKG2D:** ILC1s can express the activating receptor NKG2D, contributing to their immune effector functions.
  
  > "For instance, ILC1s secrete type 1 cytokines (e.g., IFN-γ and TNF-α)"
  >
  > — Korkmaz et al. (2021)

In summary, ILC1s in the skin are best identified by their expression of T-bet and CD127, secretion of IFN-γ and TNF-α, and lack of Eomes, with additional markers including CD161 and (contextually) NKG2D. However, robust markers for their exclusive identification remain a matter of ongoing research.

## Location

### Skin

ILC1s are a recognized immune population within adult skin, predominantly located in the upper dermis and playing roles at barrier sites:

> "ILCs are especially prevalent in barrier tissues, such as the skin, lung, and intestines, where they are thought to be important for maintenance of tissue homeostasis, as well as mediating initial inflammation in response to pathology such as infection (81)."
>
> — Johnson & Lee (2025)

They are notably found surrounding hair follicles and are enriched in both lesional and non-lesional skin in disease states:

> "Type 1 innate lymphoid cells (ILC1) show a marked increase around lesional and non-lesional hair follicles in AA patients."
>
> — Zhao et al. (2025)

### Developmental and Anatomical Variation

The prevalence and role of ILC1s change with developmental stage and tissue context. In neonates, CCR10+ NK1.1+ ILC1s are more abundant in the skin, with a noticeable decline as development progresses into adulthood:

> "CCR10 + NK1.1 + ILC1s accounted for only a very small fraction (2%-5%) of total CCR10 + ILCs in the skin or sLNs of adult mice, whereas they accounted for the majority (>50%) of CCR10 + ILCs in the neonatal skin"
>
> — Yang et al. (2020)

Additionally, ILC1s are present in lymphoid organs (e.g., secondary lymph nodes) and are capable of trafficking from these sites to the skin:

> "percentages of CCR10 + NK1.1 + ILC1s increased in the sLNs but decreased in the skin compared with untreated controls (Figures 2K and S2B), confirming that CCR10 + ILC1s of the sLNs migrate into the skin to contribute to the skin NK1.1 + ILC1 pool."
>
> — Yang et al. (2020)

## Function

### Immune Defense and Homeostasis

ILC1s are central players in type 1 immunity at barrier sites, mirroring the function of Th1-helper T cells:

> "ILC1, ILC2 and ILC3 parallel CD4 T-helper cells; TH1, TH2, and TH17 respectively; through the specific transcription factors expressed and cytokines produced [3]."
>
> — Nabatanzi et al. (2021)

ILC1 involvement includes defense against pathogens, early immune responses, and interaction with other skin-resident cells.

> "ILC1 mainly produce IFNγ and provide defenses against intracellular bacteria and protozoa (22)."
>
> — Vacca et al. (2018)

> "ILC subsets (usually categorized as ILC1, ILC2, and ILC3 cells; see Table 2 (68-80)). ILCs are especially prevalent in barrier tissues, such as the skin, lung, and intestines, where they are thought to be important for maintenance of tissue homeostasis, as well as mediating initial inflammation in response to pathology such as infection (81)."
>
> — Johnson & Lee (2025)

### Disease Context: Alopecia Areata

ILC1s have been implicated as direct effectors in the pathogenesis of autoimmune hair loss (alopecia areata, AA), functioning independently of adaptive immunity:

> "Here, we show that ILC1lc are increased in AA lesions provide the first functional evidence that expanded circulating autologous human ILC1lc suffice to induce all hallmarks of the AA hair loss phenotype (premature catagen, HF dystrophy, and HF-IP collapse) in previously healthy, organcultured human scalp HFs ex vivo and in human scalp skin xenotransplants in vivo, where they also cause the characteristic clinical hair loss phenomenon."
>
> — Britva et al. (2022)

> "Mechanistically, we demonstrate that IFN-γsecretion and NKG2D signaling are both required for this AA-pattern HF damage to occur (Figure 9). That ILC1lc alone can induce all hallmarks of AA in a healthy human (mini-) organ ex vivo and in vivo, presumably in an autoantigen-independent manner, also demonstrates that these innate/transitional lymphocytes interact directly with human HFs, rather than affecting them only indirectly."
>
> — Britva et al. (2022)

This suggests a significant pathogenic role for ILC1s in skin autoimmunity.

### Anti-tumor Immunity

ILC1s have documented activity against tumor cells, attributed primarily to their cytokine secretion, specifically IFN-γ and, in some settings, TNF-α:

> "ILC1s are also characterized by their antitumor function, which stems from the production of certain inflammatory cytokines, in particular IFN-γ, and the expression of Tbet."
>
> — Sadeghi et al. (2024)

Additionally, they can modulate tumor apoptosis and inhibit tumor cell proliferation through cytokine-mediated mechanisms.

> "non-NK ILCs inhibit tumor cell proliferation and induce tumor apoptosis through the production of TNF-α and IFN-γ [116]."
>
> — Sadeghi et al. (2024)

### Inflammation and Tissue Regulation

Beyond defense, ILC1s participate in shaping inflammatory responses and maintaining tissue homeostasis in the skin:

> "ILCs are capable of responding to environmental threats to trigger innate immune mechanisms that facilitate the induction of adaptive immune responses [120,122]. Importantly, ILCs communicate with other skin cells, including keratinocytes, dendritic cells, and mast cells, and these interactions play critical roles in ILC survival and stimulation, as well as innate immune responses."
>
> — Korkmaz et al. (2021)

## Structure / Morphology

No direct morphological description of ILC1s in the skin is provided in the traversed literature. The definition of ILC1s relies on lineage, marker expression, and functional attributes rather than distinctive morphology.
