# Neuroepithelial Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [neurecto-epithelial cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000710) (CL:0000710, broad match)

## Summary

Neuroepithelial cells are neural crest-derived progenitors identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, neural cells are part of the ectoderm-derived compartment and are enriched in early gestation. The SkO model, which derives from neural crest differentiation of pluripotent stem cells, recapitulates the neural cell compartment of prenatal skin, including neuroepithelial cells as early neural progenitors.

## Markers

Neuroepithelial cells express early neural markers including SOX2, NES (nestin), PAX6, and other neural plate/tube markers.

## Location

In the SkO, neuroepithelial cells are part of the neural crest-derived compartment, representing early-stage neural progenitors before further specialisation into Schwann cells, neurons, or melanocytes.

## Function

Neuroepithelial cells serve as multipotent neural progenitors capable of giving rise to diverse neural lineages in the skin, including neurons, Schwann cells, and melanocytes. Their presence in the SkO reflects the neural crest origin of the organoid model.
