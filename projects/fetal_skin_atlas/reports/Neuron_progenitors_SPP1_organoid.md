# Neuron Progenitor SPP1+ (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match -- SPP1+ subtype not separately represented in CL)

## Summary

SPP1-positive neuron progenitors are a molecularly distinct subpopulation of neural progenitor cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). SPP1 (osteopontin/secreted phosphoprotein 1) is a secreted glycoprotein involved in cell adhesion, migration, and immune modulation. The expression of SPP1 distinguishes this subset from other neuron progenitors, potentially indicating a role in neurovascular or immune interactions during skin neural development.

## Markers

SPP1+ neuron progenitors are defined by expression of SPP1 (osteopontin) in addition to standard neural progenitor markers.

## Atlas Context

>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In the SkO, SPP1+ neuron progenitors are part of the neural compartment and may interact with the neurovascular microenvironment.

## Function

SPP1+ neuron progenitors may represent a specialised neural precursor population involved in neurovascular interactions or tissue remodelling during skin development. SPP1 secretion could contribute to extracellular matrix organisation and cell migration guidance during neural development in the skin.
