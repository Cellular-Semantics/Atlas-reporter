# Myelinating Schwann Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [myelinating Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000218) (CL:0000218, exact match)

## Summary

Myelinating Schwann cells are peripheral nervous system glial cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, Schwann cells are part of the neural cell compartment and co-locate with macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1). Notably, Schwann cells in the SkO showed a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states even after 19 weeks of culture, suggesting that myelination-associated maturation is slow or requires additional signals not present in the organoid.

## Markers

Myelinating Schwann cells express MBP (myelin basic protein), MPZ (myelin protein zero), PLP1, PRX (periaxin), and pan-Schwann markers S100B and ERBB3.

## Location

In prenatal skin, myelinating Schwann cells are associated with peripheral nerve fibres in the dermis and co-locate with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments. In the SkO, they are part of the neural crest-derived compartment.

## Function

Myelinating Schwann cells ensheath large-diameter axons in a one-to-one relationship, producing the myelin sheath critical for rapid nerve impulse transmission. In prenatal skin, macrophage interactions through VEGFA-NRP and SEMA signalling are predicted to support their development, highlighting the importance of immune-neural crosstalk that is absent in the SkO model.
