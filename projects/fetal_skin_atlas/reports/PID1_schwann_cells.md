# PID1+ Schwann Cells (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002573) (CL:0002573, broad match -- no PID1-specific subtype exists in CL)

## Summary

PID1+ Schwann cells are a fetal-specific Schwann cell subtype annotated in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, distinguished by expression of PID1 (phosphotyrosine interaction domain containing 1). PID1 is a gene encoding a phosphotyrosine-binding domain protein implicated in insulin signaling modulation and metabolic control. Its expression in a Schwann cell subset in fetal skin may reflect a metabolically distinct or developmentally regulated subpopulation. These cells fall within the broad "Schwann cell" lineage in the atlas, part of the neural cell compartment that co-locates with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1) of prenatal skin (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

- **PID1 (Phosphotyrosine Interaction Domain Containing 1)**: The defining marker distinguishing this subtype from other Schwann cells in the atlas.

- **SOX10**: Pan-Schwann lineage transcription factor expressed from neural crest through all Schwann cell stages.

  > "Sox10 belongs to the group of HMG (high mobility group) domain-containing transcription factors (Wegner, 1999;Bowles et al., 2000). During vertebrate development, it is highly expressed in the emerging neural crest and later in the developing peripheral nervous system (PNS) and central nervous system, where its occurrence is restricted to glial cells"
  >
  > -- Finzsch et al. (2010)

- **S100B**: Pan-Schwann cell calcium-binding protein marker.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

PID1+ Schwann cells are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW). The broad Schwann cell population co-locates with endothelial and neural cells in the early neurovascular microenvironment:

> "Macrophages co-located with endothelial and neural cells in 'early and late neurovascular microenvironments' (ME1 and ME5, respectively)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

The specific functional role of PID1 in Schwann cells has not been characterized in the literature. Based on known PID1 biology, this subtype may represent Schwann cells with a distinct metabolic profile during fetal development. General Schwann cell functions include axon ensheathment and trophic support. In prenatal skin, macrophages interact with Schwann cells to support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system (Gopee et al., 2024).
