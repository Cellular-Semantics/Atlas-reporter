# Secreting Neuron (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [efferent neuron](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000527) (CL:0000527, broad match -- secreting neuron subtype not specifically represented in CL)

## Summary

Secreting neurons are a specialised neural cell population identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). These cells represent neurosecretory neurons within the skin neural compartment that produce and release neuropeptides or neurotransmitters. In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, neural cells are part of the ectoderm-derived compartment enriched in early gestation. The SkO model recapitulates the neural cell diversity of prenatal skin including this secretory neuronal subtype.

## Markers

Secreting neurons express neuronal markers combined with secretory/neuroendocrine markers, potentially including neuropeptide genes (e.g., TAC1, CALCA, VIP) and vesicle transport machinery.

## Atlas Context

> "the SkO model recapitulated the different components of prenatal skin HF, interfollicular epidermis, neural cells and dermal fibroblasts"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In the SkO, secreting neurons are part of the neural compartment, representing differentiated neurons capable of neurotransmitter/neuropeptide secretion.

## Function

Secreting neurons in skin release neuropeptides that modulate local tissue homeostasis, vasodilation, inflammation, and pain signalling. They contribute to the neuro-immune and neurovascular communication networks in the skin.
