# Monocyte-Derived Dendritic Cell Subtype 2 (moDC_2) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [monocyte-derived dendritic cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011031) (CL:0011031, broad match)

## Summary

The moDC_2 cluster represents an intermediate maturation state of monocyte-derived dendritic cells in adult human skin, as defined by Reynolds et al. (2021). Positioned along the monocyte-derived DC differentiation trajectory between the less mature moDC_1 and the terminally mature moDC_3, moDC_2 represents a transitional state progressively acquiring dendritic cell maturation features while partially retaining monocyte-derived identity markers. This cluster was identified within the PAGA analysis of 14 mononuclear phagocyte states in human skin, on the mo-DC branch distinct from Langerhans cells and myeloid DCs (DC1/DC2). The adult skin annotations including moDC subtypes were integrated into the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas for cross-developmental comparisons.

## Markers

### Transitional maturation profile

moDC_2 occupies an intermediate position in the monocyte-to-mature-DC continuum. While Reynolds et al. (2021) did not individually detail the marker profile of moDC_2 separately from the broader moDC group, its position in the PAGA trajectory indicates partial expression of maturation-associated transcripts that become fully expressed in moDC_3 (CD83, CCR7, LAMP3, CD40, CD86) and partial retention of monocyte-derived identity markers that characterize the less mature moDC_1 state.

### General moDC lineage markers

As part of the monocyte-derived DC lineage in skin, moDC_2 shares the general moDC phenotype. In the epidermis, moDCs are:

> "the epidermal HLA-DR+CD1a-Lang-CD11c+CD1c+cells are predominantly moDCs and correspond with non-LC like epidermal cells potent at stimulating T cell proliferation, pro-inflammatory cytokine production and transmission of HIV to CD4+T cells"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Human moDCs in general express CD14, CD11c, CD64, and CD88, and are potent antigen-presenting cells (Pachocki et al., 2025).

## Location

### Dermis and epidermis of adult skin

Like other moDC subtypes, moDC_2 is present in adult human skin. MoDCs occupy both dermal and epidermal compartments, with epidermal moDCs enriched in the HLA-DR+CD1a-Langerin-CD11c+CD1c+ gate (Reynolds et al., 2021). The dermal compartment contains moDCs that overlap phenotypically with CD14+ monocyte-derived cells characterized by McGovern et al. (2014) as having a short tissue half-life, consistent with ongoing monocyte recruitment and differentiation.

## Function

### Progressive maturation and antigen processing

As an intermediate maturation state, moDC_2 likely functions as an actively maturing antigen-presenting cell, having progressed beyond the initial antigen-sampling phase (moDC_1) but not yet acquired the full migratory and T-cell-priming capacity of moDC_3. This intermediate stage may correspond to cells undergoing antigen processing and beginning to upregulate co-stimulatory molecules necessary for effective T cell activation.

### Contribution to skin immune surveillance

moDC_2 contributes to the continuous cycle of monocyte-derived dendritic cell differentiation that maintains skin immune surveillance. The progressive maturation from moDC_1 through moDC_2 to moDC_3 represents the functional transition from tissue-resident sentinel to mature migratory DC, as described in the PAGA trajectory analysis by Reynolds et al. (2021).
