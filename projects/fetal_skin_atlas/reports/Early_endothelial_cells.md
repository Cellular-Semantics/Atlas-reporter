# Early Endothelial Cells (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [embryonic blood vessel endothelial progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002546) (CL:0002546, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)

## CL Mapping

**CL:0002546** — embryonic blood vessel endothelial progenitor cell
- Match type: broad
- Rationale: Early endothelial cells in the fetal skin atlas serve as the root progenitor population from which both arteriolar and venular lineages differentiate. CL:0002546 is defined as "an endothelial progenitor cell that participates in angiogenesis during development" and is classified as an embryonic mesodermal cell. This captures the progenitor, developmentally-active nature of these cells. No fetal-skin-specific endothelial progenitor term exists in CL.

## Summary

Early endothelial cells represent the root/progenitor blood vascular endothelial cell population in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), annotated under the broad "Vascular endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). Trajectory analysis establishes them as the founding state from which both main branches of the dermal vascular tree derive:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis showed that early endothelial cells in prenatal skin differentiated into either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles and arterioles) or venular pathway (postcapillary venules and venules), with expression of characteristic genes (for example, GJA5 for arteriolar and PLVAP for venular)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Their full differentiation potential is dependent on immune cell signals, as demonstrated by the failure of immunodeficient organoids to recapitulate endothelial heterogeneity.

## Markers

Marker genes are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. As the progenitor state, early endothelial cells are expected to express pan-endothelial markers such as PECAM1 (CD31), CDH5 (VE-cadherin), and CLDN5, without yet acquiring the arteriolar marker GJA5 or the venular marker PLVAP. The trajectory analysis identifies GJA5 as the defining arteriolar marker and PLVAP as the defining venular marker that are upregulated as differentiation proceeds from this progenitor state.

## Location

Early endothelial cells are found in the dermis of prenatal skin, present in the endothelial cell compartment throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Spatial analysis confirmed co-location of endothelial cells with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Bifurcating vascular progenitor

Early endothelial cells are the founding progenitor state of the fetal skin vascular tree, capable of differentiating into both arteriolar (capillaries, capillary arterioles, arterioles; GJA5+) and venular (postcapillary venules, venules; PLVAP+) lineages.

### Immune-dependent differentiation

Full differentiation from the early endothelial cell state into the complete complement of vessel subtypes requires immune cell signals:

> "immune cells are required to fully recapitulate in vivo endothelial cell development"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Absence of macrophages results in:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### Macrophage communication

Macrophage-endothelial crosstalk acts on early endothelial cells to drive downstream differentiation via pro-angiogenic ligand-receptor interactions:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
