# Differentiated Keratinocyte (c6) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match — no exact CL term for differentiated KC subcluster)

## Summary

Cluster c6 is a differentiated keratinocyte (KC) state in adult human epidermis, one of four differentiated KC clusters (c3, c4, c6, c7) identified by Leiden clustering in Reynolds et al. (2021). Differentiated KCs express suprabasal keratins KRT1 and KRT10 and terminal differentiation markers (FLG, IVL, CNFN), and are distinct from undifferentiated (c1, c5), proliferating (c2), and inflammatory differentiated (c8, "Differentiated KC*") states. Reynolds et al. (2021) describe two inferred differentiation trajectories from basal keratinocytes to terminally differentiated cells — one arm enriched for lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1) and one with lower LB levels — with IRF1 and SOX9 marking the two pathways. Clusters c3, c4, c6, and c7 represent distinct positions along these two suprabasal trajectories.

## Markers

> "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC* (cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021), Fig. 4D legend

> "Proliferating keratinocytes (CDK1+,PCNA+) have lower expression of suprabasal cell transcripts e.g. KRT1, KRT10 that characterize differentiated keratinocytes"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers of differentiated KCs:
- **KRT1, KRT10** — suprabasal keratins; define the differentiated state
- **FLG (filaggrin)**, **IVL (involucrin)**, **CNFN (cornifelin)** — terminal differentiation markers
- **ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1** — lamellar body-related transcripts (one differentiation arm)

## Location

Cluster c6 keratinocytes are suprabasal cells in the adult interfollicular epidermis. Reynolds et al. (2021) validate the spatial gene expression patterns of differentiated KC clusters against the Human Protein Atlas, confirming suprabasal localisation of KRT1 and KRT10 protein in normal adult skin.

## Function

### 1. Epidermal cornification programme

Differentiated KC clusters c3, c4, c6, and c7 collectively execute the cornification programme in adult skin. Reynolds et al. (2021) establish the dual-trajectory model:

> "Force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses reveal dual inferred differentiation trajectories from the stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA and CAV1/2)-enriched basal keratinocytes into terminally differentiated keratinocytes expressing CNFN, FLG and IVL"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "One arm expresses high levels of lamellar body (LB)­related transcripts such as ABCA12, CKAP4 and CLIP1 that characterize late epidermal differentiation and the other arm lower levels of LB-related transcripts"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### 2. Lamellar body lipid secretion

The LB-high arm of differentiation, marked by ABCA12, CKAP4, and CLIP1, secretes ceramide-rich lamellar body contents into the extracellular space of the stratum corneum to form the intercellular lipid lamellae of the skin barrier. Liu et al. (2022) describe this process:

> "force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses on scRNA-seq data defined two differentiation pathways of basal keratinocytes to suprabasal cells, with or without a high level of the lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (glucosylceramide transporter ABCA12; cytoskeleton-associated protein 4, CKAP4; and CAP-Gly domain-containing linker protein 1, CLIP1), that characterized late epidermal differentiation in healthy skin"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

### 3. Disease-associated gene expression

> "Notably, keratinocytes expressing LB-related transcripts co-express genes associated with autosomal recessive congenital ichthyosis, such as ABCA12, NIPAL4, SLC27A4 and TGM1"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) report altered proportions of each of the eight keratinocyte clusters in AD and psoriasis. The differentiated KC clusters c3, c4, c6, and c7 are distinguished from the inflammatory differentiated state (c8) by absence of ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20. Proportional changes in c6 relative to other clusters inform understanding of disease-driven epidermal reprogramming.
