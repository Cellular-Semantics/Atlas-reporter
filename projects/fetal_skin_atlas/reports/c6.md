# c6 Cell Type
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis  
Scope: adult

## Summary

The exact identity of the "c6" cell type in adult skin could not be definitively resolved from available data, as the cluster label is generic and lacks annotation mapping (Name Resolution, low confidence). Nevertheless, several lines of evidence from other studies employing the "C6" marker, chondroitin 6-sulfate (C6-S), shed light on its biological roles and localization in adult skin, particularly regarding keratinocyte proliferation, differentiation, and skin barrier function. Additionally, evidence regarding the C6 component of the complement system and the use of "C6" as a fluorescent marker for penetration studies in skin drug delivery provide context for alternative usages of the term in the skin field.

## Markers

No direct marker genes or proteins defining "c6" in adult skin have been identified in the provided supplementary findings. However, in other sources, "C6" most commonly refers to chondroitin 6-sulfate (C6-S), which localizes beneath the basal layer of the epidermis and appears to be detected using the CS-56 antibody.

> "C6-S was expressed beneath the keratin 14 (K14)-positive stem cells of the epidermal basal layer in C6st-1 WT mice."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

C6-S is absent in this layer in knockout and heterozygote animals, indicating its specific spatial presence in wild-type skin.

> "In contrast, C6-S was not detected in epidermal basal layer of C6st-1 HE and C6st-1 KO mice."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

Besides chondroitin 6-sulfate, the "C6" term appears in penetration and imaging studies, where it denotes a fluorescent probe rather than a biological marker.

> "C6 was used to as a substitute for LA to prepare C6-loaded skin keratin liposomes (C6L) [31]."
>
> — Wenfeng Wu et al. (2022)

## Location

### Epidermal Localization

Chondroitin 6-sulfate (C6-S) is specifically expressed beneath K14-positive basal keratinocyte stem cells in the epidermis of wild-type animals.

> "C6-S was expressed beneath the keratin 14 (K14)-positive stem cells of the epidermal basal layer in C6st-1 WT mice."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

Absence of C6-S in this area in genetically modified models further supports its specific localization at the epidermal-dermal interface.

### Penetration and Distribution in Skin

In drug delivery studies, "C6" markers (fluorescein derivatives) are used to track penetration dynamics. Such studies show superficial epidermal distribution and accumulation via hair follicle pathways:

> "the C6 intensity profile was high to 200 µm in depth, but then decreased quickly, suggesting the accumulation of C6 in the epidermis."
>
> — Eloy Pena-Rodríguez et al. (2021)

> "After 1 h, C6 delivered by skin keratin liposomes can permeate into the entire hair follicle and deposit at the follicle's root. The result demonstrated that hair follicles were the main penetration pathways of the C6L."
>
> — Wenfeng Wu et al. (2022)

## Function

### Regulation of Keratinocyte Proliferation and Differentiation

Chondroitin 6-sulfate plays an essential role in repressing keratinocyte hyperproliferation and maintaining proper differentiation and skin barrier function.

> "This manuscript concerns the role of a specific isoform of chondroitin sulfate, namely chondroitin 6-sulfate and its influence on proliferation of keratinocytes."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

> "the authors report defects in differentiation, increased proliferation and increased susceptibility to imiquimod, a topical agent that triggers a psoriasis-like effect in mice."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

> "the authors do convincingly show that differentiation, including cell adhesion and the permeability barrier are compromised, resulting in increased transepidermal water loss. There is hyperproliferation and suprabasal mitosis."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

### Epidermal Barrier and Inflammatory Priming

Loss of C6-S impacts gene expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines in adult skin and may predispose the epidermis to a primed inflammatory state.

> "In adult dorsal skin, the gene expression levels of some pro-inflammatory cytokines were increased by loss of C6-S."
>
> — K. Kitazawa et al. (2021)

### Drug Delivery and Penetration

Studies using C6 as a fluorescent marker highlight the importance of formulation for skin and follicular penetration, but this use is unrelated to endogenous cell types.

> "C6@ STFs-CPP and C6@DTFs-CPP Gel showed significantly enhanced C6 signals in the skin layer and tumor area compared to free C6 Gel, indicating that the CPP-modified transfersome formulations promoted skin and tumor penetration in vivo."
>
> — Ying Li et al. (2023)

> "the fluorescence signal in the C6L groups was first visualized in the hair follicles before it was distributed uniformly in the skin after 1 h."
>
> — Wenfeng Wu et al. (2022)

### Complement System Roles (Alternative Usage)

Some studies refer to "C6" as the complement component 6, which is unrelated to chondroitin 6-sulfate or endogenous cell types and instead plays a role in membrane attack complex (MAC) formation. However, experiments show that knockout of complement C6 does not alter certain inflammatory skin pathologies:

> "C6 is the   first among terminal complement components that associates with C5b to initiate the assembly of MAC."
>
> — Sidonia Mihai et al. (2011)

> "Both C6-deficient and control mice developed widespread blistering disease (Figure 2), to a similar extent, indicating that MAC is dispensable for blister formation in this model."
>
> — Sidonia Mihai et al. (2011)
