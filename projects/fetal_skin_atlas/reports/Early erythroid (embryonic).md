# Early Erythroid (Embryonic) in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [erythroid progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000038) (CL:0000038, broad match -- no CL term for embryonic-specific erythroid progenitors)

## Summary

Early erythroid (embryonic) cells are a population of erythroid progenitors identified in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024) that are distinguished from the definitive "Early erythroid" population by their embryonic identity. The "embryonic" qualifier indicates these cells derive from the primitive wave of haematopoiesis, originating in the yolk sac, and are expected to express embryonic hemoglobin genes (HBZ, HBE1). Popescu et al. (2019) demonstrated a temporal shift from embryonic hemoglobin (Gower 1 and 2) to fetal hemoglobin expression across developing haematopoietic tissues. This population represents the progenitor stage of embryonic erythropoiesis, preceding the more mature "Erythroid (embryonic)" cells also annotated in the atlas.

## Markers

Embryonic erythroid progenitors are expected to express or be transitioning toward expression of embryonic globin genes:

- **HBZ** (zeta-globin): A component of embryonic hemoglobin Gower 1 (zeta2-epsilon2) and Portland (zeta2-gamma2).
- **HBE1** (epsilon-globin): A component of embryonic hemoglobin Gower 1 (zeta2-epsilon2) and Gower 2 (alpha2-epsilon2).
- **TAL1**, **KLF1**: Erythroid-specifying transcription factors active at the progenitor stage (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "Erythroblasts show expression of haemoglobin genes, and a temporal shift from Gower 1 and 2 subunit expression (HBZ, HBE1) to fetal haemoglobin subunit expression (HBA1 and HBG2) between yolk sac and liver."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

## Location

### Prenatal skin

Early erythroid (embryonic) cells are annotated in the prenatal skin atlas spanning 7-17 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). Primitive erythroid cells originating from the yolk sac circulate in the embryonic bloodstream and can be found in multiple developing tissues during early gestation.

### Yolk sac origin

Primitive erythropoiesis originates in the yolk sac, where extra-embryonic mesoderm differentiates into haematopoietic progenitors that produce nucleated erythrocytes, megakaryocytes, and macrophages. In humans, embryonic hemoglobin appears 2-3 weeks after conception and is present in circulating blood from 4 to 10-12 weeks. The embryonic-to-fetal hemoglobin switch occurs at approximately 7-10 weeks (Popescu et al., 2019).

## Function

### Primitive erythropoiesis

Early erythroid (embryonic) progenitors give rise to primitive erythroid cells that produce embryonic hemoglobins: Hb Gower 1 (zeta2-epsilon2), Hb Gower 2 (alpha2-epsilon2), and Hb Portland (zeta2-gamma2). These hemoglobins have distinct oxygen-binding properties suited to early embryonic development before the placental circulation is fully established.

### Transitional population

Given the 7-17 PCW window of the atlas, this population likely represents a declining wave of primitive erythroid progenitors being progressively replaced by definitive erythropoiesis in the fetal liver. The temporal overlap of primitive and definitive erythropoiesis is a characteristic feature of this developmental period.

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological descriptions of early erythroid (embryonic) cells in prenatal skin were found in the traversed literature. Primitive erythroid progenitors in the yolk sac produce large, nucleated erythroblasts, in contrast to the smaller enucleated erythrocytes of definitive erythropoiesis.
