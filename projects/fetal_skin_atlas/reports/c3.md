# c3 (Macrophages, VSIG4 high)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Cluster c3 from the skin single-cell atlas is annotated as "Macrophages (VSIG4 high)", demonstrating high expression of the genes VSIG4 and CD52 (Supplementary Table 2, Supplementary Table 3). While explicit direct mapping to adult cell populations is lacking, supplementary evidence strongly supports this cluster as a subset of dermal macrophages marked by immunoregulatory and complement-associated genes. Functional studies and complementary literature detail a broad role of cutaneous macrophages and related myeloid cells in the regulation of inflammation, immune tolerance, response to injury, and skin homeostasis, often mediated through the complement system, of which VSIG4 is a component.

## Markers

Cluster c3 is characterized by the expression of VSIG4 and CD52 (Supplementary Table 3).

- **VSIG4:** Identified as a highly expressed marker gene for c3, VSIG4 is a complement receptor known to be associated with macrophage-mediated immune regulation. It acts as a negative regulator of T cell activation and mediates clearance of pathogens or damaged cells, implicating VSIG4 high macrophages in immune modulation and tissue homeostasis (Supplementary Table 2; Supplementary Table 3).
- **CD52:** Also detected as a c3 marker gene, CD52 is present on immune cells and is involved in immune signaling and modulation. Its presence implicates c3 cells as leukocytes of myeloid lineage (Supplementary Table 3).

## Location

### Skin Compartment and Context

- Functional annotation from the atlas identifies c3 as "Macrophages (VSIG4 high)" in skin, supporting localization in the dermal compartment (Supplementary Table 2).
- Literature evidence shows that complement system components (such as VSIG4 and C3 activation fragments) and complement receptor-expressing macrophages are distributed in both inflamed and normal human skin, particularly in the dermis:
  > "C3 and its activating protease, factor B, have both been detected in normal epidermis (6)."  
  > — Hammerberg et al. (1998)
- Immunofluorescence studies further confirm the deposition of complement fragments (e.g., C3d) in the epidermal basement membrane and within dermal vessels in healthy skin, supporting the physiological relevance and potential location of VSIG4 high macrophages (Nieboer, 1981).

### Developmental and Disease Context

- VSIG4 high macrophages are likely present in both healthy and diseased adult skin. Complement activation products have been observed in inflamed and normal anatomical regions:
  > "Complement deposits of C3b and C3d have been detected in and on epidermal cells within 24 h of exposure of the skin to UVB, indicating activation of C3 by UVB (10)."  
  > — Hammerberg et al. (1998)

## Function

The role of VSIG4 high macrophages in skin is closely tied to innate immunity, regulation of inflammation, complement system activity, and maintenance of tissue homeostasis.

### Regulation of Inflammation and Immunity

- Macrophages bearing complement receptors such as VSIG4 participate in modulating T cell-mediated responses and contact sensitivity:
  > "complement com- ponent 3 (C3), have been implicated in the regulation of T cell-mediated responses (1,2)."  
  > — Hammerberg et al. (1998)
- Complement activation fragments (such as C3a, C3b, and downstream products) modulate the function of myeloid cells—likely including c3 cluster macrophages—promoting or inhibiting inflammatory responses depending on the context:
  > "The C3a/C3aR axis contributes to cutaneous inflammation in IgE-dependent skin inflammation [79] and atopic dermatitis (data not shown). C3a induces the release of inflammatory mediators from skin mast cells [80]. ... In T cell mediated allergic contact dermatitis, C3 contributes the protection of skin inflammation [87]."  
  > — Masanori A. Murayama (2024)
- Macrophages and complement components are involved in regulation of immune checkpoint, tissue remodeling, and antigenic tolerance, especially after injury or exposure to ultraviolet light:
  > "C3 is required for UV-induced immunosuppression in the skin. C3 and its fragments are present in the epidermis, produced by keratinocytes, and activated locally in skin following UVB exposure."  
  > — Hammerberg et al. (1998)

### Participation in Tissue Remodeling, Injury, and Disease

- VSIG4 high macrophages, through their complement activity, contribute to the clearance of cellular debris, pathogens, and may participate in processes underlying psoriasis, bullous disorders, and tumor surveillance:
  > "C3 activation products, such as iC3b, C3b, and C3d, rather than anaphylatoxins C3a and C5a, can promote tumor growth and epithelial hyperplasia by interacting with the receptor CR3 on infiltrating myeloid cells."  
  > — Hunter Hudgins et al. (2025)
- In the setting of acute or chronic inflammation, complement receptor-expressing dermal macrophages regulate neutrophil and T cell infiltration, IL-17 production, and subsequent resolution of skin inflammation:
  > "In the absence of C3 the expression of psoriasis-relevant genes in the skin was impaired, neutrophil infiltration into the inflamed site was decreased, and IL-17 production by gd T cells in the skin and the draining lymph nodes was reduced."  
  > — Giacomassi et al. (2017)
