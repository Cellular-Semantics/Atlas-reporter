# Differentiated Keratinocyte (c3) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match — no exact CL term for differentiated KC subcluster)

## Summary

Cluster c3 represents a differentiated keratinocyte (KC) state in adult human epidermis, one of four differentiated KC clusters (c3, c4, c6, c7) identified by Leiden clustering analysis in Reynolds et al. (2021). Differentiated KCs are suprabasal cells characterised by expression of keratins KRT1 and KRT10 and terminal differentiation markers including filaggrin (FLG), involucrin (IVL), and cornifelin (CNFN). Reynolds et al. (2021) describe dual inferred differentiation trajectories from undifferentiated basal keratinocytes to terminally differentiated keratinocytes, one arm marked by high lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts and the other by lower LB transcript levels, with IRF1 and SOX9 as transcription factors differentially expressed between these pathways. The c3–c7 differentiated KC clusters collectively represent cells at varying positions along these two suprabasal differentiation trajectories in the interfollicular epidermis.

## Markers

> "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC* (cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021), Fig. 4D legend

> "Proliferating keratinocytes (CDK1+,PCNA+) have lower expression of suprabasal cell transcripts e.g. KRT1, KRT10 that characterize differentiated keratinocytes"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers of differentiated KCs:
- **KRT1, KRT10** — suprabasal keratins marking post-mitotic differentiated state
- **FLG (filaggrin)** — terminal differentiation; aggregates keratin filaments in the cornified layer
- **IVL (involucrin)** — cornified envelope precursor
- **CNFN (cornifelin)** — terminal differentiation marker

Lamellar body pathway (one differentiation arm):
- **ABCA12** — glucosylceramide transporter; essential for lamellar body lipid loading
- **CKAP4** — cytoskeleton-associated protein 4
- **CLIP1** — CAP-Gly domain-containing linker protein 1

## Location

Cluster c3 keratinocytes are suprabasal cells of the interfollicular epidermis (IFE) in adult skin. They reside above the basal layer (undifferentiated KCs, clusters 1 and 5) and have exited the cell cycle. As noted by Reynolds et al. (2021), their gene expression patterns are consistent with their spatial arrangement in the epidermis, corroborated by the Human Protein Atlas immunostaining data for KRT1 and KRT10.

## Function

### 1. Epidermal barrier formation

Differentiated KCs are the primary executors of the cornification programme. Reynolds et al. (2021) identify two inferred differentiation trajectories:

> "Force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses reveal dual inferred differentiation trajectories from the stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA and CAV1/2)-enriched basal keratinocytes into terminally differentiated keratinocytes expressing CNFN, FLG and IVL"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "One arm expresses high levels of lamellar body (LB)­related transcripts such as ABCA12, CKAP4 and CLIP1 that characterize late epidermal differentiation and the other arm lower levels of LB-related transcripts"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Liu et al. (2022) summarise this finding:

> "force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses on scRNA-seq data defined two differentiation pathways of basal keratinocytes to suprabasal cells, with or without a high level of the lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (glucosylceramide transporter ABCA12; cytoskeleton-associated protein 4, CKAP4; and CAP-Gly domain-containing linker protein 1, CLIP1), that characterized late epidermal differentiation in healthy skin"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

### 2. Ichthyosis-related gene expression

> "Notably, keratinocytes expressing LB-related transcripts co-express genes associated with autosomal recessive congenital ichthyosis, such as ABCA12, NIPAL4, SLC27A4 and TGM1"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

This co-expression connects differentiated KC transcriptional identity to skin barrier disease states at the molecular level.

### 3. Transcription factor regulation

Reynolds et al. (2021) identify two transcription factors — IRF1 and SOX9 — as differentially expressed between the two differentiation arms, with distinct immunofluorescence patterns confirming spatially distinct suprabasal compartments in healthy adult skin.

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) report proportional changes in differentiated KC clusters in atopic dermatitis (AD) and psoriasis lesional skin relative to healthy adult skin. The inflammatory differentiated KC state (cluster c8, "Differentiated KC*") expresses ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20, distinguishing it from the non-inflammatory differentiated clusters c3, c4, c6, and c7.
