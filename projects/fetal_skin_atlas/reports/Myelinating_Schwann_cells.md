# Myelinating Schwann Cells (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [myelinating Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000218) (CL:0000218, exact match)

## Summary

Myelinating Schwann cells are specialized glial cells of the peripheral nervous system that ensheath large-diameter axons in a one-to-one relationship and produce the myelin sheath essential for rapid saltatory nerve impulse conduction. In the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, myelinating Schwann cells are identified as a fine-grained annotation within the fetal (7-17 PCW) and skin organoid (SkO) datasets under the broad "Schwann cell" lineage. These cells are part of the neural cell compartment that is enriched in early gestation and co-locates with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1). Even after 19 weeks of SkO culture, Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states, indicating prolonged maturation (Gopee et al., 2024). They derive from the neural crest via Schwann cell precursors and immature Schwann cells, with SOX10 and EGR2 (Krox20) as key transcriptional regulators of the myelination program (Jessen and Mirsky, 2019; Horner et al., 2022).

## Markers

- **MBP (Myelin Basic Protein)**: A major structural protein of compact myelin whose expression marks active myelination.

  > "Schwann cells showed a differentiated cell phenotype, characterized by increased expression of pro-myelinating transcription factor Oct6 protein, myelinating regulator transcription factor Krox20 protein and mRNA (Figs 1a and b, Supplementary Figs S1 and S2), and the major myelin proteins MBP protein and mRNA"
  >
  > -- Imai et al. (2017)

- **MPZ (Myelin Protein Zero)**: The most abundant protein in peripheral nerve myelin. Expressed from the Schwann cell precursor stage onward.

  > "Markers that start to be expressed during Schwann cell precursor development include Gap43, MPZ, and S100b; and MPZ/S100b further increase during the development towards immature Schwann cells."
  >
  > -- Horner et al. (2022)

- **EGR2 (Krox20)**: The master transcription factor for myelination, activated by SOX10.

  > "The myelin cells on the other the hand express a large range of molecules that relate to the synthesis, maintenance or structure of the myelin sheath. This includes the major pro-myelin transcription factor Egr2 (Krox20), high levels of the enzymes that control cholesterol synthesis, structural proteins such as myelin protein zero (MPZ) and myelin basic protein (MBP), and membrane associated proteins like myelin associated glycoprotein (MAG), PMP22, and periaxin."
  >
  > -- Jessen and Mirsky (2019)

- **SOX10**: Expressed throughout the Schwann cell lineage, essential for specification, survival, and myelination.

  > "SOX10 is utilized at multiple steps during neural crest differentiation and is required for differentiation into pro-myelinating as well as myelinating cells, in addition to neural crest differentiation into oligodendrocyte and melanocyte lineages"
  >
  > -- Marathe et al. (2013)

- **S100B**: Pan-Schwann cell calcium-binding protein marker.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, myelinating Schwann cells are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW). They co-locate with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment:

> "TML macrophages were also predicted to co-locate with Schwann cells in prenatal skin ('early neurovascular microenvironment', ME1)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Myelination

The primary function is formation of myelin sheaths around large-diameter peripheral axons, enabling rapid saltatory conduction.

### Interaction with Macrophages in Prenatal Skin

> "TML macrophages were predicted to interact with Schwann cells, contributing to synapse formation and axon guidance (VEGFA-NRP1, VEGFA-NRP2, SEMA3C-NRP2 and SEMA3E-PLXND1)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "These findings suggest that prenatal skin macrophages may support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system during early gestation, as previously reported in mouse skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
