# Outer Root Sheath Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [outer root sheath cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002561) (CL:0002561, exact match)

## Summary

Outer root sheath (ORS) cells are a major component of the developing hair follicle identified in both prenatal human skin and the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, ORS cells are characterised by SLC26A7 expression and form part of the ORS/CL differentiation trajectory that derives from DPYSL2+ basal cells. ORS differentiation is associated with upregulation of BARX2 and SOX9. The companion layer (CL) develops along the same trajectory as the ORS, consistent with recent mouse studies.

## Location

ORS cells form the outer cylinder of the hair follicle, continuous with the basal epidermis. In the SkO, they are positioned in the outermost epithelial layer of the developing hair follicle.

## Function

The ORS provides structural support to the hair follicle and serves as a reservoir of stem cells that contribute to hair cycling, wound healing, and epidermal regeneration. ORS cells in the SkO follow the same differentiation trajectory as in prenatal skin, validating the organoid as a model for HF development.
