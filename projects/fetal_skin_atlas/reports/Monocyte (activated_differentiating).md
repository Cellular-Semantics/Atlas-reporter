# Activated/Differentiating Monocytes in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: prenatal
Cell Ontology: [classical monocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000860) (CL:0000860, broad match -- transitional state)

## Summary

Activated/differentiating monocytes represent a transitional cell state identified in prenatal human skin in the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas. These cells correspond to monocytes that have egressed from the bone marrow and are undergoing tissue adaptation, characterised by upregulation of IL1B and TNF signalling pathway genes. Suo et al. (2022) demonstrated that peripheral organ CCR2hi monocytes upregulate IL1B and TNF-alpha signalling genes compared to their bone marrow counterparts, representing a conserved maturation process as monocytes seed peripheral tissues. In prenatal skin, these activated monocytes may be transitioning toward macrophage or dendritic cell fates, consistent with the known capacity of fetal monocytes to differentiate into tissue-resident macrophages upon arrival in target organs (Hoeffel and Ginhoux, 2015). This population is distinct from the resting classical monocyte compartment and represents monocytes actively responding to the prenatal skin microenvironment.

## Markers

### Core markers: IL1B, CCR2, CD14, S100A8, S100A9

The activated/differentiating monocyte population retains classical monocyte markers (CD14, S100A8, S100A9) while acquiring tissue-adaptation signatures. Suo et al. (2022) showed that peripheral tissue monocytes are distinguished from bone marrow monocytes by their expression profiles:

> "Bone marrow CCR2 hi monocytes expressed proliferation genes, whereas peripheral organ CCR2 hi monocytes upregulated IL1B and other TNF-a signaling genes"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

- **IL1B** -- Interleukin-1 beta, upregulated in tissue-adapted monocytes; a hallmark of peripheral monocyte maturation (Suo et al., 2022).
- **CCR2** -- C-C chemokine receptor type 2, retained from the egressing monocyte state; mediates tissue recruitment (Suo et al., 2022).
- **CD14** -- Canonical monocyte marker, maintained during differentiation (Gopee et al., 2024).
- **S100A8 / S100A9** -- Alarmin genes characteristic of the monocyte lineage, maintained during tissue adaptation.

### Distinction from bone marrow monocytes

> "Among CCR2 hi monocytes, we distinguished bone marrowand peripheral organ-specific subpopulations"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

Bone marrow CCR2hi monocytes express G2/M checkpoint and proliferation genes, whereas the tissue-adapted activated monocytes downregulate these proliferation signatures and upregulate inflammatory and tissue-remodelling gene programs.

## Location

### Dermis

Activated/differentiating monocytes are located in the prenatal skin dermis, having been recruited from the circulation. They are part of the mesoderm-derived immune compartment present throughout gestation in the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas.

### Developmental timing

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Activated/differentiating monocytes are expected to appear once circulating monocytes begin seeding peripheral tissues, which occurs after granulocyte-monocyte progenitors (GMPs) produce blood monocytes from the fetal liver at 4-5 PCW (Miah et al., 2021).

## Function

### Tissue adaptation and inflammatory signalling

The activated/differentiating monocyte population represents cells transitioning from a circulating to a tissue-resident state. The upregulation of IL1B and TNF signalling genes in peripheral monocytes suggests these cells are being primed by the tissue microenvironment for effector or tissue-remodelling functions.

### Differentiation toward tissue macrophages

Fetal monocytes serve as precursors to tissue-resident macrophages in many organs. Hoeffel and Ginhoux (2015) demonstrated that fetal monocytes differentiate into macrophages when recruited to fetal tissues, progressively replacing yolk-sac-derived macrophages in most organs except brain microglia. In prenatal skin, the activated/differentiating monocyte state likely represents an intermediate in this macrophage differentiation pathway.

### Contribution to prenatal immune milieu

Suo et al. (2022) found that monocyte maturation in peripheral tissues is a conserved developmental process, with tissue-adapted monocytes acquiring both inflammatory and immunomodulatory capacities that may contribute to the balance between immune protection and tissue morphogenesis in prenatal skin.

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological data for activated/differentiating monocytes in prenatal skin was found in the traversed literature.
