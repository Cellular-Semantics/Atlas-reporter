# Plasma Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [plasma cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000786) (CL:0000786, exact match)

## Summary

Plasma cells (Plasma) are terminally differentiated antibody-secreting B lymphocytes identified as one of the 34 cell states in adult human skin by Reynolds et al. (2021). They represent the effector arm of humoral immunity. Skin-resident plasma cells express canonical markers including MZB1 and JCHAIN, and secrete immunoglobulins locally within the dermis. While present at low abundance in healthy adult skin, they are expanded in inflammatory and autoimmune skin conditions.

## Markers

Plasma cells are defined by expression of MZB1 (a cochaperone essential for plasma cell differentiation), JCHAIN (joining chain for polymeric immunoglobulins), and CD138 (SDC1, a surface marker):

> "Antibody-secreting plasma cells are effectors of the humoral immune response. Transcription factor Blimp1 (Prdm1) is essential for the generation and function of plasma cells, and it regulates many genes, including Mzb1 (pERp1)"
>
> — Andreani et al. (2018)

> "JCHAIN+ MZB1+ antibody-secreting B cells express JCHAIN, MZB1, and IGKC, which are involved in antibody secretion"
>
> — Lee et al. (2023)

## Location

Plasma cells are found in the dermis of adult human skin. In healthy skin they are rare, but can be detected in inflammatory and autoimmune conditions:

> "CD138+ plasma cells which can secrete antibodies are also present in the skin of PTM patients. Remarkably, IgG signal can be detected on the surface of these CD138+ plasma cells, suggesting that these plasma cells are secreting antibodies at local lesions"
>
> — Chen et al. (2021)

Reynolds et al. (2021) identified plasma cells as one of the 34 cell states in the comprehensive healthy adult skin scRNA-seq dataset encompassing all major immune and stromal populations.

## Function

### Antibody secretion

Plasma cells are the primary producers of immunoglobulins. Their terminal differentiation from B cells is orchestrated by the transcription factor Blimp1 (PRDM1) and involves coordinated upregulation of MZB1, JCHAIN, and immunoglobulin heavy and light chain genes:

> "Plasma cell differentiation involves coordinated changes in gene expression and functional properties of B cells"
>
> — Andreani et al. (2018)

MZB1 is required for multiple aspects of plasma cell differentiation, including secretion of IgM and β1-integrin activation that enables plasma cell trafficking to survival niches (Andreani et al., 2018).

### Role in skin immunity

In the skin dermis, resident plasma cells contribute to local humoral immunity by producing IgG and other immunoglobulin isotypes. Their local antibody secretion can sustain inflammatory responses in autoimmune skin diseases. Skin B cells, including plasma cell precursors, are present in healthy skin and can be activated by antigen-presenting cells including macrophages and dendritic cells (Cañedo-Dorantes and Cañedo-Ayala, 2019).
