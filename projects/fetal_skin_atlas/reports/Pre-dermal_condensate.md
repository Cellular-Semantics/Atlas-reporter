# Pre-dermal condensate
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)

Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

Pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells are transitional mesenchymal fibroblasts that represent the earliest committed precursors of the hair follicle dermal lineage. In mice, they are defined as PDGFRA+/FOXD1+/SOX2-low fibroblasts that aggregate to form the dermal condensate (Gopee et al., 2024). In human prenatal skin, pre-Dc cells were annotated using orthologous marker genes and were found to originate from HOXC5+ early fibroblasts. A distinctive feature is their spatial co-location with innate lymphoid cells, suggesting immune-mesenchymal crosstalk during early hair follicle formation.

## Markers

- **PDGFRA, FOXD1, SOX2 (low)**: Defining markers in mice.
  > "In mice, transitional PDGFRA + FOXD1 + SOX2 low fibroblasts termed pre-Dc cells aggregate to form the Dc (FOXD1 + SOX2 + ), which abuts the epithelial hair placode"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **CXCL12**: Upregulated during pre-Dc migration towards the epidermis.
  > "genes involved in regulation of cell adhesion (ADAMST1), cell-cell contacts (CLDN11) and directed migration (CXCL12) were upregulated as pre-Dc cells migrated towards the epidermis, which indicated a process of collective migration"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **ADAMTS1, CLDN11**: Genes involved in cell adhesion and cell-cell contacts during pre-Dc migration.

## Developmental Trajectory

Pre-Dc cells originate from HOXC5+ early fibroblasts:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis predicted that HOXC5 + early fibroblasts (located in the upper dermis (Fig. 2f) and abundant before 11 PCW (Extended Data Fig. 3c)) differentiated along two paths: the first (hair fibroblast trajectory) formed hair-specialized fibroblasts (pre-Dc cells, the Dc and Dp) and the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Signalling and Interactions

Pre-Dc cells express CXCL12 which interacts with ACKR3 on epidermal basal cells:

> "Receptor-ligand analysis predicted interactions between CXCL12 expressed by pre-Dc cells (Extended Data Fig. 4g,h) with ACKR3 on epidermal basal cells"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Pre-Dc cells co-locate with immune cells:

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "lymphoid tissue inducer and ILC3 cells were also predicted to co-locate and interact with pre-Dc cells through ligand-receptor signals implicated in the regulation of cellular adhesion and migration (CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4)"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Cross-species Comparison

> "cross-species data integration of human and mouse HF 41 showed that human pre-Dc cells and the Dc aligned not only to their counterparts in mouse skin but also to fibroblasts in earlier stages of differentiation"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "This result suggests that for corresponding cell types, HF fibroblasts are in a more differentiated state in mouse compared to human prenatal skin."
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

Pre-Dc cells reside in the dermal compartment of prenatal skin, migrating towards the epidermis to aggregate beneath hair placodes. They are part of the hair fibroblast trajectory observed from 12 PCW.

## Function

Pre-Dc cells serve as the immediate precursors of the dermal condensate. Their functions include directed migration, aggregation (expressing COL6A3, MFAP4, PTK7), and immune-mesenchymal crosstalk with LTi and ILC3 cells.

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological description beyond their identification as loosely associated mesenchymal fibroblasts undergoing collective migration.
