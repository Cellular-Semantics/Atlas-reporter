# MEMP - Megak in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [megakaryocyte-erythroid progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000050) (CL:0000050, broad match -- MEMP includes mast cell potential not captured by CL MEP; this is a megakaryocyte-biased substate)

## Summary

MEMP - Megak is a cell state identified in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024) representing a megakaryocyte-biased subpopulation within the megakaryocyte-erythroid-mast cell progenitor (MEMP) compartment. The MEMP was first characterized at single-cell resolution in human fetal liver by Popescu et al. (2019), who showed that it lies downstream of HSC/MPPs and branches into erythroid, megakaryocyte, and mast cell lineages. In the prenatal skin atlas, MEMP is resolved into substates including this megakaryocyte-biased population (MEMP - Megak). Suo et al. (2022) confirmed the presence of megakaryocyte/erythroid progenitors in developing skin.

## Markers

Popescu et al. (2019) identified genes dynamically modulated during megakaryocyte specification from the MEMP:

> "Genes dynamically modulated in the specification of erythroid, megakaryocyte and mast cell lineages are distinct: TAL1 and KLF1 in erythroid; F11R, PBX1 and MEIS1 in megakaryocyte; and CPA3 and MS4A2 in mast cells."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

- **F11R** (JAM-A/CD321): A junctional adhesion molecule upregulated during megakaryocyte commitment.
- **PBX1**: A pre-B-cell leukemia transcription factor involved in megakaryocyte specification.
- **MEIS1**: A TALE-class homeobox transcription factor co-regulating megakaryocyte development.

These markers distinguish the megakaryocyte-biased MEMP from the erythroid-biased and mast cell-biased states.

## Location

### Prenatal skin

MEMP - Megak cells are annotated in the prenatal human skin atlas between 7-17 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). They reside within the haematopoietic progenitor compartment and co-locate within the "Myeloid/Megakaryocyte-erythroid enriched" microenvironment (ME2).

> "Megakaryocytes, erythroid cells, mast cells and MEMP, are present in skin."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

### Fetal liver

The fetal liver is the principal site of MEMP-derived megakaryopoiesis during the 7-17 PCW window (Popescu et al., 2019).

## Function

### Megakaryocyte lineage commitment

MEMP - Megak represents early commitment from the shared MEMP toward the megakaryocyte lineage. These cells express F11R, PBX1, and MEIS1, driving differentiation into megakaryocyte progenitors that eventually produce platelets (Popescu et al., 2019). Fetal megakaryocytes differ from their adult counterparts in being smaller, with lower ploidy, and producing functionally hyporeactive platelets.

### Contribution to fetal platelet production

The presence of megakaryocyte-biased MEMPs in prenatal skin, alongside mature megakaryocytes (also annotated in the atlas), suggests that the skin microenvironment can support early stages of megakaryopoiesis during fetal development (Suo et al., 2022).

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological descriptions of MEMP - Megak cells in prenatal skin were found in the traversed literature.
