# Undifferentiated Keratinocyte (c5) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [basal cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002187) (CL:0002187, broad match — undifferentiated KC includes stem-like basal cells not fully captured by this term)

## Summary

Cluster c5 is an undifferentiated keratinocyte (KC) state in adult human epidermis. Reynolds et al. (2021) identify two undifferentiated KC clusters (c1 and c5), characterised by expression of basal epidermal proteins KRT5 and KRT14, enrichment in the CD49f-high (ITGA6-high) FACS gate, and expression of stemness-associated transcription factors such as TP63. Undifferentiated KCs occupy the base of the inferred differentiation trajectory — enriched for stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA, CAV1/2) — from which both differentiated trajectory arms originate. Cluster c5 represents a second transcriptionally distinct undifferentiated KC state alongside c1, both sharing the basal/undifferentiated identity but potentially reflecting spatial or functional heterogeneity within the basal epidermal compartment.

## Markers

> "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC* (cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021), Fig. 4D legend

> "Undifferentiated keratinocytes transcribe basal epidermal proteins (KRT5, KRT14) and are abundant in the CD49fhi FACS gate"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers of undifferentiated KCs:
- **KRT5, KRT14** — basal epidermal keratins; define undifferentiated state
- **TP63** — master transcription factor for epidermal progenitor identity
- **ITGA6 (CD49f)** — integrin alpha-6; hemidesmosome component; marks basal cells
- **PPP3CA, CAV1, CAV2** — stem cell-enriched genes

Liu et al. (2022) confirm:

> "undifferentiated basal keratinocytes had high expression of basal epidermal proteins (KRT5 and KRT14), hemidesmosome molecules [integrin alpha-6, ITGA6; and collagen alpha-1 (XVII) chain, COL17A1] and stemness factor (tumor protein 63, TP63)"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

## Location

Cluster c5 keratinocytes are basal cells of the adult interfollicular epidermis, residing on the basement membrane and expressing hemidesmosome genes (ITGA6, COL17A1) that anchor them to it. Undifferentiated KCs are the progenitor pool from which the suprabasal differentiated compartment is continuously replenished.

## Function

### 1. Epidermal progenitor maintenance

Undifferentiated KCs (clusters 1 and 5) form the stem/progenitor pool at the base of the two differentiation trajectories identified by Reynolds et al. (2021):

> "Force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses reveal dual inferred differentiation trajectories from the stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA and CAV1/2)-enriched basal keratinocytes into terminally differentiated keratinocytes expressing CNFN, FLG and IVL"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

TP63 expression is a defining feature of the basal progenitor state: it maintains proliferative capacity and suppresses terminal differentiation, ensuring the epidermal stem cell pool is maintained.

### 2. Self-renewal and differentiation commitment

The undifferentiated KC clusters sit at the origin point of the PAGA-inferred differentiation graph. Cells in these clusters are either self-renewing or committing to one of two suprabasal differentiation pathways — distinguished by the degree of lamellar body-related gene expression in terminally differentiated offspring.

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) show that proportions of undifferentiated KC clusters change in AD and psoriasis lesional skin. Inflammatory differentiated KCs (cluster c8, "Differentiated KC*") express ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20, representing an abnormal differentiation state distinct from the healthy clusters c1 and c5. Changes in the undifferentiated compartment reflect disease-driven epidermal remodelling.
