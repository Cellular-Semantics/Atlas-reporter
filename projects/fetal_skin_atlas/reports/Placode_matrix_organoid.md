# Placode/Matrix Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [hair follicle cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002559) (CL:0002559, broad match -- no specific CL term for placode/matrix)

## Summary

Placode/matrix cells are a combined annotation in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020) representing the hair follicle epidermal placode and matrix cell states. In prenatal skin, Gopee et al. (2024) identified these as distinct populations: the placode (a prenatal-specific cell state absent in adult HFs) and the matrix (SHH+), which is the proliferative compartment of the hair follicle bulb. In the SkO, these states overlap or are captured as a single transitional population. Matrix cells arise from the epidermal placode and express SHH and WNT10B as they differentiate.

## Location

Placode/matrix cells are located in the developing hair follicle: the placode at the epidermal invagination site, and the matrix in the bulb region surrounding the dermal papilla.

## Function

The placode initiates hair follicle formation through interactions with dermal condensate cells. Matrix cells are the highly proliferative progenitors in the hair follicle bulb that give rise to the inner root sheath, cuticle, cortex, and hair shaft. Their expression of CXCL14 may recruit Treg cells for immune protection during early HF development.
