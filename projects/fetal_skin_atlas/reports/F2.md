# Fibroblast 2 (F2) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [skin fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002620) (CL:0002620, broad match — no exact CL term for this inflammatory, disease-enriched fibroblast subtype)

## Summary

F2 corresponds to Fb2 in Reynolds et al. (2021), a disease-enriched fibroblast state in adult human dermis. In healthy skin, Fb2 is a minor population alongside the dominant Fb1 (F1) and minor Fb3 (F3). Fb2 is defined by upregulated expression of the chemokines CXCL12 and CCL19, and is significantly enriched in both atopic dermatitis and psoriasis (lesional and non-lesional skin). A key finding from Reynolds et al. is that fetal skin fibroblasts share gene expression with Fb2 adult fibroblasts — including COL1A1, COL1A2, and COL6A1 — suggesting that the Fb2 programme is a developmentally conserved fibroblast state specialised toward ECM remodelling that is co-opted in adult inflammatory skin disease.

Disease-enriched and defining markers of F2/Fb2:
- **CXCL12 (SDF-1)** — CXC chemokine ligand 12; leukocyte chemoattractant and stem cell niche factor
- **CCL19** — CC chemokine ligand 19; CCR7 ligand driving DC and T cell migration
- **COL1A1** — collagen type I alpha 1; ECM structural gene (shared with Fb1, Fb3; enriched in fetal alignment)
- **COL1A2** — collagen type I alpha 2; ECM structural gene
- **COL6A1** — collagen type VI alpha 1; microfibrillar ECM component linking structural matrix to cells

Pan-fibroblast ECM markers (shared with F1, F3):
- **MMP2** — matrix metalloproteinase 2
- **NT5E (CD73)** — ecto-5'-nucleotidase

## Location

F2 cells reside in the adult dermis alongside F1 and F3 fibroblasts. In healthy skin they form a minor subpopulation; in inflammatory disease they expand significantly. The expression of CCL19 by F2 fibroblasts positions them as organizers of local immune cell positioning — CCL19 attracts CCR7+ migratory DCs (MigDC) and CCR7+ T cells, suggesting that F2 fibroblasts help create immunologically active niches within the dermis.

## Function

### 1. Chemokine-mediated immune cell recruitment

F2 fibroblasts produce CXCL12 and CCL19, two chemokines with major roles in leukocyte trafficking:
- **CXCL12** attracts CXCR4+ haematopoietic progenitors, monocytes, and T cells; also maintains plasma cell niches
- **CCL19** attracts CCR7+ cells, including migratory DCs and naïve/central memory T cells

This chemokine profile makes F2 fibroblasts key organizers of the dermal immune microenvironment, particularly during inflammation when their numbers expand.

### 2. ECM remodelling with fetal programme features

The F2 ECM gene signature — COL1A1, COL1A2, COL6A1 — is shared with fetal skin fibroblasts. Reynolds et al. interpret this as evidence that fetal fibroblasts are functionally specialised toward ECM remodelling and maintenance, and that this programme is re-deployed in the adult F2 state during inflammatory conditions:

> "fibroblasts in
> fetal skin express more genes relating to Fb2 adult fibroblasts, including COL1A1, COL1A2
> and COL6A1, suggesting they are functionally specialized towards ECM remodeling and
> maintenance"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### 3. Structural support for immune niches

The combined expression of structural collagens (COL1A1, COL1A2, COL6A1) and immune-recruiting chemokines (CXCL12, CCL19) positions F2 as an immunomodulatory fibroblast capable of both maintaining ECM integrity and organising the local immune cell composition of inflammatory foci in the dermis.

## Disease relevance

F2/Fb2 is the fibroblast subset most directly linked to inflammatory skin disease. Reynolds et al. report significant enrichment in both psoriasis and atopic dermatitis:

> "skin fibroblast (F2) cell program is also augmented in AD and psoriasis"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Francis et al. (2024) provide orthogonal evidence for an inflammatory fibroblast state in psoriasis skin that resolves upon IL-23 blockade, consistent with the F2 programme. The expansion of F2 fibroblasts in disease — producing CCL19 that recruits CCR7+ MigDCs and T cells — connects the stromal fibroblast compartment to the adaptive immune activation that drives psoriasis and atopic dermatitis pathogenesis.
