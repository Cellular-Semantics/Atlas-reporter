# Collecting Lymphatic Endothelial Cell (LE2) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [lymphatic endothelial cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002138) (CL:0002138, broad match — no exact CL term for collecting lymphatic EC substate)

## Summary

LE2 is one of two lymphatic endothelial cell (LEC) subclusters in adult human dermis identified by Reynolds et al. (2021). Compared to LE1, LE2 expresses higher PDPN and lower CCL21, and Reynolds et al. (2021) identify LE2 as resembling PDPN+ collecting lymphatic vessels in human dermis. Collecting lymphatic vessels receive lymph from the initial capillaries (LE1), propel it unidirectionally toward the lymph nodes via valves and smooth muscle contraction, and form continuous, tight (zipper-like) endothelial junctions rather than the button-like junctions of initial lymphatics. Both LE1 and LE2 express pan-lymphatic markers LYVE1 and PDPN, and are transcriptionally distinct from the PECAM1+EMCN+ blood vascular endothelium.

Key markers:
- **PDPN (podoplanin)** — higher in LE2 than LE1; marker of collecting lymphatic vessels
- **LYVE1** — lymphatic hyaluronan receptor; expressed by lymphatic endothelium
- **PROX1** — lymphatic master transcription factor; pan-LEC marker
- **CCL21** — lower in LE2 relative to LE1

Liu et al. (2022) confirm the pan-lymphatic endothelial identity:

> "Endothelial cells in the healthy adult dermis constitute the vascular endothelium (PECAM1, EMCN, SELE, CD93, CLDN5, VWF, and CDH5) and lymphatic endothelium (LYVE1, PDPN, PROX1, and CLDN5)"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

## Location

LE2 cells form the collecting lymphatic vessels of the adult dermis. Collecting lymphatic vessels receive lymph from the initial lymphatics (LE1 capillaries), are covered by smooth muscle cells that drive unidirectional flow, and contain intraluminal valves. Their endothelial cells have elongated morphology with continuous, tight zipper-like junctions rather than the button junctions of initial lymphatics. Reynolds et al. (2021) identify both LE clusters as part of the non-immune, non-keratinocyte stromal compartment of adult skin.

## Function

### 1. Lymph propulsion and transport

Collecting lymphatic vessels (LE2) are responsible for transporting lymph — containing interstitial fluid, immune cells, and antigens — from the dermis to the skin-draining lymph nodes. Their smooth-muscle cell coating generates the contractile force for lymph propulsion; LE2 endothelial cells form the inner lining of these contractile vessels.

### 2. Unidirectional lymph flow regulation

Collecting lymphatics contain intraluminal valves that ensure unidirectional transport of lymph. The LE2 PDPN-high identity is consistent with the known enrichment of podoplanin in collecting lymphatic endothelium, where it plays a role in regulating lymphatic tone and valve function.

### 3. DC transport to lymph nodes

Dendritic cells that have entered the initial lymphatics (LE1 capillaries) travel passively within the lymph through the collecting lymphatic vessels (LE2) to reach the subcapsular sinus of the skin-draining lymph node. Reynolds et al. (2021) link the LE1 CCL21 expression specifically to DC recruitment, while LE2 serves as the conduit for downstream transport.

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) characterise both LE1 and LE2 in healthy adult skin as part of the stromal endothelial atlas. The differential CCL21/PDPN expression between LE1 and LE2 recapitulates the established distinction between initial and collecting lymphatics in human dermis, providing a molecular basis for understanding how lymphatic dysfunction in inflammatory skin diseases (e.g. dermal lymphoedema in AD) could be linked to specific LEC subsets.
