# Non-Myelinating Schwann Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [non-myelinating Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002374) (CL:0002374, exact match)

## Summary

Non-myelinating Schwann cells (Schwaan_2_non_myelinating, using the atlas-assigned 'Schwaan' spelling) are one of two Schwann cell subtypes identified by Reynolds et al. (2021) in adult human skin. Also known as Remak Schwann cells, they form Remak bundles that ensheath multiple small-diameter unmyelinated C-fibres including nociceptors and autonomic nerve fibres. They are defined by expression of NGFR (p75 neurotrophin receptor), GFAP, GLUL (glutamine synthetase), APOD, FABP7, and SMOC2, with low or absent expression of myelin genes MPZ and MBP that characterise myelinating Schwann cells (Schwaan_1). S100B is expressed in both Schwann cell subtypes as a pan-Schwann cell marker.

## Markers

Defining markers of non-myelinating (Remak) Schwann cells:

> "Non-myelinating Schwann cells express GLUL, APOD, NGFR, SMOC2, FABP7 and are distinguished from myelinating Schwann cells by low/absent MPZ and MBP expression"
>
> — Zhang et al. (2025)

NGFR as a defining marker:

> "NGFR (p75NTR) is a defining marker of Remak (non-myelinating) Schwann cells and distinguishes them from myelinating Schwann cells in peripheral nerves and skin"
>
> — Ioghen et al. (2020)

The two-subtype Schwann cell classification:

> "Two Schwann cell subtypes exist in adult human skin: myelinating (MPZ+/MBP+) and non-myelinating (NGFR+/GFAP+/GLUL+)"
>
> — Zhang et al. (2025)

Key markers: **NGFR** (p75NTR, defining marker), **GFAP** (glial fibrillary acidic protein), **GLUL** (glutamine synthetase), **APOD** (apolipoprotein D), **FABP7** (fatty acid binding protein 7), **SMOC2** (SPARC-related modular calcium-binding protein 2). Low/absent: MPZ, MBP.

## Location

Non-myelinating Schwann cells are distributed throughout the dermis along unmyelinated nerve fibre bundles. Each Remak Schwann cell simultaneously ensheaths multiple unmyelinated C-fibres in a common Remak bundle, providing metabolic support and trophic signals to multiple axons. They are particularly abundant in skin areas with dense innervation, including fingertips and genital skin.

## Function

### Remak bundle formation and C-fibre support

Non-myelinating Schwann cells form Remak bundles that house multiple C-fibre axons — unmyelinated fibres responsible for nociception (pain), itch, temperature sensation, and autonomic function. Unlike myelinating Schwann cells that produce compact myelin, Remak Schwann cells do not produce myelin but provide essential trophic and metabolic support to the C-fibres they ensheath.

### Neurotrophin signalling

NGFR (p75NTR) expressed on non-myelinating Schwann cells mediates signalling downstream of nerve growth factor (NGF) and other neurotrophins. This signalling regulates Schwann cell survival, axonal maintenance, and the response to nerve injury. NGFR can also act as a co-receptor for pro-neurotrophins to trigger apoptosis in specific contexts.

### Glutamine metabolism and axon support

GLUL (glutamine synthetase) is a functional enzyme expressed in non-myelinating Schwann cells that enables them to convert glutamate to glutamine, playing a role in neurotransmitter recycling and potentially protecting C-fibres from excitotoxic glutamate accumulation.

### Role in skin innervation and itch

Given the role of C-fibres in itch signalling, non-myelinating Schwann cells indirectly modulate itch sensation in inflammatory skin conditions such as atopic dermatitis. Changes in the neurotrophin milieu during skin inflammation can alter non-myelinating Schwann cell gene expression and their support of sensory C-fibre function.
