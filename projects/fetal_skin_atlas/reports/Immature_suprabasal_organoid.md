# Immature Suprabasal Keratinocyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match -- immature suprabasal state not separately represented in CL)

## Summary

Immature suprabasal keratinocytes are an early embryonic epidermal cell state identified in prenatal human skin and recapitulated in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, immature suprabasal cells were classified as immature IFE cells present from 7 PCW, decreasing after 11 PCW during the embryonic-to-fetal transition. These cells represent an early differentiating population above the basal layer, preceding the mature suprabasal IFE keratinocytes that express late differentiation markers.

## Markers

Immature suprabasal keratinocytes express early differentiation keratins while retaining some features of basal cells. They are distinguished from periderm, which constitutes the outermost layer in embryonic skin, and from mature suprabasal keratinocytes that emerge later.

## Atlas Context

>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

Immature suprabasal keratinocytes are located in the suprabasal layers of the early embryonic epidermis, between the basal layer and the periderm. In the SkO, they occupy a corresponding suprabasal position.

## Function

These cells represent an early stage of epidermal stratification, contributing to the formation of the multi-layered epidermis before the onset of mature terminal differentiation programs. Their decline after 11 PCW coincides with the maturation of the epidermis and the replacement of the periderm by a fully stratified cornified epithelium.
