# Capillaries (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [dermal microvascular endothelial cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011030) (CL:0011030, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)

## CL Mapping

**CL:0002144** — capillary endothelial cell
- Match type: exact
- Rationale: Capillaries in the fetal skin atlas are annotated as dermal capillary endothelial cells lining the smallest blood vessels. CL:0002144 is defined as "an endothelial cell found in capillaries" and is a subclass of microvascular endothelial cell (CL:2000008). No skin-specific capillary endothelial cell term exists in CL, making this the most precise available match.

## Summary

Capillaries represent a fine-grained blood vascular endothelial cell population in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), annotated under the broad "Vascular endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). They occupy a central position on the arteriolar differentiation trajectory, between early endothelial cells and capillary arterioles. Capillary cells in prenatal skin display active sprouting angiogenesis potential, enabled by macrophage-derived pro-angiogenic signals. Their expansion and maturation depend critically on immune cell crosstalk: immunodeficient skin organoids (SkOs) show markedly reduced endothelial heterogeneity and quantity compared to prenatal skin.

## Markers

Marker genes for Capillaries are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as differentially expressed genes (DEGs) used for annotation. The arteriolar trajectory (which includes capillaries) is characterised by **GJA5** expression:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis showed that early endothelial cells in prenatal skin differentiated into either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles and arterioles) or venular pathway (postcapillary venules and venules), with expression of characteristic genes (for example, GJA5 for arteriolar and PLVAP for venular)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

VEGF receptors **KDR** and **FLT1** are expressed on arteriolar-lineage cells in prenatal skin, downstream of GATA2:

> "Although expression of vascular endothelial growth factors (VEGF), VEGFA and VEGFB were increased in SkO cells, their receptors (KDR and FLT1) on SkO capillary arterioles were downregulated compared to prenatal skin"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Location

Capillary endothelial cells are part of the microvasculature of the prenatal dermis. They co-locate with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Capillary cells are present as part of the mesoderm-derived endothelial compartment throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Sprouting angiogenesis

Prenatal skin capillary cells exhibit elevated sprouting angiogenesis potential:

> "sprouting angiogenesis potential, assessed by scoring the 'tip' cell state, was increased in both SkO capillary arteriole cells and prenatal skin arteriole, capillary arteriole and capillary cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

This capacity is supported by macrophage-derived pro-angiogenic signals:

> "Gene module expression profiles suggested that sprouting angiogenesis (growth of new vessels) was promoted by LYVE1+ and TML macrophages, blood vessel morphogenesis by LYVE1+ macrophages and endothelial cell chemotaxis by iron-recycling macrophages"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### Requirement for immune cells

The full developmental programme of capillary endothelial cells depends on immune cell signals:

> "immune cells are required to fully recapitulate in vivo endothelial cell development"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

In the absence of macrophages:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### Macrophage communication

Predicted ligand-receptor interactions mediate reciprocal signalling between macrophages and capillary endothelial cells:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
