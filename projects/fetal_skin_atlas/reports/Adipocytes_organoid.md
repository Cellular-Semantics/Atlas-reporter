# Adipocytes in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid

## Summary

Adipocytes are a mesenchymal cell population identified in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model derived from human pluripotent stem cells (Gopee et al., 2024). The SkO generates a cyst-like structure with stratified epidermis and a fat-rich dermis containing adipocytes, pigmented hair follicles, and sebaceous glands (Lee et al., 2020). In the integrated analysis of prenatal skin, adult skin, and SkO data, adipocytes are annotated as a distinct broad cell lineage within the organoid. Although the SkO model closely recapitulates in vivo epidermal and dermal cell types, fibroblasts and other mesenchymal cells (including adipocytes) remain in a prenatal-like state even after prolonged culture, with a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

Adipocytes in the SkO are identified based on canonical adipocyte markers and their broad lineage annotation in the integrated dataset:

- **ADIPOQ**: Adiponectin, a classical mature adipocyte marker (Supplementary Table 3).
- **PPARG**: Peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma, a master regulator of adipogenesis (Supplementary Table 3).

## Location

### In the SkO model

Adipocytes reside within the fat-rich dermal compartment of the SkO. The organoid model generates a cyst-like structure where adipocytes are distributed in the dermis, reflecting the dermal adipose tissue layer observed in native skin.

> "a cyst-like skin organoid composed of stratified epidermis, fat-rich dermis and pigmented hair follicles that are equipped with sebaceous glands"
>
> -- Lee et al. (2020)

### Comparison with prenatal skin

In prenatal skin, adipocytes are also identified as a distinct population. The SkO adipocytes match more closely with the prenatal state than with adult dermal adipose tissue.

## Function

### Dermal structural support

Adipocytes contribute to the structural architecture of the SkO dermis, forming the fat-rich component that underlies the epidermal layers. In native skin, dermal adipocytes play roles in thermal insulation, energy storage, and signalling to neighbouring cell types including hair follicle stem cells and fibroblasts.

### Developmental state in SkO

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

This observation extends to the adipocyte lineage, suggesting that SkO adipocytes retain a prenatal-like developmental state, consistent with the broader finding that dermal cell maturation in the SkO lags behind epidermal maturation.
