# Companion Layer Cells in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid

## Summary

Companion layer (CL) cells are a specialised hair follicle cell population identified in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model (Gopee et al., 2024). The companion layer forms a single-cell layer positioned between the outer root sheath (ORS) and the inner root sheath (IRS) of the hair follicle. In the integrated prenatal skin and SkO trajectory analysis, CL cells arise along the ORS/CL differentiation trajectory from POSTN+ basal cells, together with DPYSL2+ basal cells and ORS cells (Gopee et al., 2024). The CL is one of the hair follicle components faithfully recapitulated in the SkO model, reflecting the close correspondence between SkO and prenatal skin epidermal and hair follicle cell states.

## Markers

- **KRT75**: Keratin 75, a known companion layer marker in the hair follicle.
- **KRT6**: Keratin 6, expressed in the companion layer.

The companion layer is identified in the atlas scRNA-seq data as a distinct cell state within the hair follicle epidermal compartment (Supplementary Table 6).

> "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### In the SkO model

CL cells are located between the ORS and IRS within SkO hair follicles. They form part of the hair follicle architecture recapitulated during SkO differentiation from pluripotent stem cells.

### In the hair follicle structure

The companion layer constitutes the innermost layer of the ORS and is morphologically and biochemically distinct from cuboidal ORS cells. Companion cells are elongated and vertically oriented, forming intimate contact with cells of Henle's layer through desmosomes and gap junctions, providing a slippage plane that allows the growing hair fibre to move along the ORS (Ellis et al., 2001).

## Function

### Structural role in hair follicle

The companion layer forms a slippage plane between the upward-moving hair shaft and IRS and the stationary ORS, facilitating hair growth. The CL cells are in intimate contact with Henle's layer cells through desmosomes and gap junctions, forming a mechanically distinct boundary.

### Differentiation trajectory

In the SkO model, CL cells differentiate along the ORS/CL trajectory from POSTN+ basal cells, with upregulation of genes associated with ORS differentiation such as BARX2 and SOX9 (Gopee et al., 2024).

> "ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Interactions with mesenchymal cells

CellPhoneDB analysis in the atlas revealed that interactions between HF dermal cells and late epithelial cells including CL were conserved between prenatal skin and SkO (Fig. 2g, Extended Data Fig. 5c; Supplementary Table 8).
