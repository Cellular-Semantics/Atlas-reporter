# Innate T Type1 Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: no_direct_match — NTR filed (see cl_term_request.json)

## Summary

Innate T type1 cells are PLZF (ZBTB16)-expressing unconventional alpha-beta T cells with type 1 (IFN-γ-producing) effector potential, identified in the prenatal human skin atlas as a distinct T cell subset within the fetal T cell lineage. They arise via thymocyte-thymocyte (T-T) selection in the fetal thymus and are essentially absent in postnatal thymus, classifying them as developmental-specific unconventional T cells (Suo et al., 2022). T cells broadly — including Innate T type1 — emerge in skin from approximately 10 PCW, coinciding with formation of the thymus, bone marrow and spleen (Gopee et al., 2024). In vitro differentiation experiments support T-T selection as the origin mechanism for this population. Unlike their mouse counterparts, human innate-like T cells develop a mixed rather than discrete type 1 effector fate (Loh et al., 2023).

## Markers

- **ZBTB16 (PLZF)**: master transcription factor defining innate T cells; expressed across Innate T type1, type3, and related unconventional T cell populations (Suo et al., 2022)
- **alpha-beta TCR**: Innate T type1 cells express a varied alpha-beta TCR repertoire (Suo et al., 2022)
- **Type 1 DC co-localisation**: Innate T type1 cells co-localise with DC1 cells near the cortico-medullary junction in the thymic medulla (Suo et al., 2022)
- Single-cell TCRαβ sequencing was used in the atlas to resolve T cell subsets (Gopee et al., 2024)

## Location

T cells including Innate T type1 cells emerge in prenatal skin from approximately 10 PCW:

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

Pre-dermal condensate cells co-located with lymphoid cells in spatial transcriptomics:

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

In the developing thymus, Innate T type1 cells are located near the cortico-medullary junction, co-localising with type 1 dendritic cells (Suo et al., 2022).

## Function

Innate T type1 cells are developmental-specific unconventional T cells with rapid type 1 (IFN-γ/TNF-α) effector potential. Their near-absence in postnatal thymus marks them as a fetal-enriched population:

> "Type 1 and 3 innate T cells were almost negligible in postnatal thymus, whereas CD8AA T cell abundance rebounded in pediatric age groups before a further decline in adulthood. Thus, type 1 and 3 innate T cells, but not CD8AA, appear to be developmentalspecific unconventional T cells."

(Suo et al., 2022)

In vitro T-T selection experiments supported the thymocyte-thymocyte origin hypothesis for this population:

> "SP_T cells differentiated in vitro were dominated by ZBTB16-expressing unconventional T cells. Both label transfer and similarity scoring on merged embeddings showed that the in vitro SP_T were most similar to in vivo type 1 innate T cells. Thus, our in vitro experiments support the T-T origin hypothesis of unconventional T cells."

(Suo et al., 2022)

A broader class of innate-like T cells (including MAIT cells, iNKT cells, and gamma-delta T cells) capable of Th1 cytokine production is present in the human fetus:

> "A number of innate-like T cells capable of Th1 cytokine production are present in the human fetus, including gammadelta T cells, invariant natural killer T (iNKT) cells, and mucosa-associated invariant T (MAIT) cells. These cells share certain key features: a semi-invariant TCR repertoire, non-classical MHC restriction, and rapid production of inflammatory cytokines such as IFN-gamma and TNF-alpha"

(Rackaityte and Halkias, 2020)

> "both innate-like B and T cells were abundant during early development and their precise role at this developmental time-point warrants further investigations. Their reported debris-removal, antigen-reactivity, and regulatory functions may confer these prenatal innate-like B and T cells with tissue-homeostatic and important immunological roles."

(Suo et al., 2022)

Unlike mouse, human innate-like T cells do not separate cleanly into discrete effector subtypes:

> "Unlike the mouse, human Tinn cells do not differentiate into multiple effector subsets but develop a mixed type I/type III effector potential."

(Loh et al., 2023)

Direct characterisation of innate-type T cells in fetal skin by CyTOF has been limited by technical constraints:

> "Our ability to comment on innate-type T cells (i.e., MAIT, gd, and NK T cells) in fetal skin was also limited by existing hurdles in combining tetramers with CyTOF and by the total number of fetal skin leukocytes we had to analyze."

(Dhariwala et al., 2020)
