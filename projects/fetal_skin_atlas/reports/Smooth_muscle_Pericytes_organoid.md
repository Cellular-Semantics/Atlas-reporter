# Smooth Muscle Cell/Pericyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [mural cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0008034) (CL:0008034, broad match)

## Summary

Smooth muscle cells and pericytes are mural cell populations identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, mural cells are part of the mesoderm-derived dermal compartment. This combined annotation reflects the shared developmental origin and overlapping marker expression of smooth muscle cells and pericytes in skin vasculature. Like fibroblasts and Schwann cells, mural cells in the SkO showed a low probability of correspondence to adult skin states, indicating immature differentiation. Notably, the SkO lacked immune cells and had markedly reduced endothelial cell heterogeneity and quantity.

## Markers

Mural cells express RGS5, PDGFRB, ACTA2 (smooth muscle actin), and NG2 (CSPG4). Pericytes are additionally characterised by DES (desmin) and CD146.

## Location

In prenatal skin, smooth muscle cells and pericytes are associated with blood vessels and the arrector pili muscle. In the SkO, they are present but with reduced endothelial neighbours, potentially limiting their full vascular maturation.

## Function

Smooth muscle cells provide contractile function for blood vessel tone regulation and hair follicle erection. Pericytes stabilise capillaries and regulate vascular permeability. The reduced endothelial population in the SkO and absence of macrophage-derived growth factors may explain the immature mural cell state, as macrophages were shown to drive vascular network remodelling in SkO co-culture experiments.
