# HOXC5+ Early Fibroblasts in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000057) (CL:0000057, broad match — no exact CL term)

## Summary

HOXC5+ early fibroblasts are a dermal fibroblast progenitor population identified in prenatal human skin by Gopee et al. (2024). Located in the upper dermis and abundant before 11 PCW, these cells serve as the common progenitor for both hair follicle-specialized fibroblasts (pre-dermal condensate cells, the dermal condensate and dermal papilla) and dermal fibroblasts (WNT2+ and PEAR1+ fibroblasts). This key developmental role makes HOXC5+ early fibroblasts central to the establishment of the dermal fibroblast lineage hierarchy in human prenatal skin.

## Markers

The defining marker for this subset is **HOXC5**, a homeobox transcription factor. The full list of differentially expressed genes is provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

Genes associated with a pro-inflammatory fibroblast phenotype (APOE, IGFBP7 and ITM2A) were upregulated during the transition from HOXC5+ fibroblasts into PEAR1+ fibroblasts (Gopee et al., 2024; Solé-Boldo et al., 2020; Ascension et al., 2020).

## Location

### In prenatal skin

HOXC5+ early fibroblasts are located in the upper dermis of prenatal human skin and are abundant before 11 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). Spatial transcriptomics (Visium) data confirmed their upper dermal location.

> "Inferred trajectory analysis predicted that HOXC5 + early fibroblasts (located in the upper dermis (Fig. 2f) and abundant before 11 PCW (Extended Data Fig. 3c)) differentiated along two paths: the first (hair fibroblast trajectory) formed hair-specialized fibroblasts (pre-Dc cells, the Dc and Dp) and the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts (abundant after 11 PCW)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### 1. Common dermal fibroblast progenitor

HOXC5+ early fibroblasts serve as the common progenitor for both the hair fibroblast trajectory and the dermal fibroblast trajectory. Similar to fibroblast differentiation in mouse skin, pre-dermal condensate cells, the dermal condensate, dermal papilla, and dermal fibroblasts in human prenatal skin all originated from this common fibroblast progenitor (Gopee et al., 2024).

> "Additionally, similar to fibroblast differentiation in mouse skin, pre-Dcs, the Dc and Dp and dermal fibroblasts in human prenatal skin also originated from a common fibroblast progenitor (HOXC5 + early fibroblast) (Fig. 2e and Extended Data Fig. 4d)."
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### 2. Hair follicle morphogenesis

The hair fibroblast trajectory originating from HOXC5+ early fibroblasts generates the specialized fibroblasts required for hair follicle development, including pre-dermal condensate cells, the dermal condensate, and the dermal papilla. Similar signalling pathways to those in mouse hair follicle formation, including WNT, BMP, and TGF-beta, were identified along this trajectory (Gopee et al., 2024; Saxena et al., 2019).

### 3. Cross-species conservation

The HOXC5+ progenitor role is conserved between human prenatal skin and the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model. Trajectory alignment analysis showed high numbers of matching transcription factors across pseudotime, indicating similar activated gene regulatory programs during hair follicle fibroblast differentiation (Gopee et al., 2024). However, human pre-dermal condensate cells aligned to more immature fibroblast states in mouse skin, suggesting that HF fibroblasts are in a more differentiated state in mouse compared to human prenatal skin (Gopee et al., 2024).
