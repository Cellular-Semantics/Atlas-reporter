# Schwann Cell/Schwann Precursor (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002573) (CL:0002573, broad match)

## Summary

Schwann cells and Schwann precursors are neural crest-derived glial cell populations identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). This annotation encompasses both immature Schwann cells and their precursors, representing the non-myelinating glial lineage in the skin. In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, Schwann cells are part of the neural compartment and co-locate with macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment. Like myelinating Schwann cells, this population shows slow maturation in the SkO.

## Markers

Schwann cells/precursors express pan-Schwann markers S100B, ERBB3, SOX10, and neural crest markers, but lack the mature myelination markers (MBP, MPZ) found in myelinating Schwann cells.

## Location

In prenatal skin, Schwann cells/precursors are associated with developing peripheral nerve fibres in the dermis. In the SkO, they form part of the neural crest-derived compartment.

## Function

Schwann cells and their precursors provide trophic support to peripheral neurons and participate in nerve development. In prenatal skin, macrophage interactions through VEGFA-NRP signalling support their development. Their immature state in the SkO highlights the importance of immune-neural crosstalk for Schwann cell maturation.
