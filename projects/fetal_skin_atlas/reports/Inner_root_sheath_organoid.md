# Inner Root Sheath Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [inner root sheath cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002560) (CL:0002560, exact match)

## Summary

Inner root sheath (IRS) cells are a component of the developing hair follicle identified in both prenatal human skin and the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). Gopee et al. (2024) identified IRS cells from 14 PCW alongside other HF cell types. In the SkO, IRS cells are part of the IRS differentiation trajectory that derives from POSTN+ basal cells through placode and matrix stages. The IRS forms the inner cylindrical sheath of the hair follicle, moulding and supporting the growing hair shaft.

## Markers

The atlas identifies IRS cells as part of the hair follicle epithelial compartment, emerging along the IRS trajectory that includes placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS cells.

## Location

IRS cells are located within the inner cylinder of the developing hair follicle, surrounding the hair shaft. In the SkO, they occupy the same anatomical position within organoid-derived hair follicles.

## Function

The IRS provides structural support and moulds the developing hair shaft. IRS cells undergo a distinct differentiation program from the matrix cells of the hair follicle bulb, producing trichohyalin-rich keratinised layers. The conserved IRS trajectory between prenatal skin and SkO reinforces the utility of the organoid model for studying hair follicle morphogenesis.
