# SPP1+ Proliferating Neuron Progenitors (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match -- no SPP1-specific neural progenitor subtype exists in CL)

## Summary

SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitors are a fetal-specific neural progenitor subtype annotated in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, distinguished by expression of SPP1 (secreted phosphoprotein 1, also known as osteopontin) and an actively proliferating state. SPP1 is a multifunctional glycoprotein involved in cell adhesion, migration, immune regulation, and extracellular matrix remodeling. Its expression in a proliferating neural progenitor subset suggests a role in expansion and migration of neural precursors during early skin innervation. These cells belong to the broad "Neuronal cells" lineage in the fetal dataset and are part of the ectoderm-derived neural compartment enriched in early gestation (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

- **SPP1 (Osteopontin)**: The defining marker of this subtype. SPP1 is a secreted glycoprotein with roles in cell adhesion, migration, and proliferation regulation.

- **Proliferation markers**: As a proliferating population, these cells are expected to express cell cycle genes (e.g., MKI67, TOP2A).

- **Neural progenitor markers**: SOX10, SOX2, or other neural/neural crest markers as defined in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitors are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW). Neural cells are enriched in early gestation:

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm, which constitutes the first skin permeability barrier 2 , were enriched in early gestation, whereas suprabasal epidermal and HF cells were mainly observed in later gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

These proliferating progenitors likely represent an expanding neural precursor population during early skin innervation. SPP1 expression may facilitate their migration through the dermal extracellular matrix or interaction with immune cells during the establishment of the peripheral nervous system.

> "These findings suggest that prenatal skin macrophages may support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system during early gestation, as previously reported in mouse skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
