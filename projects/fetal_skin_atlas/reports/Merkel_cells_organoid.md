# Merkel Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [Merkel cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000242) (CL:0000242, exact match)

## Summary

Merkel cells are specialised mechanoreceptor cells of the epidermis identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, Merkel cells are part of the ectoderm-derived neural cell compartment of the skin. These cells develop from epidermal progenitors through a program dependent on the transcription factor ATOH1. The SkO model recapitulates the neural cell components of prenatal skin, including Merkel cells, demonstrating that the organoid system supports the differentiation of this specialised mechanosensory cell type.

## Markers

Merkel cells are characterised by expression of ATOH1 (master transcription factor), KRT20 (cytokeratin 20), SOX2, and neuroendocrine markers including CHGA and SYP. They also express components of the SNARE complex involved in neurotransmitter release.

## Location

In skin, Merkel cells are found in the basal epidermis and outer root sheath of hair follicles, particularly concentrated in touch-sensitive areas (touch domes). In the SkO, they derive from epidermal progenitors.

## Function

Merkel cells function as mechanoreceptors for light touch by forming synapse-like contacts with somatosensory afferent nerve endings. They contribute to slowly adapting type I (SAI) tactile responses and exhibit neuroendocrine properties including regulated neurotransmitter release.
