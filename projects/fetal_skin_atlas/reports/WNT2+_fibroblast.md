# WNT2+ Fibroblast in Fetal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Source dataset: Gopee et al. (2024), prenatal skin, 7–17 PCW
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast of dermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002551) (CL:0002551, broad match)

## Summary

The WNT2+ fibroblast cluster in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas represents a WNT2-expressing dermal fibroblast population that emerges from HOXC5+ early fibroblast progenitors along the dermal fibroblast trajectory and becomes abundant after 11 PCW. Together with PEAR1+ fibroblasts, WNT2+ fibroblasts constitute the two major dermal fibroblast subsets of late-gestation fetal skin. These cells express progressively more adult-like, pro-inflammatory gene profiles as gestation advances: late-gestation WNT2+ fibroblasts upregulate collagen deposition genes (COL1A1), whereas early WNT2+ fibroblasts express growth and differentiation genes (SFRP1). LYVE1+ macrophages and TML (TREM2+ microglia-like) macrophages co-locate with WNT2+ fibroblasts, and the predicted macrophage–fibroblast interactions have been proposed to contribute to the scarless healing capacity of early prenatal skin.

## Markers

### Lineage-defining marker: WNT2

WNT2 (Wingless-Type MMTV Integration Site Family Member 2) expression defines this fibroblast subset within fetal dermis. WNT2+ fibroblasts arise from HOXC5+ early fibroblast progenitors along the dermal fibroblast trajectory:

> "the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts (abundant after 11 PCW)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Temporal maturation: early vs late gestation gene expression

WNT2+ fibroblasts display temporally evolving transcriptional profiles within the population across gestation:

> "Late gestation WNT2 + fibroblasts had upregulated genes related to extracellular matrix and collagen deposition (for example, COL1A1), whereas early WNT2 + fibroblasts had DEGs involved in cellular growth and differentiation (for example, SFRP1)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Adult-like inflammatory gene profile in later gestation

WNT2+ and PEAR1+ fibroblasts collectively show increasing acquisition of adult-type gene expression across gestation:

> "The adult fibroblast gene expression profile was increased in WNT2 + and PEAR1 + prenatal fibroblasts, which were abundant in later gestation"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### Fetal dermis, abundant from 11 PCW

WNT2+ fibroblasts are a fine-grained cell type annotated in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024). They reside in the fetal dermis and become abundant after 11 PCW, spatially localised in microenvironments where they co-locate with LYVE1+ macrophages.

## Function

### Extracellular matrix production and dermal scaffold maintenance

WNT2+ fibroblasts contribute to extracellular matrix production in the fetal dermis. Late-gestation cells upregulate collagen deposition genes, contributing to the progressive acquisition of a mature dermal architecture across gestation.

### Macrophage-mediated niche in early prenatal skin

A key finding from Gopee et al. (2024) is the spatial association between LYVE1+ macrophages and WNT2+ fibroblasts in early fetal skin, with predicted PDGF-mediated interactions proposed to support fibroblast maintenance and contribute to the scarless healing capacity of early fetal skin:

> "Specifically, LYVE1 + macrophages co-located with WNT2 + fibroblasts"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

TML macrophages also co-locate with WNT2+ fibroblasts, and their downregulation of IL-6 signalling has been proposed to confer anti-fibrogenic properties:

> "TML macrophages were predicted to co-locate with WNT2 + fibroblasts in early prenatal skin (6-8 PCW)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)
