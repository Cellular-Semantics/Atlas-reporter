# ILC2 (Group 2 Innate Lymphoid Cells) in Prenatal Human Skin
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: prenatal (7-17 PCW)  
CL mapping: [CL:0001069](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0001069) — group 2 innate lymphoid cell (exact match, high confidence)

## Summary

Group 2 innate lymphoid cells (ILC2s), annotated as "ILC2_3" in this atlas, are tissue-resident innate lymphocytes present in human prenatal skin from early gestation. They are defined by expression of the lineage transcription factor GATA3 and additional markers IL1RL1, IL7R, and TNFRSF18 (Supplementary Table 4). ILC2s belong to the broader family of innate lymphoid cells that populate prenatal skin alongside macrophages and other ILC subsets before the emergence of adaptive immune cells (Gopee et al., 2024). ILC2 precursors and mature ILC2s are detectable in fetal tissues including the intestine, lung, and skin during embryonic development, with the majority of adult tissue-resident ILC2s thought to derive from neonatal seeding waves (Matha et al., 2022). In developing skin, ILC2s are poised to contribute to tissue homeostasis, wound repair, and type 2 immune responses.

## Markers

ILC2s in prenatal skin are defined by the following marker set identified through differential expression analysis:

- **GATA3:** The lineage-defining transcription factor for ILC2 identity. GATA3 drives the type 2 cytokine program including IL-5 and IL-13 production, and is essential for ILC2 development, maintenance, and function. GATA3 expression distinguishes ILC2 from other ILC subsets in single-cell analyses (Supplementary Table 4).

- **IL1RL1 (ST2):** The receptor for IL-33, a key epithelial alarmin that activates ILC2s. IL1RL1 expression marks responsiveness to tissue damage signals (Supplementary Table 4).

- **IL7R (CD127):** The interleukin-7 receptor alpha chain, which supports ILC2 development, survival, and tissue maintenance. IL7R is a common ILC lineage marker that helps distinguish ILCs from NK cells (Supplementary Table 4).

- **TNFRSF18 (GITR):** A TNF receptor superfamily member involved in immune modulation and co-stimulation (Supplementary Table 4).

Additional markers identified in the broader literature for skin ILC2s include CRTH2 (PTGDR2), CD25, CD161, ICOS, c-Kit, and CD103:

> "Reynolds et al. showed by single-cell RNA sequencing that ILCs in the epidermis and dermis of AD patients and normal healthy subjects could be classified into four subgroups, namely, ILC1/3, ILC2, ILC1/NK, and NK, with ILC2s (IL7R, PTGDR2, and GATA3) having the most distinct signature (42)."
>
> -- Jia et al. (2023)

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

ILC2s are part of the innate immune compartment present from the earliest stages of prenatal skin development:

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "Some subsets of macrophages, ILCs and fibroblasts exhibited distinct gene expression profiles between early and late gestation, which suggested that functional maturation or dual waves of production occur during development"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Developmental Origins

ILC2s are seeded to peripheral tissues from fetal hematopoietic sources and undergo postnatal expansion:

> "ILC precursors and ILC2s can be detected in the fetal intestine, lung and skin during embryonic development in mice (7,9), while the majority of adult ILC2s seems to be neonatally derived (Figure 1A)."
>
> -- Matha et al. (2022)

> "Tissue-resident ILC2 cells in the lung are seeded during both perinatal and neonatal periods. Lung ILC2 originate from common precursors in the fetal liver, fetal intestine, and bone marrow"
>
> -- Verma et al. (2025)

ILC2 expansion during the neonatal period depends on IL-33 and IL-7 signaling:

> "ILC2 expansion is dependent on IL-33 and IL-7 signaling (7,11,12)."
>
> -- Matha et al. (2022)

### Tissue Compartment in Skin

In postnatal and adult skin, ILC2s are found primarily in the dermis with tissue-specific phenotypic diversity:

> "ILC2s, which highly depend on tissue-derived factors, adapt their cellular phenotypes to the location in which they reside."
>
> -- Kobayashi et al. (2022)

> "The ILC2 subsets in dermis and subcutis display different profiles. ILC2s that express ST2, CD25, Sca-1, and KLRG1, which are expressed on typical ILC2s in lungs, are enriched in the deepest layer of the skin, the subcutis."
>
> -- Kobayashi et al. (2022)

> "Tissue resident and circulating ILC2s are present in the skin."
>
> -- Matha et al. (2022)

## Function

### Tissue Homeostasis and Repair

ILC2s are central effectors of tissue repair in the skin, responding to epithelial damage signals:

> "skin ILC2s play significant positive roles in tissue repair and regeneration. When a wound occurs, IL-33 is secreted by damaged keratinocytes, strongly activating skin ILC2s, which secrete type 2 cytokines like IL-5 and IL-13. While these cytokines exacerbate inflammation, at wound sites, they promote re-epithelialization and collagen deposition, thereby aiding in the physical recovery of the skin. Additionally, the tissue-protective function of ILC2s plays a crucial role in hair follicle homeostasis."
>
> -- Kim et al. (2025)

### Type 2 Immunity

ILC2s are potent innate producers of type 2 cytokines:

> "ILC2s in normal human skin were found to be a homogenous population that expresses type 2 cytokines IL-13 and IL-4 and the transcription factor GATA3"
>
> -- Leyva-Castillo et al. (2021)

> "ILC2s control the adaptive immune response locally promoting the activation of dendritic cells, suppressing Treg functions, interacting with CD4 + T cells, and inducing chemoattraction of T helper type 2 cells"
>
> -- Leyva-Castillo et al. (2021)

### Plasticity

Skin ILC2s demonstrate functional plasticity, particularly in inflammatory contexts:

> "ILC2s present in AD lesional skin were found to be heterogeneous and expressed type 2 as well as type 17 cytokines and the transcription factors that drive these cytokines, including GATA3, AHR, and RORC (Alkon et al., 2021)"
>
> -- Leyva-Castillo et al. (2021)

> "Single-cell RNAseq analysis of human skin in atopic dermatitis further revealed the heterogeneous composition of human skin ILC2s (23). The results indicated that a discrete ILC2 cluster expressing GATA3 and RORA was segregated from the NK, ILC1, and ILC3 clusters. ILC2s co-expressing ILC3 markers RORC and AHR were found in healthy human skin and these co-expressing cells were increased in atopic skin."
>
> -- Kobayashi et al. (2022)

### Prenatal Context

In prenatal skin, ILC2s are part of the early innate immune landscape that precedes adaptive immunity. The atlas demonstrates that ILC subsets show distinct gene expression profiles between early and late gestation, suggesting functional maturation occurs during development (Gopee et al., 2024). ILC2 progenitors in fetal tissues express GATA3 but may also co-express ILC1 and ILC3 transcription factors T-bet and RORgammaT, reflecting the broader multipotency of fetal ILC progenitors (Verma et al., 2025).
