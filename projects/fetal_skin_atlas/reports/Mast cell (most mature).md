## Summary

Mast cell (most mature) is the most differentiated transcriptional state within the mast cell maturation series in fetal human skin (7–17 PCW), identified by the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas. This cluster represents the subset of fetal skin mast cells closest to the adult connective tissue mast cell phenotype, though even these cells are rare compared to adult skin:

> "Mature chymase + mast cells are not present until the end of the second trimester, and even then they are scarce in comparison to adult skin in which approximately 50% of mast cells are chymase + ."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

The progressive maturation of mast cells across gestation establishes a continuous differentiation axis in fetal skin:

> "Mast cells have been detected in fetal skin as early as 8 PCW, 41 and continue to mature and expand throughout the second trimester."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

Even at the most mature stage, fetal mast cell granules retain characteristics of immaturity:

> "Mast cells in first-and second-trimester fetal skin contain immature granules, 39,62 which is reflected in the delayed detection of positive tryptase or toluidine blue staining in the second trimester, 8,39 despite tryptase transcripts being detected from 8 PCW 41 and the presence of a distinct ª 2020 The Authors. Immunology published by John Wiley & Sons Ltd., Immunology, 160, 149-156 population of CD117 + mast cells being detected from 10 to 12 PCW."
> — Botting and Haniffa (2020), *Immunology*

## Markers

The most mature mast cell stage in fetal skin is expected to express:

- **KIT (CD117)** — retained; characteristic of all mast cell stages
- **TPSAB1 / TPSB2 (tryptase)** — protein expression detectable; tryptase-positive
- **CMA1 (chymase)** — beginning to be expressed; very rare in fetal skin even at this stage
- **FcεRI** — high expression
- **CPA3** — carboxypeptidase A3; high expression
- **GATA2** — sustained expression as a master regulator
- **MS4A2** — FcεRIβ chain; marks mature, IgE-responsive mast cells

Specific differentially expressed markers for this most mature cluster are provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

## Location

Mast cell (most mature) cells are located in the dermal immune compartment of fetal skin during prenatal development, predominantly in the later second trimester (around 17 PCW and beyond). They were isolated as CD45+ immune cells by FACS prior to scRNA-seq. These cells are expected to reside near blood vessels and developing hair follicle appendages, consistent with the perivascular and perifollicular localisation of connective tissue mast cells in adult skin.

## Function

At the most mature stage, fetal skin mast cells are approaching functional competence, though they remain less active than their adult counterparts. Key functional features:

- Tryptase secretion can contribute to extracellular matrix remodelling and activation of protease-activated receptors
- Emerging chymase activity supports angiotensin II production and skin fibroblast activation
- FcεRI-mediated IgE signalling becomes possible, though in utero IgE levels are very low
- These cells may contribute to tissue homeostasis and injury responses in late fetal skin development
- Reduced degranulation compared to adult mast cells may support the scarless wound healing characteristic of fetal skin

## Structure / Morphology

The most mature fetal skin mast cells have granule-filled cytoplasm that stains positively for tryptase in late second trimester. Toluidine blue (metachromatic) staining may first become positive at this stage. Chymase expression, when present, marks the transition to a connective tissue mast cell phenotype. Electron microscopy would show electron-dense, scroll-rich or mixed-type secretory granules. The nucleus is round to oval.
