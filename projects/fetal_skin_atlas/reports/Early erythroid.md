# Early Erythroid Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [erythroid progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000038) (CL:0000038, exact match)

## Summary

Early erythroid cells are a population of erythroid-committed progenitors identified in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024) spanning 7-17 PCW. This annotation represents cells that have progressed beyond the MEMP stage into erythroid lineage commitment, corresponding to erythroid progenitors (ErP) in the atlas nomenclature. In the fetal liver haematopoietic hierarchy mapped by Popescu et al. (2019), erythroid progenitors differentiate from the MEMP through upregulation of TAL1 and KLF1, progressing through burst-forming unit-erythroid (BFU-E) and colony-forming unit-erythroid (CFU-E) stages before entering terminal erythropoiesis. Unlike the "Early erythroid (embryonic)" population in the same atlas, these cells are not annotated with the embryonic qualifier, suggesting they represent definitive-wave erythroid progenitors.

## Markers

Key transcription factors marking erythroid progenitor commitment include:

- **TAL1** (SCL): Essential for erythroid specification from the MEMP (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **KLF1** (EKLF): Drives erythroid lineage commitment and activates beta-globin gene expression (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **GATA1**: A critical transcription factor for erythroid differentiation, expressed in erythroid progenitors and their progeny.

> "Genes dynamically modulated in the specification of erythroid, megakaryocyte and mast cell lineages are distinct: TAL1 and KLF1 in erythroid; F11R, PBX1 and MEIS1 in megakaryocyte; and CPA3 and MS4A2 in mast cells."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

## Location

### Prenatal skin

Early erythroid cells are annotated in the prenatal skin atlas within the haematopoietic progenitor compartment (Gopee et al., 2024). The atlas abbreviation ErP (erythroid progenitors) is used in figure legends describing haematopoietic cell diversity in fetal skin.

> "we detected B cell progenitors in almost all prenatal organs, megakaryocyte/erythroid progenitors in developing spleen and skin, and myeloid progenitors in the thymus, spleen, skin, and kidney"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

### Fetal liver

The principal site of erythroid progenitor expansion and differentiation during this developmental window is the fetal liver (Popescu et al., 2019). Early stage fetal liver samples show erythroid lineage bias, with erythroid cell representation decreasing relative to lymphoid and myeloid lineages at later gestational ages.

## Function

### Erythropoiesis

Early erythroid cells are committed progenitors that give rise to erythroblasts and ultimately to mature red blood cells. In the fetal context, definitive erythropoiesis produces erythrocytes bearing primarily fetal hemoglobin (HbF, alpha2-gamma2), which has a higher oxygen affinity than adult hemoglobin, facilitating oxygen transfer across the placenta.

### Contribution to fetal blood supply

The presence of erythroid progenitors in prenatal skin, alongside the broader haematopoietic progenitor compartment, supports the concept that extramedullary haematopoiesis occurs in multiple developing organs during the 7-17 PCW window (Suo et al., 2022).

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological descriptions of early erythroid cells in prenatal skin were found in the traversed literature. In general, early erythroid progenitors (BFU-E and CFU-E stages) are small, blast-like cells that are not yet morphologically distinguishable as erythroblasts.
