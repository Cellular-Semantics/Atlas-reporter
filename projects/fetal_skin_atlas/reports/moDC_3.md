# Monocyte-Derived Dendritic Cell Subtype 3 (moDC_3) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [monocyte-derived dendritic cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011031) (CL:0011031, broad match)

## Summary

The moDC_3 cluster represents the terminally mature state of monocyte-derived dendritic cells in adult human skin, as defined by Reynolds et al. (2021). This state sits at the convergence point of three mononuclear phagocyte differentiation branches (Langerhans cells, myeloid DCs, and mo-DCs) in PAGA analysis, sharing a common migratory DC gene signature with LC4 and Mig. DC. moDC_3 expresses a comprehensive set of DC maturation, immunoregulatory, and migration genes, representing a cell that has acquired full capacity for lymph node homing and T cell priming while losing its original monocyte-derived subset identity. The adult skin annotations including moDC subtypes were integrated into the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas for cross-developmental comparisons.

## Markers

### DC maturation genes: CD83, CCR7, LAMP3, CD40, CD86

Reynolds et al. (2021) identified moDC_3 at the convergence of multiple DC differentiation branches:

> "The clusters at the convergence of these branches (moDC3, LC4 and migratory DC (Mig. DC)) express transcripts associated with DC maturation (CD83, CCR7, LAMP3, CD40, CD86), immunoregulation (CD274, IDO1, CD200, PDCD1LG2, SOCS1) and migration (FSCN1, PLGRKT, TRAF1, BCL2A1, CFLAR and REL)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

These maturation markers indicate full dendritic cell activation: CD83 marks mature DCs, CCR7 enables lymph node homing, LAMP3 is associated with antigen processing, and CD40/CD86 provide T cell co-stimulation.

### Immunoregulatory genes: CD274, IDO1, CD200, PDCD1LG2, SOCS1

The expression of immunoregulatory molecules including PD-L1 (CD274), IDO1 (indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase), and PD-L2 (PDCD1LG2) indicates that mature moDC_3 cells may also function in immune tolerance and regulation, not solely in immune activation. This dual capacity is a hallmark of the migratory DC program.

### Migration genes: FSCN1, PLGRKT, TRAF1, BCL2A1, CFLAR, REL

FSCN1 (fascin) is required for dendritic cell dendrite formation and migration. The expression of anti-apoptotic genes (BCL2A1, CFLAR) and NF-kB components (REL, TRAF1) supports survival during the migratory phase from skin to draining lymph nodes.

### Loss of subset identity

A key feature of moDC_3 is the loss of monocyte-derived identity genes:

> "Acquisition of this common gene signature is associated with loss of genes conferring subset identity in mice, which we also observe here for moDC3, LC4 and Mig. DC"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

This convergent loss of origin-specific markers makes moDC_3 transcriptionally similar to mature migratory DCs derived from other lineages (LC4, Mig. DC).

## Location

### Skin and transit to draining lymph nodes

moDC_3 cells are present in adult human skin but, given their expression of CCR7 and migration genes, are functionally poised to leave the tissue via afferent lymphatics and migrate to skin-draining lymph nodes. Their location represents a transitional state between tissue residence and lymph node arrival.

## Function

### Migratory DC function and T cell priming

moDC_3 represents the functional endpoint of moDC maturation: a cell capable of efficient antigen transport to lymph nodes and T cell priming. The shared migratory DC signature with LC4 and Mig. DC indicates a convergent functional program regardless of developmental origin.

### Cross-species conservation

Reynolds et al. (2021) demonstrated that this migratory DC signature is conserved:

> "as in migratory murine dermal DC2 and splenic DC1"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

This conservation underscores the fundamental importance of the moDC_3/migratory DC program in cutaneous immunity across species.

### Immunoregulation and tolerance

The co-expression of maturation/co-stimulatory molecules alongside immunoregulatory genes (IDO1, CD274, PDCD1LG2, SOCS1) suggests that moDC_3 cells can mediate both immune activation and peripheral tolerance, depending on the context of antigen encounter. In homeostatic conditions, the migratory DC program contributes to tolerance to innocuous environmental antigens (Reynolds et al., 2021).

### Augmentation in disease

Reynolds et al. (2021) noted that the migratory DC signature present in moDC_3 is augmented in multiple disease states including tonsillitis, ascites, lung cancer, and rheumatoid arthritis, suggesting that this maturation program is amplified during pathological immune activation.
