# Neuron Progenitor (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match)

## Summary

Neuron progenitors are neural-lineage precursor cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, neural cells are enriched in early gestation and form part of the skin peripheral nervous system. Neuron progenitors represent cells committed to the neuronal lineage within the skin neural compartment, including sensory and autonomic neuron precursors.

## Markers

Neuron progenitors express neural lineage markers and early neuronal commitment genes. They are distinguished from neuroepithelial cells by their more restricted neuronal fate commitment.

## Atlas Context

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm... were enriched in early gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In the SkO, neuron progenitors are part of the neural compartment, representing intermediate precursors between multipotent neuroepithelial cells and mature neurons.

## Function

Neuron progenitors differentiate into the peripheral neurons of the skin, contributing to cutaneous innervation including sensory nerve fibres and autonomic innervation of hair follicles and blood vessels.
