# LMCD1+ Mural Cell (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [pericyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000669) (CL:0000669, broad match); new_term_needed

## Summary

LMCD1+ mural cells are a fine-grained mural cell subtype identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas (7–17 PCW), defined by expression of LMCD1 (LIM and cysteine-rich domains 1), a transcriptional cofactor with roles in cardiac and vascular muscle development. Mural cells in this atlas encompass pericytes and vascular smooth muscle cell-like populations that coat the abluminal surface of blood vessels, regulating vascular tone and stability. The LMCD1+ subtype likely represents a pericyte-like population with a distinct transcriptional state reflecting early vascular morphogenesis in fetal skin. Mural cells as a class are present throughout gestation and, like fibroblasts and Schwann cells, show slow maturation relative to adult skin cell states even in long-term organoid culture.

## Markers

- **LMCD1 (LIM and cysteine-rich domains 1)**: The defining marker of this mural cell subtype. LMCD1 is a transcriptional cofactor that modulates transcription factor activity (including GATA factors) and has established roles in cardiac hypertrophy, vascular smooth muscle, and mechanosensing.

- **PDGFRB**: Pan-pericyte marker; pericytes characteristically express platelet-derived growth factor receptor beta, which mediates PDGF-BB signalling from endothelial cells.

- **RGS5**: A canonical pericyte/vascular smooth muscle marker, part of the CL:0000669 definition.

- **NG2 (CSPG4)**: Pericyte-associated proteoglycan expressed by many pericyte subtypes; part of the CL:0000669 definition.

- **CD146 (MCAM)**: Expressed by pericytes and vascular mural cells; part of the CL:0000669 definition.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

LMCD1+ mural cells are identified as a fine-grained annotation in the prenatal skin dataset (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024) within the broad mural cell category. Mural cells associate with the vasculature of the developing dermis and are present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Mural cells co-locate with endothelial cells in the neurovascular microenvironments (ME1, ME5) predicted by Visium deconvolution analysis.

## Function

### Vascular Support and Immature Differentiation

As a mural cell subtype, LMCD1+ cells likely regulate capillary blood flow and vascular stability through physical association with endothelial cells and PDGF–PDGFRβ signalling. Fetal mural cells including this subtype show slow correspondence to adult mural cell states:

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## New Term Request

No existing CL term captures a mural cell subtype defined by LMCD1 expression. The closest existing term is CL:0000669 pericyte (broad match). A new CL term is needed to represent this transcriptionally defined prenatal pericyte-like mural cell subtype marked by LMCD1 expression.
