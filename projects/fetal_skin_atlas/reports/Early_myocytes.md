# Early Myocytes (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [skeletal muscle myoblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000515) (CL:0000515, broad match -- represents an early differentiating muscle cell)

## Summary

Early myocytes are a fetal-specific cell type annotated in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas within the broad "Skeletal muscle" lineage. These cells represent an early stage of skeletal muscle differentiation, likely corresponding to newly differentiated myocytes that have exited the cell cycle and begun expressing muscle-specific contractile proteins but have not yet fully matured. In fetal skin, skeletal muscle cells are associated with the arrector pili muscles that attach to hair follicles, as well as the panniculus carnosus (subcutaneous striated muscle layer present in early human development). The atlas identifies early myocytes in both the fetal skin and skin organoid (SkO) datasets, indicating that the SkO model recapitulates some aspects of muscle differentiation. These cells are part of the mesoderm-derived compartment present throughout gestation (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

Early myocytes are expected to express early muscle differentiation markers including MYOG (myogenin), MYH3 (embryonic myosin heavy chain), ACTA1 (skeletal muscle alpha-actin), and DES (desmin), while maintaining lower levels of mature myofiber markers. Specific markers distinguishing this cluster are defined in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

Early myocytes are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW) within the dermal/subcutaneous compartment. Mesoderm-derived cells including muscle are present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

In skin, skeletal muscle cells form the arrector pili muscles and potentially the panniculus carnosus.

## Function

Early myocytes represent the initial stage of skeletal muscle fiber formation in prenatal skin. They are post-mitotic cells that have begun terminal differentiation and will fuse to form multinucleated myofibers. In skin, their primary function is to form the arrector pili muscles responsible for piloerection.
