# Type 1 Conventional Dendritic Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [conventional dendritic cell type 1](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002399) (CL:0002399, exact match)

## Summary

DC1 cells in adult human skin correspond to type 1 conventional dendritic cells (cDC1), defined by expression of CLEC9A (DNGR-1), XCR1, and CD141 (BDCA-3). Reynolds et al. (2021) identify DC1 and DC2 as a distinct myeloid DC branch within the mononuclear phagocyte (MP) landscape, confirmed by gene signature enrichment for murine splenic Xcr1+ DCs. DC1 cells are present in embryonic skin from 7 post-conception weeks (PCW), and in adult skin they are specialised for cross-presentation of exogenous antigens on MHC class I to CD8+ cytotoxic T cells, representing a critical link between innate and adaptive immunity. Note: this report describes the adult DC1 population; the fetal DC1 population is documented separately (DC1_fetal.md).

## Markers

Defining markers of DC1 (cDC1):

> "CLEC9A or DNGR1 is a C-type lectin receptor that mediates endocytosis, but not phagocytosis, with low pH endosomes promoting the drift toward cross-presentation. Importantly, CLEC9A binding of antigens induces antigen presentation on both MHC I (cross-presentation) and MHC II. It is highly and specifically expressed on CD11c+ CD141+ XCR1+ cDCs"
>
> — Gornati, Zanoni and Granucci (2018)

> "BDCA-3+ DCs exhibit strong similarities with mouse Cd8alpha DCs and express CLEC9A, XCR1 and high levels of TLR3"
>
> — Klarquist and Janssen (2012)

Reynolds et al. (2021) confirm DC1 identity by gene signature enrichment:

> "enrichment of gene signatures for murine splenic Xcr1+DC (DC1)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers: **CLEC9A** (DNGR-1, defining C-type lectin), **XCR1** (chemokine receptor, defining), **CD141** (BDCA-3, defining surface marker), **TLR3** (innate immune receptor).

## Location

DC1 cells are found in the dermis of adult human skin and are present from very early in fetal development:

> "We observe Dendritic Cells 1 and 2 (DC1, DC2) and Langerhans cells (LCs) in embryonic skin as early as 7 PCW, prior to bone marrow hematopoiesis, but macrophages are the dominant MP in first trimester skin"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

In adult skin, DC1 and DC2 form the myeloid DC branch within a three-branch differentiation topology (alongside Langerhans cell and monocyte-derived DC branches):

> "Partition-based approximate graph abstraction (PAGA) analysis revealed three branches of differentiation: LCs, myeloid DCs (DC1 and DC2) and monocyte-derived DCs (mo-DCs)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

## Function

### Antigen cross-presentation

DC1 cells are specialised for cross-presentation — the presentation of exogenous peptide antigens on MHC class I molecules to CD8+ cytotoxic T cells. The C-type lectin CLEC9A (DNGR-1) binds dead cell material and routes antigens into low-pH endosomes that favour cross-presentation. CLEC9A binding induces antigen presentation on both MHC class I and MHC class II (Gornati et al., 2018), enabling DC1 cells to activate both CD8+ and CD4+ T cells.

### Innate immune sensing

DC1 cells express high levels of TLR3, enabling detection of double-stranded RNA (dsRNA) from virally infected cells. This positions DC1 as a key sensor of viral infection in skin, capable of bridging innate virus detection with adaptive CD8+ T cell priming.

### Role in skin disease

Reynolds et al. (2021) show that myeloid DC gene programmes are co-opted in inflammatory skin disease. DC1 cells are present in the adult skin mononuclear phagocyte landscape alongside DC2 cells, and their relative abundance and activation state may change in conditions such as atopic dermatitis and psoriasis.
