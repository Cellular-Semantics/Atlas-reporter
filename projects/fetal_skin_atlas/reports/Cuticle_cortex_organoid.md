# Cuticle/Cortex Cells in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid
Cell Ontology: [keratinized cell of hair follicle](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0010000) (CL:0010000, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)

## Summary

Cuticle/cortex cells are a specialised hair follicle cell population identified in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model (Gopee et al., 2024). These cells form the inner layers of the hair follicle, comprising the hair cuticle (the outermost protective layer of the hair shaft) and the cortex (the main structural component of the hair shaft). In the integrated prenatal skin and SkO trajectory analysis, cuticle/cortex cells arise along the IRS differentiation trajectory from POSTN+ basal cells, together with placode, matrix, and inner root sheath cells (Gopee et al., 2024). The SkO model faithfully recapitulates the production of these hair shaft components, consistent with its ability to generate pigmented hair follicles.

## Markers

- **KRT35**: Hair cortex type I keratin, a marker of cortical differentiation.
- **KRT85**: Hair cortex type II keratin, expressed during cortical maturation.
- **SHH**: Sonic hedgehog, upregulated along the IRS trajectory and a known matrix/cuticle-cortex precursor marker (Extended Data Fig. 4b,c; Supplementary Table 7).
- **WNT10B**: Upregulated along the IRS trajectory (Extended Data Fig. 4b,c; Supplementary Table 7).

> "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### In the SkO model

Cuticle/cortex cells are located in the inner layers of the hair follicle within the SkO, forming the hair shaft structure. They are produced by the differentiation of matrix cells at the base of the hair follicle bulb.

### In the hair follicle structure

The cuticle forms the outermost layer of the hair shaft, consisting of flat, overlapping scale-like cells that interlock with the inner root sheath cuticle. The cortex lies beneath the cuticle and constitutes the bulk of the hair shaft, composed of terminally differentiated cells packed with keratin filaments.

## Function

### Hair shaft formation

Cuticle/cortex cells represent the terminally differentiated products of the IRS trajectory. They undergo extensive keratin synthesis and cross-linking to form the rigid hair shaft structure. The cortex provides tensile strength to the hair, while the cuticle provides a protective outer barrier.

### IRS trajectory differentiation

In the integrated prenatal skin and SkO analysis, cuticle/cortex cells are the terminal state of the IRS trajectory, which progresses from POSTN+ basal cells through placode and matrix stages.

> "IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "AGR2 was downregulated along the IRS trajectory, whereas known matrix markers such as SHH and WNT10B were upregulated"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Hair type in SkO

The SkO produces lanugo-like hairs rather than terminal hairs, as indicated by the absence of medullary markers in organoid hair follicles (Lee et al., 2020). The cuticle/cortex cells therefore correspond to this prenatal-type hair production.
