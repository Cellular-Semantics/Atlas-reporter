# Pre pro B cell
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal

## Summary
Pre-pro-B cells are the most primitive B-lineage committed progenitors identified in the fetal skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024). They represent the earliest stage of B cell development, corresponding to Hardy fraction A. In the related fetal liver haematopoiesis study, pre-pro-B cells are characterized by expression of CD34, SPINK2 and IGLL1, distinguishing them from more mature pro-B and pre-B stages that progressively acquire B cell transcripts such as MS4A1, CD79B, DNTT and HLA-DRA (Popescu et al., 2019). The presence of pre-pro-B cells in fetal skin suggests that the most primitive B cell progenitors can seed non-lymphoid tissues during early development.

## Markers

Pre-pro-B cells in fetal skin are defined by the following markers:

- **CD34**: A stem/progenitor cell marker expressed on pre-pro-B cells, indicating their primitive developmental state (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **SPINK2**: A serine protease inhibitor expressed in the earliest B cell progenitors (Popescu et al., 2019).
- **IGLL1**: Immunoglobulin lambda-like polypeptide 1, a component of the surrogate light chain complex, expressed at the pre-pro-B stage (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "primitive 'pre pro-B' clusters expressing CD34, SPINK2, and IGLL1 to 'pro-B' and 'pre-B' clusters with increasing expression of B cell transcripts MS4A1, CD79B, DNTT and HLA-DRA and reducing expression of JCHAIN and LTB"
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

## Location

### Fetal Skin

- Pre-pro-B cells are identified in the fetal scope of the prenatal skin atlas, appearing from around 10 PCW as B cell development commences (Supplementary Tables 3-4, Gopee et al., 2024).
- In fetal liver, pre-pro-B cells are detected as the starting point of the B cell developmental trajectory (Popescu et al., 2019).

### Non-Lymphoid Tissues

- Interestingly, in non-lymphoid tissues (NLT) including skin and kidney, pro-B, pre-B and B cells are present but HSC/MPPs and pre-pro-B cells are absent, suggesting that pre-pro-B cells may have a transient presence or that more mature B cell stages are preferentially seeded (Popescu et al., 2019).

> "Comparing liver with yolk sac and NLT, pro-B, pre-B and B cells are present in NLT but HSC/MPPs and pre pro-B cells are absent."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

Note: The Gopee et al. (2024) atlas does annotate a 'Pre pro B cell' population in fetal skin, which may reflect improved detection sensitivity or later gestational stages than the Popescu et al. (2019) study.

## Function

### B Cell Development

Pre-pro-B cells represent the initiation point of B lymphopoiesis. At this stage, D-J rearrangement at the immunoglobulin heavy chain locus begins (Li and Xia, 2024). The transition from pre-pro-B to pro-B cell stage requires IL-7 receptor signalling in adult development, through upregulation of the transcription factor EBF (Kikuchi et al., 2008). However, fetal B cell development may be IL-7 independent (Kikuchi et al., 2008).

> "IL-7 is necessary for EBF expression in pre-pro-B cells, as well as for transition to more mature stages during adult B cell development."
>
> -- Kikuchi et al. (2008)

> "fetal B cell development is IL-7 independent"
>
> -- Kikuchi et al. (2008)

## Structure / Morphology

- Pre-pro-B cells are small lymphoid cells with a high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio, similar to other early B cell progenitors (Popescu et al., 2019).
