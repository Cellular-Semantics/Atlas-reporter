# CD8 T Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal

Cell Ontology: [CD8-positive, alpha-beta T cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000625) (CL:0000625, broad match — no exact CL term)

## Summary

CD8+ T cells are an adaptive immune cell population identified in prenatal human skin from around 10 post-conception weeks (PCW), emerging later in gestation than innate immune cells such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (Gopee et al., 2024). In fetal skin, CD8+ T cells are substantially less abundant than in adult skin, comprising only 4-7% of the T cell density seen in adult tissue (Botting and Haniffa, 2020). Unlike their adult counterparts -- annotated as "Tc" (cytotoxic T cells) and defined by high expression of GZMA, GZMK, and PRF1 -- fetal skin CD8+ T cells are predominantly naive and proliferative, with high expression of CD45RA, CD27, Ki-67, and CD31, the latter indicating recent thymic emigration (Dhariwala et al., 2020). A minor but notable subset of CD45RO+ fetal CD8+ T cells demonstrates memory-like features and enriched IFN-gamma production capacity, suggesting early antigen-dependent activation in utero (Dhariwala et al., 2020; Feyaerts et al., 2022). Critically, fetal CD8+ T cells have markedly reduced cytotoxic effector function compared to adult cells, consistent with the tolerogenic bias of the fetal immune system (Schreurs et al., 2021).

## Markers

CD8+ T cells in prenatal skin are defined by the following markers in the atlas and supporting literature:

- **CD8A and CD8B**: Core lineage markers for the CD8+ T cell population, encoding the alpha and beta chains of the CD8 co-receptor that mediates MHC class I recognition (Supplementary Table S2; Gopee et al., 2024).
- **CD3E**: Pan-T cell marker confirming T cell lineage identity (Supplementary Table S2).
- **CD45RA (PTPRC)**: Highly expressed on the majority of fetal skin CD8+ T cells, indicating a naive phenotype. CD45RA is a marker of antigen-inexperienced lymphocytes (Dhariwala et al., 2020).
- **CD27**: Elevated expression in fetal skin CD8+ T cells, further supporting a naive/recently activated state (Dhariwala et al., 2020).
- **Ki-67 (MKI67)**: Elevated in fetal skin CD8+ T cells, indicating active proliferation. This is a distinguishing feature compared to the largely quiescent CD8+ T cells in adult skin (Dhariwala et al., 2020).
- **CD31 (PECAM1)**: Elevated on fetal skin T cells, particularly CD45RO-negative conventional T cells, marking enrichment for recent thymic emigrants (Dhariwala et al., 2020).
- **CD45RO (PTPRC isoform)**: Expressed on a minority (up to ~30%) of fetal skin CD8+ T cells, identifying a memory-like subset with heightened TCR signaling (high Nur77) and enriched IFN-gamma production (Dhariwala et al., 2020).

Notably, fetal skin CD8+ T cells express substantially lower levels of activation and effector markers compared to adult skin counterparts:

- **CD25 (IL2RA)**: Low expression in fetal skin CD8+ T cells (Dhariwala et al., 2020).
- **Granzyme B (GZMB), Perforin (PRF1)**: Markedly reduced in fetal CD8+ T cells; the adult Tc cluster expresses high GZMA, GZMK, and PRF1 (Supplementary Table S3; Schreurs et al., 2021).
- **PD-1 (PDCD1)**: Elevated on fetal/infant effector CD8+ T cells, contributing to reduced cytotoxic capacity (Schreurs et al., 2021).

## Location

### In prenatal skin

CD8+ T cells are present in prenatal human skin from around 10 PCW onward, after the onset of thymic T cell output (Gopee et al., 2024; Botting and Haniffa, 2020). They reside exclusively in the dermis; epidermal T cells have not been identified in fetal skin (Botting and Haniffa, 2020; Schuster et al., 2012).

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "Fetal skin contains both CD4+ and CD8+ T-cells, of which naive CD4+ T-cells are the most abundant and increase in frequency across development."
>
> -- Botting and Haniffa (2020)

Spatial transcriptomics (Cell2location and NMF analysis) in the atlas revealed that pre-dermal condensate cells co-locate with dendritic and lymphoid cells, suggesting that lymphocytes including CD8+ T cells may participate in microanatomical niches associated with early hair follicle development (Gopee et al., 2024).

### Across prenatal organs

In the cross-organ developing human immune system atlas, conventional T cells including CD8+ T cells were identified across multiple prenatal tissues (Suo et al., 2022). T cell progenitors were restricted to the thymus, reflecting stringent niche requirements for T cell development. Mature single-positive T cells migrate from the thymus to peripheral tissues including skin, with conserved processes of proliferation and maturation accompanying this egression.

> "prenatal conventional T cells (CD4+T, CD8+T, and Tregs), whereas resident and effector memory T cells did not have a developmental equivalent"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

### Temporal dynamics

CD8+ T cells appear in fetal skin from around 10 PCW, consistent with the onset of thymic output. The proportion of CD45RO+ (memory-like) CD8+ T cells remains relatively stable across the second trimester, in contrast to Tregs which progressively accumulate (Dhariwala et al., 2020). Adaptive fetal immune memory in peripheral organs is observed after gestational week 14 (Feyaerts et al., 2022).

## Function

### Predominantly naive and tolerogenic phenotype

The majority of fetal skin CD8+ T cells display a naive, proliferative phenotype with limited effector function, reflecting the tolerogenic bias of the prenatal immune environment (Dhariwala et al., 2020; Feyaerts et al., 2022).

> "Conventional Foxp3neg CD4+ and CD8+ T cells in fetal skin are largely distinguished from their adult counterparts by high expression of Ki-67 and CD27 and low expression of CD25 and CD45RO, indicative of a naive phenotype."
>
> -- Dhariwala et al. (2020)

> "Fetal CD8+ T cell immunity is functional in its antigen recognition, even if less potent (i.e., decreased cytotoxicity) compared to adult cells."
>
> -- Feyaerts et al. (2022)

### Memory-like subset with IFN-gamma capacity

A subset of CD45RO+ CD8+ T cells in fetal skin demonstrates enriched capacity for IFN-gamma production and higher Nur77 levels consistent with antigen-driven TCR signaling (Dhariwala et al., 2020).

> "Strikingly, up to 30% of CD4+ Tconv and slightly fewer CD8+ T cells in fetal skin were CD45RO+."
>
> -- Dhariwala et al. (2020)

> "Higher levels of Nur77, a specific reporter of T cell receptor (TCR) signaling, were detected in fetal CD45RO+ versus CD45RA+ cells, further suggesting antigen stimulation in this putative memory population."
>
> -- Dhariwala et al. (2020)

However, even these memory-like fetal CD8+ T cells have reduced cytokine breadth compared to adult skin counterparts, producing substantially less TNF-alpha (Dhariwala et al., 2020).

### Reduced cytotoxic effector capacity

Fetal CD8+ T cells across tissues show markedly impaired cytotoxic function compared to adults. In fetal intestine (an analogous tissue), CD8+ T cells have reduced granzyme B production and high PD-1 expression (Schreurs et al., 2021). This functional impairment contributes to tolerance during early antigen exposure.

> "Functional cytotoxic capacity, including cytokine and granzyme B production of infant intestinal effector CD8+ T cells was, however, markedly reduced compared to adult intestinal CD8+ T cells. This was in line with the high expression of the inhibitory molecule PD-1 by infant intestinal effector CD8+ T cells."
>
> -- Schreurs et al. (2021)

### Recent thymic emigrants and tissue seeding

Fetal skin CD8+ T cells are enriched for recent thymic emigrants, as indicated by high CD31 expression (Dhariwala et al., 2020). The seeding of naive T cells into fetal skin appears to occur independently of CCL27, which is only expressed by differentiated keratinocytes toward the end of the second trimester (Botting and Haniffa, 2020).

### Unique T cell subsets in fetal skin

Reitermaier et al. (2021) identified a unique population of dual TCR alpha-beta-gamma-delta+ (DP) T cells in fetal skin that co-express CD4 and CD8 in early second trimester, trending toward single-positive populations later in development. These DP T cells express naive/thymic emigrant markers and participate in cell-cell interactions suggesting roles in skin embryogenesis and immune homeostasis.

## Comparison with adult counterpart (Tc)

The adult skin counterpart of the fetal CD8 T cell is the "Tc" (cytotoxic T cell) cluster, defined by high CD8A, CD8B, GZMA, GZMK, and PRF1 expression (Supplementary Tables S2, S3; Gopee et al., 2024). Key differences between fetal and adult CD8+ T cells in skin include:

| Feature | Fetal CD8 T cell | Adult Tc |
|---|---|---|
| Predominant phenotype | Naive (CD45RA+, CD27-high) | Memory/effector (CD45RO+) |
| Proliferation (Ki-67) | High | Low |
| Cytotoxicity (GZMA/B, PRF1) | Low/absent | High |
| IFN-gamma production | Present in CD45RO+ subset | Characteristic |
| TNF-alpha production | Markedly reduced | High |
| CD31 (thymic emigrant) | High | Low |
| Tissue location | Dermis only | Dermis and stroma |
