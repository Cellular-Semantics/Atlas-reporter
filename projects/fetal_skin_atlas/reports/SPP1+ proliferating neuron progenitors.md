# SPP1+ Proliferating Neuron Progenitors (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match)

## Summary

SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitors are a fine-grained neural cell subtype identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas (7–17 PCW), defined by expression of SPP1 (secreted phosphoprotein 1, also known as osteopontin) in a proliferating neural progenitor population. Neural cells as a class are enriched in early gestation in fetal skin, consistent with the early establishment of the cutaneous peripheral nervous system. The SPP1+ proliferating subtype likely represents a pool of actively dividing progenitors that give rise to neurons and/or glia of the skin's peripheral nervous system. SPP1/osteopontin is a multifunctional matricellular protein with known roles in neural progenitor proliferation and survival. The broader neural cell compartment in prenatal skin co-locates with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1), which support axon guidance and synaptogenesis.

## Markers

- **SPP1 (osteopontin, secreted phosphoprotein 1)**: The defining marker of this neural progenitor subtype. SPP1 is a secreted phosphoprotein with roles in cell adhesion, migration, and survival signalling via integrin and CD44 receptors. Its expression in proliferating neural progenitors in fetal skin may reflect autocrine or paracrine survival and proliferation signals.

- **Ki67 (MKI67)**: Expected proliferation marker given the "proliferating" designation of this cell state; marks cells in G1, S, G2, and M phases.

- **SOX2**: Transcription factor expressed by neural progenitor cells; maintains progenitor identity.

- **NES (Nestin)**: Intermediate filament protein expressed in neural progenitors and stem cells.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitors are identified in fetal skin (7–17 PCW) as a fine-grained annotation in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas. Neural cells are enriched in early gestation:

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm, which constitutes the first skin permeability barrier 2 , were enriched in early gestation, whereas suprabasal epidermal and HF cells were mainly observed in later gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4). Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Neural cells co-locate with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment, suggesting a microanatomical niche for neural progenitor activity in fetal skin:

> "TML macrophages were also predicted to co-locate with Schwann cells in prenatal skin ('early neurovascular microenvironment', ME1) (Figs. 1d and 3g) and expressed genes related to cell migration and"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "neural development (Fig. 3f, Extended Data Fig. 8c and Supplementary Tables 22 and 23), which mirrored the functions of brain microglia and peripheral nerve-associated macrophages in mouse skin 65 . TML macrophages were predicted to interact with Schwann cells, contributing to synapse formation and axon guidance (VEGFA-NRP1, VEGFA-NRP2, SEMA3C-NRP2 and SEMA3E-PLXND1) 66 (Extended Data Fig. 8h and Supplementary Table 8). These findings suggest that prenatal skin macrophages may support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system during early gestation, as previously reported in mouse skin 65 ."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Neural Progenitor Proliferation

As a proliferating progenitor population, SPP1+ cells are actively expanding the neural cell pool in fetal skin during early gestation. SPP1/osteopontin may function as an autocrine/paracrine survival and proliferation factor, and the macrophage-rich early neurovascular microenvironment provides trophic support for neural differentiation.

### Organoid Comparison

Neural cells including neuron progenitors were present in the SkO model, though the immune cell deficit in SkO likely affected their full maturation:

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5). By contrast, accelerated differentiation was observed in keratinocytes and melanocytes, with alignment to adult cell states seen as early as 4 weeks of SkO culture (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
