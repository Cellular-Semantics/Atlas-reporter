# Myoblast (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [myoblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000056) (CL:0000056, exact match)

## Summary

Myoblasts are muscle precursor cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the context of skin development, myoblasts represent precursors of the arrector pili muscle or other smooth muscle populations associated with hair follicles and dermal vasculature. The SkO recapitulates several dermal cell lineages including mural cells. Myoblasts are committed to differentiating into myocytes and represent a transient proliferative state in the muscle lineage.

## Markers

Myoblasts are characterised by expression of myogenic regulatory factors and muscle precursor markers such as PAX7, MYF5, and MYOD1.

## Atlas Context

> "the SkO model recapitulated the different components of prenatal skin HF, interfollicular epidermis, neural cells and dermal fibroblasts"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In prenatal skin and the SkO, myoblasts are located in the dermal compartment, where they are associated with developing smooth muscle structures including the arrector pili muscle of hair follicles.

## Function

Myoblasts are committed muscle precursor cells that proliferate and differentiate into myocytes. In skin, they contribute to the formation of the arrector pili muscle that controls hair erection and to vascular smooth muscle.
