# Inflammatory Pericyte in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [pericyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000669) (CL:0000669, broad match)

## Summary

Inflammatory pericytes (Pericyte_2_inflamm) are one of two pericyte subtypes identified by Reynolds et al. (2021) in adult human skin. They represent an activated pericyte state characterised by high expression of TAGLN (transgelin) and ACTA2, with upregulated expression of inflammatory cytokines, chemokines, and adhesion molecules compared to non-inflammatory pericytes (Pericyte_1). Pericytes are specialised mesenchymal cells embedded in the basement membrane of capillaries and are identified by co-expression of ACTA2, RGS5, and PDGFRB. The inflammatory TAGLN+ pericyte subtype (Pericyte_2) is implicated in regulating vascular permeability, leukocyte trafficking, and inflammatory signalling in the skin.

>
> — Ganier et al. (2024)

Key markers distinguishing inflammatory pericytes: **TAGLN** (transgelin, defining for Pericyte_2), **ACTA2** (high expression), **PDGFRB** (pan-pericyte), **CCL2** (MCP-1, upregulated in inflammatory state), **ICAM1** (adhesion molecule, upregulated in inflammatory state).

## Location

Inflammatory pericytes are located at the abluminal surface of capillaries and post-capillary venules in the dermis, particularly in areas of active or resolving inflammation. Pericyte_2 cells are enriched in inflamed skin compared to healthy resting skin, where Pericyte_1 (RGS5+) predominates.

## Function

### Inflammatory activation and vascular permeability

Inflammatory pericytes upregulate cytokines, chemokines, and cell adhesion molecules, enabling them to modulate vascular permeability and leukocyte extravasation at sites of tissue injury or infection. Upregulation of CCL2 (MCP-1) and ICAM1 promotes monocyte and T cell recruitment to the dermis.

### Contractility and vascular remodelling

The high expression of TAGLN (transgelin) and ACTA2 in Pericyte_2 cells reflects their highly contractile phenotype. TAGLN is an early marker of smooth muscle differentiation and contributes to actin cytoskeleton organisation and vascular tone regulation in activated pericytes.

### Role in skin disease

In inflammatory skin conditions such as atopic dermatitis and psoriasis, pericyte activation contributes to the neovascularisation and vascular remodelling that characterise the inflammatory microenvironment. The balance between non-inflammatory (Pericyte_1, RGS5+) and inflammatory (Pericyte_2, TAGLN+) pericyte states may contribute to the resolution or perpetuation of skin inflammation (Bensa et al., 2023).
