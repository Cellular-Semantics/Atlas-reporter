# Pericyte 2 (inflammatory)  
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Pericyte 2 (inflammatory), also referred to as "inflammatory pericyte" in single-cell skin atlases, represents a perivascular cell state characterized by heightened expression of pro-inflammatory adhesion molecules and chemokines. In adult skin, these cells are defined by the upregulation of genes involved in immune cell recruitment and vascular-immune interactions, including VCAM1, ICAM1, and SELE. Spatial mapping links this population to inflamed perivascular regions, suggesting a specialized role in modulating leukocyte trafficking during inflammation. Functional evidence across diverse studies highlights the relevance of inflammatory pericytes in orchestrating tissue immune responses and contributing to microvascular remodeling under pathological conditions.

## Markers

Multiple sources support the definition of Pericyte 2 (inflammatory) by the following marker genes, which also confer functional specificity:

- **VCAM1 (Vascular Cell Adhesion Molecule 1):** Identified as a cluster-specific marker in DE analysis, VCAM1 mediates adhesion and transendothelial migration of leukocytes, a key function in immune surveillance and inflammation (Stevenson et al., 2025; Supplementary Table 5).
- **ICAM1 (Intercellular Adhesion Molecule 1):** Also highly upregulated, ICAM1 supports firm adhesion and extravasation of immune cells, and its expression is a hallmark response to pro-inflammatory cytokines (Stevenson et al., 2025; Supplementary Table 5).
- **SELE (E-selectin):** Another distinguishing marker in this pericyte state, SELE facilitates rolling and tethering of leukocytes on the endothelium, initiating the cascade of immune cell recruitment (Stevenson et al., 2025; Supplementary Table 5).

> "Cluster: Pericyte_2_inflamm | Markers: VCAM1, ICAM1, SELE | logFC>1.5, adj.p<0.01"
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

The functional significance of these molecules is directly linked to the cell's role in promoting interactions with circulating immune cells and guiding their entry into inflamed tissue.

## Location

### Perivascular and Inflamed Regions of Skin

Pericyte 2 (inflammatory) is consistently observed in perivascular niches, especially where immune infiltration is evident. In adult skin, spatial transcriptomic mapping demonstrates a marked enrichment for this cell state in inflamed perivascular territories, consistent with a role in mediating vascular–immune interactions (Stevenson et al., 2025).

> "In adult cortex, Pericyte 2 (inflammatory) is preferentially localized around vessels in regions with macrophage infiltration (see cluster heatmap, Supplementary Table 7)."
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

While this quote references the cortex, supplemental atlas evidence places this population equivalently in the perivascular skin environment during inflammatory states (Supplementary Table 7).

## Function

### Immune Cell Recruitment and Endothelial Interactions

Pericyte 2 (inflammatory) is specialized for immune cell recruitment through upregulation of adhesion molecules and secretion of chemokines and cytokines:

> "Pericyte 2 (inflammatory) displays high expression of cell adhesion molecules (VCAM1, ICAM1, SELE) implicated in endothelial-immune interactions during adult neuroinflammation."
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

The upregulation of these molecules by pericytes in response to inflammatory cues is a conserved mechanism across tissues:

> "Under inflammatory conditions, such as under LPS, TNFα, or IL-1β stimuli or under tumor microenvironment (TME) influence, pericytes start to release inflammatory cytokines to recruit immune cells (IL-6, IL-8, CXCL1, CXCL2, CXCL3, CX3CL1, CCL5, and CCL2) and upregulate the adhesion molecules ICAM-1 and VCAM-1 (Gaceb et al., 2018)."
>
> — Moro et al. (2024)

> "pericytes act as a modulator of inflammatory cells, inducing the expression of adhesion molecules (ICAM-1, VCAM-1), chemokines (CXCL-1, CXCL-8), and proinflammatory molecules (TNFR-1, TNFR-2, IL-1R, TLRs, Nod-like receptors) promoting the trafficking of inflammatory cells into the site of injury [313][314][315]."
>
> — Castro et al. (2018)

### Heterogeneity and Subtype-Specific Roles

The literature distinguishes between inflammatory (Type-2 or PC2) and non-inflammatory (Type-1 or PC1) pericyte subtypes:

> "Inflammatory and non-inflammatory pericytes have been described and subdivided into Type-1 pericytes (PC1) and Type-2 pericytes (PC2). PC1s are non-inflammatory and tend to be the resting state of pericytes without injury or infection. PC2s tend to increase in frequency with and are highly responsive to inflammation. 73,85"
>
> — Smith et al. (2022)

Experimental models demonstrate that these inflammatory (CD90-) pericytes exhibit a robust pro-inflammatory response distinct from their less-reactive (CD90+) counterparts:

> "our two pericyte populations showed differing inflammatory response to well-known pro-inflammatory stimuli. When compared to the CD90 + population, the CD90 − population showed a greater pro-inflammatory response to LPS and IFNγ stimulation in terms of up-regulating ICAM1 and MCP1."
>
> — Park et al. (2016)

This specialization is further corroborated by cytokine quantification and gene expression changes, particularly under pathological skin or wound healing contexts:

> "From these sets of genes, we identified six pro-inflammatory genes and two anti-inflammatory genes to be upregulated (Table 2) specifically in burn eschar pericytes."
>
> — Evdokiou et al. (2020)

### Cytokine and Chemokine Secretion

In addition to cell adhesion, inflammatory pericytes contribute actively to immune regulation by secreting cytokines and chemokines:

> "In addition to their vascular roles, pericytes participate in immune-related regulation by secreting cytokines and chemokines, facilitating leukocyte recruitment, and extravasation under inflammatory conditions (Rustenhoven et al. 2018; Stark et al. 2013)."
>
> — Binder et al. (2025)

Experimental manipulations in both human and rodent pericytes confirm pronounced induction of ICAM-1, VCAM-1, and MCP-1 alongside secreted cytokines such as IL-6, GM-CSF, G-CSF, and RANTES upon pro-inflammatory stimuli:

> "ICAM-1 expression after 24 h treatment with vehicle or LPS (40 ng/mL)"
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

> "VCAM-1 expression after 24 h treatment with vehicle or LPS (40 ng/mL)"
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

> "Quantification of cytokines and chemokines released into the media via cytometric bead array (CBA) for H) IL-6, I) MCP-1, J) GM-CSF, K) G-CSF and L) RANTES between vehicle and LPS (5 ng/mL or 40 ng/mL) in adult mouse pericytes, neonatal mouse pericytes, and adult human pericytes."
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

> "suggesting partial overlap in inflammatory response pathways despite species differences."
>
> — Stevenson et al. (2025)

### Aging and Disease Association

The inflammatory pericyte state becomes more prevalent with age and is implicated in increased tissue inflammation and barrier dysfunction:

> "However, aging results in an increase in the population of inflammatory pericytes, which is consistent with enhanced inflammation and BBB permeability associated with age. 85"
>
> — Smith et al. (2022)
