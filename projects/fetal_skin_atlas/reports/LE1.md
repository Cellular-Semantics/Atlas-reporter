# Lymphatic Endothelial 1 (LE1)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Lymphatic Endothelial 1 (LE1) cells, also known as "Lymphatic Endothelial 1," represent a subset of lymphatic endothelial cells in adult human skin. These cells are distinguished by the high expression of canonical lymphatic markers such as PROX1, LYVE1, PDPN, and VEGFR3, along with CCL21, and are characteristically CD31 positive. In the dermal compartment, they form part of the lymphatic capillary network and are anatomically defined by their location in the subcapsular and medullary sinus regions. LE1 cells have a role in maintaining homeostasis and immune regulation, particularly through their interactions with immune cells and involvement in lymphangiogenesis. Their distinct marker profile and high expression of immune-modulatory molecules, such as CD200, reflect specialized functional properties in the skin's vascular landscape.

## Markers

LE1 cells are defined by a distinctive set of molecular markers, each of which has functional and diagnostic significance:

- **PROX1**: A master transcription factor essential for lymphatic endothelial cell fate determination. High PROX1 expression is a defining characteristic of LE1 cells, confirming their lymphatic lineage (Supplementary Table S3; Bauer et al., 2025).
- **LYVE1**: Lymphatic vessel endothelial hyaluronan receptor 1, specialized for the transport and binding of hyaluronan, a glycosaminoglycan abundant in lymph (Supplementary Table S3; Lawrance et al., 2016).

    > "we detected anti-lymphatic vessel endothelial hyaluronan receptor 1 (LYVE-1) immunoreactivity in serial sections and overlaid them during SEM observation."
    >
    > — Hayashida et al. (2023)

- **PDPN (podoplanin)**: A mucin-type transmembrane protein critical for lymphatic endothelial cell adhesion, lymphatic development, and immune cell trafficking (Supplementary Table S3). Its co-expression with CD31 is commonly used to identify lymphatic versus blood endothelial cells.

    > "podoplanin and PROX1 were used interchangeable as lymphatic markers for various analyses in this study"
    >
    > — Rütsche et al. (2022)

- **VEGFR3 (FLT4)**: Receptor tyrosine kinase involved in lymphangiogenesis and lymphatic endothelial cell proliferation (Supplementary Table S3; Aithabathula et al., 2023; Bauer et al., 2025).

    > "we performed quantitative real-time PCR (qRT-PCR) experiments to determine the mRNA expression of key LEC markers, FLT4 (VEGFR3), LYVE-1 and podoplanin (PDPN) in these LECs and compared them with human aortic endothelial cells (HAoEC, Lonza) (S1 Table ). The qRT-PCR data demonstrated significantly higher mRNA expression of all these markers in LECs compared with HAoEC"
    >
    > — Aithabathula et al. (2023)

- **CCL21**: A chemokine involved in directing dendritic cell and lymphocyte migration towards lymphatic vessels, and found among the top differentially expressed genes in LE1 (Supplementary Table S6).

- **CD31 (PECAM1)**: An endothelial marker; LE1 cells are identified as CD31 and podoplanin double-positive, which distinguishes them from blood endothelial cells (Bauer et al., 2025; Dellinger et al., 2011).

    > "Primary adult human dermal lymphatic endothelial cells (LECs) were purchased from LONZA (CC-2810). The certificate analysis sheet supplied by LONZA for each vial of cells indicated that greater than 95% of the cells were LECs (CD31 and podoplanin double-positive). This was determined by FACS."
    >
    > — Dellinger et al. (2011)

- **CD200**: A cell surface glycoprotein involved in immunoregulation, with significantly higher expression on lymphatic than blood capillaries in adult skin.

    > "significantly higher expression of CD200 on j/a lymphatic capillaries as compared to the j/a blood capillaries (67.0 ± 16% vs. 14.0 ± 2.0%, respectively, *** p < 0.0001)"
    >
    > — Rütsche et al. (2022)

The robust co-expression of these markers validates the lymphatic identity and functional specialization of LE1 cells.

## Location

### Dermal Skin Compartment

LE1 serves as a major lymphatic endothelial population in adult human skin, residing within the dermal lymphatic capillary networks (Name resolution; Bauer et al., 2025; Dellinger et al., 2011; Aithabathula et al., 2023).

### Lymph Node Sinus Regions

Spatial mapping indicates LE1 cells are predominantly found in the subcapsular and medullary sinus regions of adult lymph nodes (Supplementary Data S2), correlating their position in the skin-draining lymphatics with microanatomical zones important for immune cell trafficking.

### Quantitative Proportion

Lymphatic endothelium, including LE1 cells, is a significant yet minority population within dermal vessels:

> "lymphatic endothelium represents 31.0 ± 10.6% of all vessels present in this skin type"
>
> — Rütsche et al. (2022)

This highlights the compartmental contribution of LE1 to tissue homeostasis and immune surveillance.

## Function

### Lymphangiogenesis and Vascular Maintenance

LE1 cells express markers — such as VEGFR3 and PROX1 — critical for the formation and maintenance of lymphatic vessels. The presence of LYVE1 further implicates them in the regulation of extracellular matrix components (e.g., hyaluronan turnover), which is essential for vessel permeability and leukocyte transmigration (Lawrance et al., 2016; Hayashida et al., 2023).

> "On days 2 and 5, spherical cells, probably myeloid cells, were attached and fused to the LYVE-1-positive lymphatic vessel walls."
>
> — Hayashida et al. (2023)

While the above observation is from a mouse model, it underscores the functional relevance of LYVE1+ lymphatic endothelial cells like LE1 in regeneration processes.

### Immune Regulation and Leukocyte Trafficking

LE1 cells exhibit high levels of CCL21, which is pivotal for dendritic cell and lymphocyte migration into lymphatics, thus influencing the initiation and regulation of immune responses (Supplementary Table S6). In parallel, their elevated expression of CD200 supports a modulatory role over immune cell activation and tissue inflammation:

> "significantly higher expression of CD200 on j/a lymphatic capillaries as compared to the j/a blood capillaries (67.0 ± 16% vs. 14.0 ± 2.0%, respectively, *** p < 0.0001)"
>
> — Rütsche et al. (2022)

### Distinction from Blood Endothelium

LE1 cells are identified as CD31+PDPN+ and show faint co-expression of CD31 compared to blood endothelial cells, which are CD31+PDPN−.

> "lymphatic capillaries showed the expression of podoplanin, a lymphatic endothelium-specific marker, and a faint CD31 co-expression (white arrows)"
>
> — Rütsche et al. (2022)

## Structure / Morphology

LE1 cells in skin form a capillary network characterized by their CD31 and podoplanin double positivity, with differential CD200 expression that visually and functionally distinguishes them from neighboring blood capillaries:

> "lymphatic capillaries are CD31 + Podo + (white arrows). Note the presence of blood capillaries (CD31 + Podo − ) which are mainly negative for CD200 (empty arrows), as well as the presence of lymphatic capillaries (CD31 + Podo + ) showing the expression of CD200 marker (white filled arrows)."
>
> — Rütsche et al. (2022)

Their prevalence has been quantified at about one third of all capillaries in the adult skin dermis, indicating their structural prominence within the microvasculature.
