# Afferent Lymphatic Endothelial Cell (LE1) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [lymphatic endothelial cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002138) (CL:0002138, broad match — no exact CL term for initial afferent lymphatic EC substate)

## Summary

LE1 is one of two lymphatic endothelial cell (LEC) subclusters in adult human dermis identified by Reynolds et al. (2021). Reynolds et al. identify LE1 and LE2 by differential expression of CCL21 and PDPN: LE1 has higher CCL21 and lower PDPN relative to LE2. The CCL21 expression and co-expression of angiogenesis factors CAVIN2 and CCND1 support the interpretation of LE1 as representing initial afferent lymphatic capillaries — the blind-ended lymphatic structures that absorb interstitial fluid and capture antigen-laden dendritic cells from the dermis. Both LE1 and LE2 express pan-lymphatic markers LYVE1 and PDPN, and are distinct from the PECAM1+EMCN+ vascular endothelium in adult dermis.

## Markers

> "two sub-clusters of lymphatic endothelial cells defined by the differential expression of CCL21 and PDPN, which are higher in LE1 and LE2 respectively"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "LE1 cells express higher levels of the chemoattractant CCL21, which mediates dendritic cell (DC) migration into skin draining lymph nodes, as well as angiogenesis factors CAVIN2 and CCND1, further supporting their function as initial afferent lymphatics"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers:
- **LYVE1** — lymphatic hyaluronan receptor; pan-lymphatic LEC marker
- **PDPN (podoplanin)** — lymphatic marker; lower in LE1 than LE2
- **PROX1** — lymphatic transcription factor; pan-LEC marker
- **CCL21** — chemokine; higher in LE1; mediates CCR7+ DC entry into lymphatics
- **CAVIN2** — caveolae-associated protein; angiogenesis/vascular function
- **CCND1** — cyclin D1; cell cycle/angiogenesis factor expressed by LE1

Liu et al. (2022) confirm lymphatic endothelial markers in adult skin:

> "Endothelial cells in the healthy adult dermis constitute the vascular endothelium (PECAM1, EMCN, SELE, CD93, CLDN5, VWF, and CDH5) and lymphatic endothelium (LYVE1, PDPN, PROX1, and CLDN5)"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

## Location

LE1 cells form the initial (capillary) lymphatics of the adult dermis. Initial lymphatics are blind-ended, highly permeable vessels that absorb interstitial fluid and immune cells from the tissue. They are characterised by overlapping "oak-leaf" shaped endothelial cells with discontinuous button-like junctions, enabling dendritic cell and macrophage entry. Reynolds et al. (2021) identify LE1 and LE2 by UMAP analysis of non-immune, non-keratinocyte cells in healthy adult skin.

## Function

### 1. DC recruitment to skin-draining lymph nodes

LE1 cells constitutively express CCL21, the primary chemoattractant that guides CCR7+ dendritic cells (including migratory LC4 and Mig. DC) from the dermis into the lymphatic lumen. Reynolds et al. (2021) specifically reference this functional role:

> "LE1 cells express higher levels of the chemoattractant CCL21, which mediates dendritic cell (DC) migration into skin draining lymph nodes, as well as angiogenesis factors CAVIN2 and CCND1, further supporting their function as initial afferent lymphatics"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### 2. Interstitial fluid drainage

As initial afferent lymphatics, LE1 cells form vessels responsible for draining excess interstitial fluid from the dermis. This fluid-homeostatic function is complementary to the immune function of DC transport and prevents oedema in the skin.

### 3. Angiogenesis and vascular remodelling support

Expression of CAVIN2 and CCND1 in LE1 cells links this subset to regulation of lymphangiogenesis and vascular remodelling — functions important for maintaining lymphatic homeostasis in healthy skin and potentially for lymphatic expansion during inflammation.

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) include LE1 and LE2 in their comprehensive atlas of adult healthy and diseased skin endothelial cells. The LE1 CCL21-rich phenotype means that any expansion or activation of this population in AD or psoriasis could amplify DC traffic to regional lymph nodes, contributing to systemic T cell sensitisation.
