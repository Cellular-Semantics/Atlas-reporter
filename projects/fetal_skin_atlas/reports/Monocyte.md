# Monocytes in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: prenatal
Cell Ontology: [classical monocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000860) (CL:0000860, broad match)

## Summary

Monocytes are myeloid mononuclear phagocytes identified in prenatal human skin from early gestation (7-17 PCW) in the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas. In the companion cross-organ developmental immune atlas, Suo et al. (2022) distinguished three prenatal monocyte subsets -- CXCR4hi, CCR2hi, and IL1Bhi -- reflecting a maturation trajectory from bone marrow retention through tissue egress to peripheral tissue adaptation. Monocytes in prenatal skin are characterised by expression of CD14, S100A8, S100A9, and FCN1. Unlike macrophages, which are seeded from yolk-sac-derived progenitors as early as 6 PCW, monocytes derive from definitive haematopoiesis and are recruited to peripheral tissues including skin via CCR2-dependent mechanisms. In the prenatal skin microenvironment, monocytes are present in a downregulated immune milieu compared to adult skin, consistent with a possible role in tissue homeostasis and morphogenesis rather than classical inflammatory functions during early gestation.

## Markers

### Canonical monocyte markers: CD14, S100A8, S100A9, FCN1

Monocytes in prenatal tissues are defined by a core transcriptional signature shared with adult classical monocytes. Suo et al. (2022) identified three monocyte subsets distinguished by differential expression of CXCR4, CCR2, and IL1B:

> "Our cross-tissue analysis distinguished three distinct subsets of monocytes, characterized by a differential distribution between prenatal bone marrow and peripheral tissues and by the expression of CXCR4, CCR2, or IL1B"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

- **CD14** -- The canonical monocyte surface marker, used in FACS isolation of immune cells from prenatal skin (Gopee et al., 2024; Schuster et al., 2012).
- **S100A8 / S100A9** -- Alarmin genes highly expressed by classical monocytes. In cord blood monocytes, S100A8 and S100A9 expression is strongly associated with fetal inflammation (Golubinskaya et al., 2020).
- **FCN1** -- Ficolin-1, a complement pathway activator characteristic of classical monocytes.
- **CCR2** -- Chemokine receptor marking egressing monocytes; CCR2hi monocytes in prenatal bone marrow express proliferation genes prior to seeding peripheral tissues (Suo et al., 2022).
- **IL1B** -- Upregulated in peripheral tissue-adapted monocytes, marking the most mature monocyte state in prenatal skin (Suo et al., 2022).

### Developmental monocyte surface markers

Schuster et al. (2012) demonstrated that prenatal human dermis contains CD45+HLA-DR+ leukocytes from 9 weeks estimated gestational age (EGA), including CD11c+ monocyte-lineage cells.

## Location

### Dermis

Monocytes are located in the prenatal skin dermis, recruited from the circulation. Gopee et al. (2024) identified immune cells including monocytes as mesoderm-derived populations present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Developmental timing

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Monocytes arrive in prenatal skin as part of the innate immune cell wave. Miah et al. (2021) reviewed that the fetal liver produces the first granulocyte-monocyte progenitors (GMPs) and blood monocytes at 4-5 PCW, which then seed peripheral organs including skin.

## Function

### Tissue homeostasis and morphogenesis (early gestation)

Suo et al. (2022) demonstrated that prenatal monocytes in early gestation (<12 PCW) express pro-inflammatory chemokines (CXCL3, CXCL2, CXCL8) associated with angiogenesis and tissue morphogenesis, rather than classical immune effector functions. This parallels the pattern observed in prenatal macrophages and mast cells:

> "These findings suggest that early macrophages and mast cells may contribute to angiogenesis, tissue morphogenesis and homeostasis as previously reported, prior to adopting traditional immunological functions."
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

### Monocyte maturation and tissue seeding

A conserved process of proliferation and maturation accompanies monocyte migration from bone marrow to peripheral tissues. Suo et al. (2022) proposed a model whereby CXCR4hi monocytes are retained in bone marrow until they transition to a CCR2hi proliferative state, enabling egress. In peripheral tissues, CCR2hi monocytes further mature into IL1Bhi monocytes.

### Differentiation potential

In prenatal tissues, monocytes can serve as precursors to tissue macrophages and dendritic cells. Miah et al. (2021) reviewed that fetal monocytes arise from both yolk-sac-derived myeloid-biased progenitors (YSMPs) and definitive HSC-derived GMPs, with the fetal liver serving as the main haematopoietic organ during this period.

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological data for prenatal skin monocytes was found in the traversed literature. In general, monocytes are mononuclear cells 14-20 microns in diameter with a kidney-shaped nucleus and fine azurophilic granules.
