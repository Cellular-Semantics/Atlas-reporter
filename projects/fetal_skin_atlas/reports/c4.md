# Differentiated Keratinocyte (c4) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match — no exact CL term for differentiated KC subcluster)

## Summary

Cluster c4 is a differentiated keratinocyte (KC) state in adult human epidermis. It is one of four differentiated KC clusters (c3, c4, c6, c7) identified by Leiden clustering in Reynolds et al. (2021). Differentiated KCs are suprabasal cells distinguished from undifferentiated (basal) KCs by expression of suprabasal keratins KRT1 and KRT10, and from proliferating KCs (c2) by absence of cell cycle genes such as CDK1 and PCNA. Reynolds et al. (2021) describe two inferred differentiation trajectories from basal keratinocytes — one with high lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1) and one with lower LB transcript levels — with IRF1 and SOX9 as differentially expressed transcription factors marking the two arms. Cluster c4, together with c3, c6, and c7, occupies the differentiated (suprabasal) compartment of this trajectory space.

## Markers

> "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC* (cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021), Fig. 4D legend

> "Proliferating keratinocytes (CDK1+,PCNA+) have lower expression of suprabasal cell transcripts e.g. KRT1, KRT10 that characterize differentiated keratinocytes"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers of differentiated KCs:
- **KRT1, KRT10** — suprabasal keratins; define the differentiated state
- **FLG (filaggrin)**, **IVL (involucrin)**, **CNFN (cornifelin)** — terminal differentiation markers
- **ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1** — lamellar body-related transcripts enriched in one differentiation arm

## Location

Cluster c4 keratinocytes are suprabasal cells of the adult interfollicular epidermis. The gene expression of differentiated KC clusters in Reynolds et al. (2021) was validated against the Human Protein Atlas for spatial markers (KRT1, KRT10), confirming their suprabasal epidermal localisation.

## Function

### 1. Epidermal cornification

Differentiated KC clusters represent cells undergoing the cornification programme: producing structural proteins and lipids required for the stratum corneum barrier. Reynolds et al. (2021) identify dual differentiation trajectories:

> "Force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses reveal dual inferred differentiation trajectories from the stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA and CAV1/2)-enriched basal keratinocytes into terminally differentiated keratinocytes expressing CNFN, FLG and IVL"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "One arm expresses high levels of lamellar body (LB)­related transcripts such as ABCA12, CKAP4 and CLIP1 that characterize late epidermal differentiation and the other arm lower levels of LB-related transcripts"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### 2. Barrier lipid production

The lamellar body pathway arm, enriched for ABCA12, CKAP4, and CLIP1, is responsible for secreting intercellular lipids into the stratum corneum. As summarised by Liu et al. (2022):

> "force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses on scRNA-seq data defined two differentiation pathways of basal keratinocytes to suprabasal cells, with or without a high level of the lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (glucosylceramide transporter ABCA12; cytoskeleton-associated protein 4, CKAP4; and CAP-Gly domain-containing linker protein 1, CLIP1), that characterized late epidermal differentiation in healthy skin"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

### 3. Co-expression of ichthyosis-related genes

> "Notably, keratinocytes expressing LB-related transcripts co-express genes associated with autosomal recessive congenital ichthyosis, such as ABCA12, NIPAL4, SLC27A4 and TGM1"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) document altered proportions of the eight keratinocyte clusters in AD and psoriasis lesional skin. The inflammatory differentiated state (c8, "Differentiated KC*") is distinct from c4, expressing ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20. The proportional changes in c3–c7 clusters reflect disease-specific remodelling of the epidermal differentiation programme.
