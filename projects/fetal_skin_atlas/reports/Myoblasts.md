# Myoblasts (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [skeletal muscle myoblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000515) (CL:0000515, exact match)

## Summary

Myoblasts are proliferative muscle precursor cells identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas as a fine-grained annotation within the broad "Skeletal muscle" lineage in the fetal (7-17 PCW) dataset. Myoblasts are mononucleate, mitotic cells committed to the muscle lineage that do not yet express mature skeletal muscle myosin heavy chains. They undergo proliferation, migrate to target sites, and then differentiate into myocytes that fuse to form multinucleated muscle fibers. In fetal skin, myoblasts contribute to the formation of arrector pili muscles and potentially the panniculus carnosus. The atlas also identifies myoblasts in the skin organoid (SkO) model. They are part of the mesoderm-derived compartment (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

Myoblasts are characterized by expression of PAX7 (satellite cell/myoblast marker), MYOD1 (myogenic determination factor), MYF5, and DES (desmin), while lacking expression of mature muscle structural proteins like MYH1/2. Specific markers for this atlas cluster are defined in Supplementary Table 3.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

Myoblasts are identified in fetal skin (7-17 PCW). They reside in the dermal/subcutaneous compartment where skeletal muscle forms:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

Myoblasts serve as the proliferative precursor pool for skeletal muscle formation in prenatal skin. They proliferate, migrate to appropriate sites, and upon terminal differentiation, fuse with each other or with existing myofibers to form the multinucleated muscle cells of the arrector pili muscles.
