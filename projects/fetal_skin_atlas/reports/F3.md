# Fibroblast 3 (F3) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [skin fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002620) (CL:0002620, broad match — no exact CL term for this minor fibroblast subtype)

## Summary

F3 corresponds to Fb3 in Reynolds et al. (2021), one of three fibroblast subsets present in healthy adult human dermis. Fb3 is a minor population alongside the dominant Fb1 (F1) and moderately abundant Fb2 (F2). All three fibroblast subsets share expression of extracellular matrix (ECM)-related genes including MMP2, COL1A1, COL1A2, and NT5E (encoding CD73). F3 is distinguished from F1 and F2 by its distinct transcriptional profile, though its specific marker genes are less well characterised than the chemokine-enriched Fb2/F2. Reynolds et al. (2021) note that dermal fibroblast heterogeneity, encompassing structural and immunomodulatory subtypes, has been previously reported at the single-cell level, positioning F3 within a well-established framework of functional fibroblast diversity in human skin.

ECM-related markers shared across fibroblast subsets (F1, F2, F3):
- **MMP2** — matrix metalloproteinase 2; ECM remodelling
- **COL1A1** — collagen type I alpha 1; structural ECM
- **COL1A2** — collagen type I alpha 2; structural ECM
- **NT5E (CD73)** — ecto-5'-nucleotidase; fibroblast surface marker

Independent single-cell studies (Sole-Boldo et al., 2020) define four human dermal fibroblast subpopulations with differential secretory, mesenchymal, and pro-inflammatory functional annotations, providing context for F3 as a distinct functional subtype:

> "Our results define four main subpopulations that can be spatially localized and show differential secretory, mesenchymal and pro-inflammatory functional annotations."
>
> — Sole-Boldo et al. (2020)

## Location

F3/Fb3 cells reside in the adult dermis alongside the dominant F1 and moderately abundant F2 fibroblast populations. The dermis contains spatially segregated fibroblast subpopulations corresponding to distinct anatomical positions (papillary vs. reticular dermis), consistent with the functional heterogeneity captured by the three-subset classification of Reynolds et al.

## Function

### 1. Extracellular matrix maintenance

As a dermal fibroblast, F3 contributes to synthesis and remodelling of the dermal ECM through shared expression of COL1A1, COL1A2 (collagen type I), and MMP2 (matrix metalloproteinase 2). These genes underpin the structural integrity of the dermis.

### 2. Fibroblast subset diversity

F3 represents a minor, transcriptionally distinct fibroblast state within the healthy adult dermis. The broader framework of fibroblast heterogeneity — encompassing structural and immunomodulatory subtypes — has been documented across multiple single-cell studies of human skin, with Sole-Boldo et al. (2020) demonstrating age-related changes in fibroblast subpopulation balance:

> "we found that this fibroblast 'priming' becomes reduced with age"
>
> — Sole-Boldo et al. (2020)

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) report that Fb2/F2 fibroblasts are significantly enriched in inflammatory skin disease (psoriasis and atopic dermatitis), while specific changes in Fb3/F3 abundance in disease are less characterised. F3 may represent a homeostatic fibroblast subtype whose relative proportions shift in the context of fibroblast reprogramming during inflammation.
