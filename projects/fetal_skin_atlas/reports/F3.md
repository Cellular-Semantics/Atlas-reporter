# Dermal Fibroblast 3 (F3)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Dermal fibroblast 3 (F3), also resolved as "Dermal fibroblast 3", represents a defined subpopulation of adult human skin fibroblasts. F3 cells are predominantly located in the reticular dermis, the deeper and structurally denser layer of the skin. Their molecular signature includes high expression of genes such as C7, MFAP4, and FMO2, and they are functionally annotated with roles in extracellular matrix (ECM) organization. As part of the broader fibroblast heterogeneity within the dermis, F3 likely contributes to the maintenance of skin structure and integrity, specifically within the reticular compartment, and is involved in tissue homeostasis and wound repair.

## Markers

Key marker genes for Dermal fibroblast 3 include C7, MFAP4, and FMO2, identified through differential expression analysis.

- **C7 (Collagen type VII alpha 1 chain):** C7 is integral to the formation of anchoring fibrils at the dermal-epidermal junction, important for maintaining skin integrity and resistance to mechanical stress (Supplementary Table 2). The presence of C7 reinforces the subtype’s connective tissue specialization.
- **MFAP4 (Microfibril-associated protein 4):** MFAP4 is associated with elastic fiber assembly and is characteristic of a population engaged in extracellular matrix production and remodeling (Supplementary Table 2).
- **FMO2 (Flavin containing monooxygenase 2):** FMO2, while less characterized in the context of fibroblast biology, may relate to cellular detoxification processes and is noted for its specific enrichment in this subtype (Supplementary Table 2).

These markers together highlight the ECM-organizing and structural role of the F3 subpopulation.

## Location

### Reticular Dermis

Dermal fibroblast 3 is predominantly found in the reticular dermis, the deeper layer of the dermis characterized by dense bundles of collagen and extracellular matrix components. Spatial mapping confirms that:

> "Dermal fibroblast 3 was predominantly located in the reticular dermis (see Supplementary Fig. 5b)."

This anatomical placement is consistent with literature identifying the reticular dermis as having a more defined structure relative to the papillary dermis:

> "reticular fibroblasts exist in the reticular dermis, which is thicker and has a well-organized structure of collagen fiber bundles [62]."
>
> — Oh et al. (2022)

## Function

### Extracellular Matrix Organization

Functional annotation and gene ontology enrichment analyses consistently identify ECM organization as a central role for F3:

> "GO term enrichment for F3: extracellular matrix organization"
>
> — Supplementary Table 4

This aligns with broader fibroblast specialization within the reticular dermis:

> "reticular fibroblast presents an enhanced expression of extracellular matrix (ECM)-related genes such as LOXL3, a lysyl oxidase related to elastin and collagen synthesis [21]."
>
> — Salazar et al. (2022)

### Role in Skin Structure and Repair

Reticular fibroblasts are implicated in the initial phases of wound healing and the establishment of a collagen-rich dermal environment:

> "Reticular fibroblasts mediate the initial phase of wound repair by migrating into the wound site and producing a collagen-enriched dermis; however, reticular dermis is unable to form hair follicles [63]."
>
> — Oh et al. (2022)

### Contribution to Dermal Subtype Diversity

Single-cell and transcriptomic studies support the presence of distinct fibroblast subtypes, of which F3 forms one, emphasizing the diversity and specialization within adult skin:

> "fibroblasts may robustly be assigned to three major types (axes A-C), which in turn are composed of ten major subtypes (clusters) which we denominated A1-A4, B1-B2 and C1-C4."
>
> — Ascensión et al. (2020)

These subpopulations, mapped across multiple datasets, account for most adult dermal fibroblasts and underscore F3's role in skin homeostasis.

## Structure / Morphology

The reticular dermis, where F3 resides, is defined by its thickness and organized collagen bundles:

> "reticular fibroblasts exist in the reticular dermis, which is thicker and has a well-organized structure of collagen fiber bundles [62]."
>
> — Oh et al. (2022)

This structural context correlates with the functional attributes of F3 in supporting dermal strength and resilience.
