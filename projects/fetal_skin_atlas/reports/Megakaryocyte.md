# Megakaryocyte in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [megakaryocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000556) (CL:0000556, exact match)

## Summary

Megakaryocytes are large haematopoietic cells that produce platelets through a process of endomitosis and proplatelet formation. They are identified as a distinct cell population in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024), within the haematopoietic progenitor compartment spanning 7-17 PCW. Popescu et al. (2019) confirmed the presence of megakaryocytes in fetal skin alongside erythroid cells, mast cells, and MEMPs. Suo et al. (2022) demonstrated that megakaryocyte/erythroid progenitors are present in developing skin and spleen, indicating that haematopoiesis extends beyond the liver and bone marrow during fetal development. Fetal megakaryocytes differ substantially from adult megakaryocytes in size, ploidy, and the functional properties of the platelets they produce.

## Markers

Megakaryocyte-specifying genes identified in the fetal haematopoietic hierarchy include:

> "Genes dynamically modulated in the specification of erythroid, megakaryocyte and mast cell lineages are distinct: TAL1 and KLF1 in erythroid; F11R, PBX1 and MEIS1 in megakaryocyte; and CPA3 and MS4A2 in mast cells."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

- **F11R** (JAM-A/CD321): Upregulated during megakaryocyte specification from the MEMP.
- **PBX1**: Transcription factor involved in megakaryocyte commitment.
- **MEIS1**: Co-regulatory transcription factor for megakaryocyte development.
- **CD41** (ITGA2B): A canonical megakaryocyte/platelet marker used to identify megakaryocytes in developing tissues.
- **PF4** (CXCL4/Platelet Factor 4): A megakaryocyte-specific chemokine.

## Location

### Prenatal skin

Megakaryocytes are annotated in the prenatal skin atlas within the broad "Megakaryocyte" category at both fine and broad granularity levels (Gopee et al., 2024). They reside within the haematopoietic compartment and are part of the "Myeloid/Megakaryocyte-erythroid enriched" microenvironment (ME2) identified by spatial transcriptomics.

> "Megakaryocytes, erythroid cells, mast cells and MEMP, are present in skin."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

> "we detected B cell progenitors in almost all prenatal organs, megakaryocyte/erythroid progenitors in developing spleen and skin, and myeloid progenitors in the thymus, spleen, skin, and kidney"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

### Fetal liver

The fetal liver is the principal site of megakaryopoiesis during the 7-17 PCW developmental window. Fetal liver megakaryocytes provide higher thrombopoietic activity than yolk sac megakaryocytes, releasing platelets through both membrane budding and proplatelet formation.

## Function

### Platelet production (thrombopoiesis)

Megakaryocytes produce platelets, anucleate cell fragments essential for haemostasis. Mature megakaryocytes undergo endomitosis (nuclear replication without cell division), developing polyploid nuclei and a complex demarcation membrane system that serves as the template for proplatelet and platelet formation. Each megakaryocyte can produce thousands of platelets.

### Fetal-specific features

Fetal megakaryocytes differ from adult megakaryocytes in several important respects. Fetal megakaryocyte progenitors proliferate faster and produce larger megakaryocyte colonies. However, fetal megakaryocytes are smaller, have lower ploidy, and produce functionally hyporeactive platelets compared to adult megakaryocytes. Fetal platelets have reduced expression of integrin-activating proteins and lower surface P-selectin expression, reflecting a fetal-specific transcriptional program regulated in part by the Lin28b-let7 axis.

### Potential roles beyond haemostasis

Megakaryocytes and platelets have roles beyond haemostasis, including regulation of angiogenesis through the release of pro- and anti-angiogenic growth factors (such as VEGF-A and thrombospondin), immune modulation, and vascular development. In the prenatal skin context, these functions may contribute to the developing vasculature.

## Structure / Morphology

Megakaryocytes are the largest haematopoietic cells, typically 50-100 micrometres in diameter in adults. They are characterized by a large, multilobulated nucleus resulting from endomitosis, and abundant cytoplasm containing a demarcation membrane system and alpha granules. Fetal megakaryocytes tend to be smaller than their adult counterparts.
