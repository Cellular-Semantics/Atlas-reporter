# Dermal condensate
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Cell Ontology: No CL term (new term needed; related but distinct from CL:0000346 hair follicle dermal papilla cell, which is the mature form)  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

The dermal condensate (Dc) is a transient mesenchymal aggregate that forms beneath the hair placode during embryonic hair follicle morphogenesis. It is the immediate precursor of the dermal papilla and plays a central role in instructing epithelial invagination. In the prenatal skin atlas, dermal condensate cells were identified from 12 PCW and are characterized by FOXD1+/SOX2+ expression (Gopee et al., 2024). The Dc forms through aggregation of pre-Dc cells and subsequently becomes encapsulated at the base of the invaginating follicle.

## Markers

- **FOXD1+, SOX2+**: Defining markers of the Dc, distinguishing it from pre-Dc (SOX2-low).
  > "In mice, transitional PDGFRA + FOXD1 + SOX2 low fibroblasts termed pre-Dc cells aggregate to form the Dc (FOXD1 + SOX2 + ), which abuts the epithelial hair placode"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **COL6A3, MFAP4, PTK7**: Genes for collagen fibril formation and cell adhesion expressed during Dc aggregation.
  > "Genes implicated in collagen fibril formation and cell adhesion (COL6A3, MFAP4 and PTK7) were expressed as the pre-Dc cells aggregated into the Dc"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **FAM3C, EFNB1**: Expressed by the Dc, mediating interactions with overlying placode.
  > "The Dc, for which formation is accompanied by invagination of the placode, expressed FAM3C and EFNB1, which were, respectively, predicted to interact with LAMP1 or CXADR and EPHB6 on the placode, and have been reported to promote cell migration and invasion"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

The Dc forms from aggregating pre-Dc cells and matures into the dermal papilla:

> "Following HF invagination, the Dc becomes encapsulated at its base as the Dp (NDP + , SOX2 + )"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Signalling

Interactions were conserved between prenatal skin and the SkO model:

> "the highlighted interactions were conserved between the mesenchymal and epithelial cells of the SkO model for corresponding stages during HF formation"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

> "Within the dermal compartment, we observed HF-specialized fibroblasts, the dermal condensate (Dc) and dermal papilla (Dp), from 12 PCW"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

The dermal condensate is the organizing center for early hair follicle morphogenesis: it instructs placode invagination and serves as the precursor to the dermal papilla.

## Structure / Morphology

The Dc is a compact mesenchymal aggregate located immediately beneath the hair placode, consisting of fibroblasts expressing collagen and cell adhesion genes.
