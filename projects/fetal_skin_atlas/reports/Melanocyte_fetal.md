# Melanocyte (Fetal) in Fetal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Source dataset: Gopee et al. (2024), prenatal skin, 7–17 PCW
Cell Ontology: [melanocyte of skin](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_1000458) (CL:1000458, exact match)

## Summary

The Melanocyte (fetal) cluster in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas represents differentiated pigment cells of fetal skin present from approximately 7 PCW through 17 PCW. Fetal skin melanocytes express the master regulatory transcription factor MITF and the full suite of melanogenic enzymes — TYR (tyrosinase), TYRP1, and DCT — as well as melanosomal structural proteins PMEL and MLANA. They are distinguished from melanoblasts in the same atlas by their mature melanogenic gene expression programme. A key finding from the prenatal-to-organoid comparison in Gopee et al. (2024) is that the melanocyte lineage achieves adult-like molecular maturation more rapidly than most other fetal skin cell types, with alignment to adult cell states detectable as early as 4 weeks of skin organoid (SkO) culture.

## Markers

### Melanogenic enzyme programme: TYR, TYRP1, DCT

Differentiated melanocytes are defined by expression of the core melanogenic enzymes: tyrosinase (TYR), tyrosinase-related protein 1 (TYRP1), and dopachrome tautomerase (DCT). These enzymes catalyse the sequential steps of melanin biosynthesis within melanosomes.

### Melanosomal structural proteins: PMEL and MLANA

Pre-melanosome protein (PMEL) and melanoma antigen recognised by T cells (MLANA, also known as Melan-A) are structural components of melanosomes and are characteristic of differentiated melanocytes. Their expression distinguishes melanocytes from melanoblasts in this atlas.

### Master regulator: MITF

Microphthalmia-associated transcription factor (MITF) is maintained in differentiated melanocytes and acts as a master regulator of the melanogenic programme, activating transcription of TYR, TYRP1, DCT, and PMEL.

### Rapid maturation toward adult state

> "By contrast, accelerated differentiation was observed in keratinocytes and melanocytes, with alignment to adult cell states seen as early as 4 weeks of SkO culture"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### Basal layer of the fetal epidermis and hair follicle outer root sheath, from ~7 PCW

Fetal melanocytes are annotated as a fine-grained cell type in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024), present throughout the 7–17 PCW sampling window. They reside in the basal layer of the interfollicular epidermis and in the outer root sheath of developing hair follicles, where they transfer melanin to adjacent keratinocytes.

## Function

### Melanin synthesis and transfer to keratinocytes

The primary function of fetal skin melanocytes is to synthesise melanin pigment within melanosomes and transfer it to surrounding basal keratinocytes, providing photoprotection to the developing fetal skin. This function relies on the full melanogenic enzyme programme (TYR, TYRP1, DCT) being active, distinguishing this cell from its melanoblast precursor.

### Early developmental maturation

The Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal-to-organoid comparison demonstrated that melanocytes and keratinocytes are among the first fetal skin lineages to achieve adult-like molecular states, suggesting that the transcriptional programme governing melanocyte identity is established early in fetal development and is robustly conserved across in vivo and organoid contexts.
