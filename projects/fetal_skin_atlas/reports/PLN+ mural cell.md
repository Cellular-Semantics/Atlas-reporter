# PLN+ Mural Cell (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [pericyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000669) (CL:0000669, broad match); new_term_needed

## Summary

PLN+ mural cells are a fine-grained mural cell subtype identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas (7–17 PCW), defined by expression of PLN (phospholamban), a small integral membrane protein that regulates the sarco/endoplasmic reticulum Ca2+-ATPase (SERCA) pump and thereby controls intracellular calcium cycling in smooth muscle and cardiac cells. In the context of fetal skin, PLN expression in this mural cell subtype likely reflects vascular smooth muscle cell-like contractile properties. The PLN+ subtype represents a distinct transcriptional state within the broad mural cell lineage, possibly corresponding to a more differentiated smooth muscle-like pericyte compared to the LMCD1+ subtype. Mural cells are present throughout gestation and show slow maturation toward adult cell states even after prolonged organoid culture.

## Markers

- **PLN (phospholamban)**: The defining marker of this mural cell subtype. Phospholamban is canonically expressed in cardiac and vascular smooth muscle, where it inhibits SERCA2a in its unphosphorylated state. Expression in fetal skin mural cells suggests a contractile, smooth muscle-like cell state.

- **PDGFRB**: Pan-pericyte/vascular smooth muscle marker; mediates PDGF-BB signalling for vessel stabilisation.

- **ACTA2 (αSMA)**: Expected in smooth muscle cell-like pericyte populations; part of the CL:0000669 definition.

- **RGS5**: Canonical pericyte marker included in the CL:0000669 definition.

- **CD146 (MCAM)**: Mural cell surface marker expressed by pericytes and smooth muscle cells.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

PLN+ mural cells are identified as a fine-grained annotation in the prenatal skin dataset (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024) within the broad mural cell category. Mural cells associate with the developing vasculature of the dermis and are present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Vascular Contractility and Mural Cell Maturation

PLN expression confers calcium-regulated contractile properties via SERCA pump modulation, suggesting that PLN+ mural cells contribute to regulation of vascular tone in fetal skin. Mural cells in fetal skin show a prolonged immature state relative to keratinocytes and melanocytes:

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## New Term Request

No existing CL term captures a mural cell subtype defined by PLN expression. The closest existing term is CL:0000669 pericyte (broad match). A new CL term is needed to represent this transcriptionally defined prenatal smooth muscle-like mural cell subtype marked by PLN expression.
