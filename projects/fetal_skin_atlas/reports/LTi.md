# Lymphoid Tissue Inducer (LTi) Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [CL:0011018](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011018) lymphoid tissue–inducer cell

## Summary

Lymphoid tissue inducer (LTi) cells are a subset of group 3 innate lymphoid cells (ILC3s) identified in the prenatal human skin atlas as a distinct fetal cell type within the ILC broad lineage. In the atlas, LTi cells are defined by their co-location with pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells in spatial transcriptomics and their predicted signalling interactions with pre-Dc cells via CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4 ligand-receptor pairs. This positions LTi cells within immune–mesenchymal crosstalk circuits during early skin morphogenesis. LTi cells are ontologically classified as a subtype of ILC3; they are distinguished from other non-LTi ILC3s by their canonical role in secondary lymphoid organ (SLO) formation during fetal development (Miller et al., 2018).

## Markers

No atlas-specific marker panel is reported for LTi cells in the supplementary data. General ILC3/LTi markers from the wider literature include:
- **RORγt** (RORC): master transcription factor for ILC3s including LTi cells
- **IL-7R** (CD127): regulates LTi pool size and lymph node organogenesis (Hernandez-Torres and Stehle, 2022)
- **CXCR4**: receptor mediating LTi interaction with pre-Dc cells via CXCL12 (Gopee et al., 2024)
- **DPP4**: additional receptor in CXCL12-DPP4 axis linking LTi to pre-Dc cells (Gopee et al., 2024)

## Location

Spatial deconvolution of Visium data revealed correlation between pre-dermal condensate cell abundance and LTi/ILC3 cell type abundances across skin-covered spots (Extended Data Fig. 1e,f). This co-localisation was confirmed by predicted ligand-receptor interactions:

> "Notably, lymphoid tissue inducer and ILC3 cells were also predicted to co-locate and interact with pre-Dc cells through ligand-receptor signals implicated in the regulation of cellular adhesion and migration (CXCL12-CXCR4 and CXCL12-DPP4)"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

## Function

LTi cells are canonically required for the formation of secondary lymphoid organs (SLOs) and isolated lymphoid tissues, including Peyer's patches, during fetal development:

> "Type 3 ILCs (ILC3s) were divided into two main groups: lymphoid tissue inducer (LTi) cells and non-LTi ILC3s. LTi cells are critical for the formation of secondary lymphoid organs (SLO) and isolated lymphoid tissues (i.e., Peyer's patches) during fetal development"

(Miller et al., 2018)

In the context of prenatal skin, the atlas data indicate a non-classical role: LTi cells appear to occupy microanatomical niches alongside pre-Dc mesenchymal cells, where they may support tissue morphogenesis rather than (or in addition to) classical lymphoid organogenesis:

> "These observations indicated that immune cells may occupy defined microanatomical niches where they have non-immune functions during early gestation."

(Gopee et al., 2024)

IL-7 signalling controls the pool size of IL-7R+ LTi cells that initiate lymphoid organogenesis, while retinoic acid regulates RORγt expression and secondary lymphoid organ size in fetal ILCs (Hernandez-Torres and Stehle, 2022).

> "IL-7 is known to play a key role in LN organogenesis by not only regulating the pool size of IL-7R+ LTi cells"

(Hernandez-Torres and Stehle, 2022)

Earlier developmental signatures (prior to 10–12 PCW) of macrophages, mast cells and NK cells suggest broader roles of innate lymphoid populations in tissue morphogenesis before acquisition of classical immune effector functions (Suo et al., 2022).
