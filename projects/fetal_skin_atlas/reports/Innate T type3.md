# Innate T Type3 Cells in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: no_direct_match — NTR filed (see cl_term_request.json)

## Summary

Innate T type3 cells are PLZF (ZBTB16)-expressing unconventional alpha-beta T cells with type 3 (IL-17-associated) effector potential, identified in the prenatal human skin atlas as a distinct T cell subset within the fetal T cell lineage. Like Innate T type1 cells, they arise via thymocyte-thymocyte (T-T) selection and are essentially absent in postnatal thymus, classifying them as developmental-specific unconventional T cells (Suo et al., 2022). A subset expresses TRGV8 or TRGV10 TCR gamma-chain usage. In fetal thymus, type 3 innate T cells and Tregs share both medullary microenvironments. Wave-like patterning of type 1 and type 3 effector fate commitment has been described among fetal gamma-delta thymocytes (Sanchez Sanchez et al., 2022), and neonatal IL-17-producing dermal gamma-delta T cells — the closest adult skin parallel — are required to prevent spontaneous atopic dermatitis (Spidale et al., 2020).

## Markers

- **ZBTB16 (PLZF)**: master transcription factor defining innate T cells; expressed across Innate T type3 and related unconventional T cell populations (Suo et al., 2022)
- **TRGV8 / TRGV10**: enriched TCR gamma-chain usage in the CD8AA and type 3 innate T cell gamma-delta subtype (Suo et al., 2022)
- **alpha-beta TCR**: Innate T type3 express a varied alpha-beta TCR repertoire; type 3 effector potential involves IL-17 production
- Single-cell TCRαβ sequencing was used in the atlas to resolve T cell subsets (Gopee et al., 2024)

## Location

T cells including Innate T type3 cells emerge in prenatal skin from approximately 10 PCW:

> "Innate immune cells, such as macrophages and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs), were present from early gestation, whereas B cells and T cells emerged later, accompanying thymus, bone marrow and spleen formation from around 10 PCW"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

Pre-dermal condensate cells co-located with lymphoid cells in spatial transcriptomics:

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

In the fetal thymus, Innate T type3 cells are located in thymic medullary microenvironments alongside Treg cells (Suo et al., 2022).

## Function

Innate T type3 cells are developmental-specific unconventional T cells with type 3 (IL-17/IL-22-associated) rapid effector potential. Their near-absence from postnatal thymus marks them as fetal-enriched:

> "Type 1 and 3 innate T cells were almost negligible in postnatal thymus, whereas CD8AA T cell abundance rebounded in pediatric age groups before a further decline in adulthood. Thus, type 1 and 3 innate T cells, but not CD8AA, appear to be developmentalspecific unconventional T cells."

(Suo et al., 2022)

> "Treg and type 3 innate T cells were located within both microenvironments."

(Suo et al., 2022)

Fetal gamma-delta thymocytes are committed to type 1, type 3, or type 2-like effector fates in a wave-like pattern depending on gestational age:

> "Mature fetal gammadelta thymocytes (both the Vgamma9Vdelta2 and nonVgamma9Vdelta2 subsets) are committed to either a type 1, a type 3 or a type 2-like effector fate displaying a wave-like pattern depending on gestation age"

(Sanchez Sanchez et al., 2022)

A large proportion of gamma-delta T cells of the type 3 innate T cell subtype show TRGV8 or TRGV10 TCR chain usage:

> "there was also a large proportion of gammadelta T cells, particularly those of CD8AA and type 3 innate T cell subtypes, expressing TRGV8 or TRGV10 instead"

(Suo et al., 2022)

In the post-natal skin context, IL-17-producing dermal gamma-delta T cells of neonatal origin provide a functional parallel, preventing spontaneous atopic dermatitis (Spidale et al., 2020). Innate-like T cells broadly present in the human fetus include populations with Th17 characteristics (Rackaityte and Halkias, 2020):

> "A number of innate-like T cells capable of Th1 cytokine production are present in the human fetus, including gammadelta T cells, invariant natural killer T (iNKT) cells, and mucosa-associated invariant T (MAIT) cells."

(Rackaityte and Halkias, 2020)

Direct characterisation of innate-type T cells in fetal skin has been limited by technical constraints:

> "Our ability to comment on innate-type T cells (i.e., MAIT, gd, and NK T cells) in fetal skin was also limited by existing hurdles in combining tetramers with CyTOF and by the total number of fetal skin leukocytes we had to analyze."

(Dhariwala et al., 2020)
