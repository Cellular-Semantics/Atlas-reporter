# Inner root sheath
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
CL mapping: CL:0002560 (inner root sheath cell) -- exact match  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

Inner root sheath (IRS) cells form the channel that surrounds and guides the growing hair shaft within the follicle. The IRS is composed of three concentric layers: Henle's layer, Huxley's layer, and the IRS cuticle. In the prenatal skin atlas, IRS cells were identified from 14 PCW as part of the hair follicle epithelial compartment (Gopee et al., 2024). IRS cells differentiate from matrix progenitors along the IRS trajectory and are the first follicular cells to undergo keratinization.

## Markers

- **IRS trajectory markers**: IRS cells are identified as part of the IRS differentiation trajectory that also involves placode, matrix, and cuticle/cortex cells.
  > "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

IRS cells differentiate along the IRS trajectory from POSTN+ basal cells:

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

IRS cells are present in the inner layer of the hair follicle, forming a channel between the outer root sheath and the hair shaft. They are first detected at 14 PCW in prenatal skin.

## Function

The IRS forms the structural channel that guides and shapes the growing hair shaft. Henle's layer keratinizes first, providing early structural support. The IRS cuticle interlocks with the hair shaft cuticle to anchor the hair during growth. Near the skin surface, the IRS degenerates to release the hair shaft.

## Disease Relevance

> "Genes harbouring mutations known to cause reduced hair growth (hypotrichosis) or abnormally shaped hair (for example, pili torti) (Supplementary Table 12) were expressed along the ORS/CL trajectory, IRS trajectory and hair fibroblast trajectory pseudotimes"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

The IRS consists of three concentric layers (from outside to inside): Henle's layer, Huxley's layer, and the IRS cuticle. These layers differentiate from matrix cells and undergo trichohyalin-mediated keratinization distinct from that of the hair shaft or interfollicular epidermis.
