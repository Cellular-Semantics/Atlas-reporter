# Differentiated Keratinocyte (c7) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [keratinocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000312) (CL:0000312, broad match — no exact CL term for differentiated KC subcluster)

## Summary

Cluster c7 is a differentiated keratinocyte (KC) state in adult human epidermis, one of four differentiated KC clusters (c3, c4, c6, c7) identified by Leiden clustering in Reynolds et al. (2021). The eight-cluster Leiden partition divides adult keratinocytes into undifferentiated (c1, c5), proliferating (c2), differentiated (c3, c4, c6, c7), and inflammatory differentiated (c8, "Differentiated KC*") states. Differentiated KCs express suprabasal keratins KRT1 and KRT10 and terminal differentiation genes (FLG, IVL, CNFN). Reynolds et al. (2021) describe two inferred differentiation trajectories from stem-cell-gene-enriched basal keratinocytes, distinguished by levels of lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1) and the transcription factors IRF1 and SOX9. Cluster c7 represents one of the transcriptionally distinct positions within these two suprabasal trajectories.

## Markers

> "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC* (cluster 8)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021), Fig. 4D legend

> "Proliferating keratinocytes (CDK1+,PCNA+) have lower expression of suprabasal cell transcripts e.g. KRT1, KRT10 that characterize differentiated keratinocytes"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Key markers of differentiated KCs:
- **KRT1, KRT10** — suprabasal keratins defining the differentiated state
- **FLG (filaggrin)**, **IVL (involucrin)**, **CNFN (cornifelin)** — terminal differentiation markers
- **ABCA12, CKAP4, CLIP1** — lamellar body-related transcripts enriched in one arm of differentiation

## Location

Cluster c7 keratinocytes are suprabasal cells of the adult interfollicular epidermis. The Human Protein Atlas immunofluorescence staining confirms spatially distinct suprabasal compartments for the marker proteins (KRT1, KRT10, IRF1, SOX9) used to characterise differentiated KC states in Reynolds et al. (2021).

## Function

### 1. Terminal epidermal differentiation

The differentiated KC clusters (c3, c4, c6, c7) collectively represent cells executing the cornification programme. Reynolds et al. (2021) define dual trajectories:

> "Force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses reveal dual inferred differentiation trajectories from the stem cell genes (TP63, PPP3CA and CAV1/2)-enriched basal keratinocytes into terminally differentiated keratinocytes expressing CNFN, FLG and IVL"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "One arm expresses high levels of lamellar body (LB)­related transcripts such as ABCA12, CKAP4 and CLIP1 that characterize late epidermal differentiation and the other arm lower levels of LB-related transcripts"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### 2. Transcription factor control of differentiation pathway

> "IRF1 and SOX9, transcription factors that are differentially expressed in the two pathways"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

IRF1 and SOX9 were confirmed by immunofluorescence in healthy adult skin to mark distinct cells in suprabasal layers, supporting the existence of two molecularly distinct differentiation routes within the differentiated KC compartment.

### 3. Lamellar body lipid loading and barrier maintenance

As reviewed by Liu et al. (2022), the LB-related transcript arm defined in Reynolds et al. (2021) represents cells specialised for loading ceramide-rich lipids into lamellar bodies for secretion into the stratum corneum:

> "force-directed graph (FDG) and PAGA analyses on scRNA-seq data defined two differentiation pathways of basal keratinocytes to suprabasal cells, with or without a high level of the lamellar body (LB)-related transcripts (glucosylceramide transporter ABCA12; cytoskeleton-associated protein 4, CKAP4; and CAP-Gly domain-containing linker protein 1, CLIP1), that characterized late epidermal differentiation in healthy skin"
>
> — Liu et al. (2022)

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) document proportional shifts in keratinocyte cluster composition in AD and psoriasis lesional skin. Clusters c3–c7 are the non-inflammatory differentiated states; their relative proportions differ between healthy and diseased skin, reflecting disease-driven epidermal reprogramming distinct from the c8 "Differentiated KC*" state defined by co-expression of ICAM1, TNF, and CCL20.
