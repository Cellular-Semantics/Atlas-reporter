# Companion layer
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
CL mapping: new_term_needed (no "companion layer cell" in CL; nearest: CL:0002559 hair follicle cell)  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

The companion layer (CL) is a single-cell layer situated between the outer root sheath and the inner root sheath of the hair follicle. In the prenatal skin atlas, companion layer cells were identified from 14 PCW as a distinct cell population within the hair follicle epithelial compartment (Gopee et al., 2024). CL cells differentiate along the ORS/CL trajectory from DPYSL2+ basal cells and are transcriptionally more similar to ORS than to the inner layer cells.

## Markers

- **CL annotation**: Identified as a distinct population in scRNA-seq.
  > "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

CL cells differentiate along the ORS/CL trajectory:

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

The companion layer is located between the outer root sheath and Henle's layer (the outermost layer of the inner root sheath), forming a boundary between the two major sheath compartments of the hair follicle.

## Function

The companion layer acts as a slippage plane that facilitates the upward movement of the inner root sheath and hair shaft relative to the stationary outer root sheath during hair growth. It is thought to play a role in the controlled desquamation of the IRS.

## Disease Relevance

> "Genes harbouring mutations known to cause reduced hair growth (hypotrichosis) or abnormally shaped hair (for example, pili torti) (Supplementary Table 12) were expressed along the ORS/CL trajectory, IRS trajectory and hair fibroblast trajectory pseudotimes"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

The companion layer is a single cell layer that encases Henle's layer of the IRS. It consists of flattened epithelial cells that are transcriptionally more similar to ORS cells than to IRS cells.
