# Cuticle/cortex
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Cell Ontology: [keratinized cell of hair follicle](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0010000) (CL:0010000, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

Cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex) represent the inner layers of the hair follicle that form the hair shaft proper. In the prenatal skin atlas, they are identified as a combined population from 14 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). These cells differentiate from matrix progenitors along the IRS trajectory and undergo terminal keratinization to produce the structural components of the hair fiber. The cuticle forms the protective outer surface of the hair shaft while the cortex constitutes its bulk, providing tensile strength through keratin intermediate filaments.

## Markers

- **Cuticle/cortex annotation**: Identified in scRNA-seq as part of the inner layers of the HF.
  > "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

Cuticle/cortex cells differentiate along the IRS trajectory from matrix progenitors:

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

Cuticle/cortex cells form the innermost concentric layers of the hair follicle, constituting the hair shaft itself. They are located internal to the IRS cuticle, with which they interlock.

## Function

The hair cuticle forms a protective multi-layered surface (approximately 6 cell layers thick) that provides physical integrity and chemical resistance to the hair shaft through cysteine-rich keratin crosslinks. The cortex comprises the bulk of the hair shaft, consisting of tightly packed keratin filaments and keratin-associated proteins organized into macrofibrils, providing tensile strength and flexibility.

## Disease Relevance

> "Genes harbouring mutations known to cause reduced hair growth (hypotrichosis) or abnormally shaped hair (for example, pili torti) (Supplementary Table 12) were expressed along the ORS/CL trajectory, IRS trajectory and hair fibroblast trajectory pseudotimes"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

The cuticle consists of overlapping, scale-like flattened cells that form the outermost layer of the hair shaft. The cortex is composed of elongated, fully keratinized cells that extrude their organelles during terminal differentiation and become filled with keratin filaments crosslinked by disulfide bonds.
