# Placode
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Cell Ontology: [hair follicle cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002559) (CL:0002559, broad match — no exact CL term; new term needed)  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

Hair follicle placodes are the earliest ectodermal progenitors of the hair follicle, representing a prenatal-specific cell state that is absent in established adult hair follicles (Gopee et al., 2024). In the prenatal skin atlas, placode cells were identified from 14 PCW alongside other hair follicle epithelial lineages. The placode arises from the budding of basal epidermal cells under the influence of WNT and EDA signalling, initiating hair follicle morphogenesis through crosstalk with the underlying dermal mesenchyme. Placode specification involves the loss of AGR2 expression and increased cellular migration in POSTN+ basal cells (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Markers

- **SHH**: Marks matrix cells that arise from the placode; used in the atlas to distinguish the matrix (SHH+) population.

- **WNT10B**: Known matrix marker upregulated along the IRS trajectory from placode cells.
  > "AGR2 was downregulated along the IRS trajectory, whereas known matrix markers such as SHH and WNT10B 25,26 were upregulated"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **AGR2 (loss)**: Downregulated as placode specification proceeds.

- **POSTN+ basal cell origin**: Placodes arise from POSTN+ basal cells via increased cellular migration.
  > "Our findings suggest that increased cellular migration in POSTN + basal cells may be involved in placode specification and dermal invagination."
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

The placode sits at a key branch point in hair follicle epithelial differentiation:

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Signalling

WNT and EDA signalling are critical for placode specification:

> "Similar signalling pathways were upregulated, including WNT and EDA for hair placode specification, bone morphogenetic protein (BMP) and noggin to inhibit hair formation in IFE cells, and PDGFA and TGFbeta signalling for HF down growth"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

The dermal condensate interacts with the placode via FAM3C and EFNB1:

> "The Dc, for which formation is accompanied by invagination of the placode, expressed FAM3C and EFNB1, which were, respectively, predicted to interact with LAMP1 or CXADR and EPHB6 on the placode, and have been reported to promote cell migration and invasion"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

Placode cells were identified in human prenatal skin from 14 PCW:

> "At 14-15 PCW, budding of basal cells (hair placode and germ cells) and elongation of HFs (hair pegs) were observed"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

The placode is the epithelial anlage of hair follicle morphogenesis and a prenatal-specific cell state:

> "We evaluated hair matrix cells, which arise from the epidermal placode, a prenatal-specific cell state absent in established adult HFs"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Disease Relevance

Genes causing hypotrichosis and abnormally shaped hair are expressed along trajectories involving placodes:

> "Genes harbouring mutations known to cause reduced hair growth (hypotrichosis) or abnormally shaped hair (for example, pili torti) (Supplementary Table 12) were expressed along the ORS/CL trajectory, IRS trajectory and hair fibroblast trajectory pseudotimes"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological description beyond identification as budding basal cells at the onset of hair follicle formation.
