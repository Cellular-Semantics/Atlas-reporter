# LE — Lymphatic Endothelium (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [dermis lymphatic vessel endothelial cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_2000011) (CL:2000011, exact match)

## CL Mapping

**CL:2000011** — dermis lymphatic vessel endothelial cell
- Match type: exact
- Rationale: LE (lymphatic endothelium) in the fetal skin atlas represents the mature lymphatic endothelial cell population in prenatal dermis. CL:2000011 is defined as "any endothelial cell of lymphatic vessel that is part of a dermis," precisely matching this skin-localised lymphatic endothelial cell state. It is a subclass of CL:0002138 (endothelial cell of lymphatic vessel), characterised by expression of Prox-1, VEGFR-3, and LYVE-1.

## Summary

LE (lymphatic endothelium) is a fine-grained cell annotation in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), classified under the broad "Lymphatic endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). The abbreviation is explicitly defined in the atlas:

> "LE, lymphatic endothelium"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

LE represents the mature lymphatic endothelial cell state in fetal dermis, distinct from the earlier "Early LE" population within the same broad category. Both populations are present throughout the 7–17 PCW gestational window. Lymphatic endothelial cells co-locate with macrophages in prenatal skin neurovascular microenvironments.

## Markers

Marker genes are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. Canonical lymphatic endothelial cell markers consistent with the CL:0002138 parent class include PROX1, LYVE1, PDPN (podoplanin), and FLT4 (VEGFR3). These markers define the mature lymphatic endothelial identity distinguishing LE from the progenitor Early LE population.

## Location

LE cells are localised to lymphatic vessels within the dermis of prenatal skin. Co-location with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments was confirmed by spatial analysis:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

LE cells are present throughout gestation as part of the mesoderm-derived endothelial compartment:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Lymphatic vessel maintenance and fluid homeostasis

As the mature lymphatic endothelial cell state in fetal skin, LE cells line functional lymphatic vessels in the dermis, regulating interstitial fluid drainage, lipid transport, and immune cell trafficking — functions characteristic of dermis lymphatic vessel endothelial cells.

### Immune cell interactions

Macrophage-endothelial crosstalk supports endothelial cell development broadly in prenatal skin. Immunodeficient organoids lacking macrophages show reduced endothelial heterogeneity:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Reciprocal ligand-receptor interactions support angiogenesis and chemotaxis:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
