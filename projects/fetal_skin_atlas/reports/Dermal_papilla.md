# Dermal papilla
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
CL mapping: CL:0000346 (hair follicle dermal papilla cell) -- exact match  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

The dermal papilla (Dp) is the permanent mesenchymal signalling center at the base of the hair follicle, derived from encapsulation of the dermal condensate following follicular invagination. In the prenatal skin atlas, dermal papilla cells were identified from 12 PCW and are characterized by NDP+ and SOX2+ expression (Gopee et al., 2024). The Dp regulates hair cycling throughout life and coordinates the differentiation of adjacent matrix cells through secretion of RSPO3 and WNT5A.

## Markers

- **NDP+, SOX2+**: Defining markers of the Dp.
  > "Following HF invagination, the Dc becomes encapsulated at its base as the Dp (NDP + , SOX2 + )"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **RSPO3, WNT5A**: Genes characterizing Dp formation that coordinate matrix cell differentiation.
  > "Formation of the Dp was characterized by genes (RSPO3 and WNT5A) (Extended Data Fig. 4f and Supplementary Table 7) that coordinate the differentiation of adjacent hair matrix cells"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Developmental Trajectory

The Dp derives from the dermal condensate which itself originates from HOXC5+ early fibroblasts via the hair fibroblast trajectory:

> "the first (hair fibroblast trajectory) formed hair-specialized fibroblasts (pre-Dc cells, the Dc and Dp) and the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Signalling

RSPO3 from the Dp interacts with LGR4/LGR6 on overlying matrix cells:

> "RSPO3 from the Dp was predicted to interact with LGR4 and LGR6 (Fig. 2g, Extended Data Fig. 5b and Supplementary Table 8) in overlying matrix cells to contribute to the proliferation of HF epithelial cells"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

> "Within the dermal compartment, we observed HF-specialized fibroblasts, the dermal condensate (Dc) and dermal papilla (Dp), from 12 PCW"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

The Dp is the permanent inductive mesenchymal component of the hair follicle. It regulates matrix cell proliferation and differentiation, controlling hair shaft size, shape, and growth. It also bears hair inductive potential and regulates the hair cycle.

## Structure / Morphology

The Dp is a fusiform cluster of specialized fibroblasts located at the base of the hair follicle bulb, encapsulated by the overlying matrix cells and rich in capillaries.
