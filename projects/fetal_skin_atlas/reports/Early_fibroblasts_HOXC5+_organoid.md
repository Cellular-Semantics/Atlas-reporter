# Early Fibroblasts HOXC5+ in Skin Organoid

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Tissue context: hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO)
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast of dermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002551) (CL:0002551, broad match)

## Summary

The Early fibroblasts HOXC5+ population represents the principal upper-dermal progenitor fibroblast state in both prenatal skin and the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO). Defined by expression of the HOX transcription factor HOXC5, these cells are located in the upper dermis and are most abundant before 11 PCW, positioning them as the dominant fibroblast progenitor at the earliest well-sampled stages of prenatal skin development. Trajectory analysis in Gopee et al. (2024) establishes HOXC5+ early fibroblasts as a common progenitor that bifurcates into two differentiation paths: a hair fibroblast trajectory producing pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc), dermal condensate (Dc), and dermal papilla (Dp) cells, and a dermal fibroblast trajectory producing WNT2+ and PEAR1+ fibroblasts. The SkO system recapitulates this progenitor population and its downstream differentiation, with the gene regulatory programs active during hair follicle fibroblast differentiation in SkO closely matching those in prenatal skin, providing a tractable model for studying human dermal progenitor biology.

## Markers

### Defining marker: HOXC5

HOXC5 is the defining marker for this early fibroblast progenitor population. HOX transcription factors confer positional and temporal identity to fibroblasts, and HOXC5 marks this population in the upper dermis before 11 PCW. Gopee et al. (2024) characterise its dual trajectory output:

> "HOXC5 + early fibroblasts (located in the upper dermis and abundant before 11 PCW) differentiated along two paths: the first (hair fibroblast trajectory) formed hair-specialized fibroblasts (pre-Dc cells, the Dc and Dp) and the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts"

— Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

HOXC5+ early fibroblasts are situated in the upper dermis of prenatal skin, where they are the predominant fibroblast type before 11 PCW (Extended Data Fig. 3c; Gopee et al., 2024). This upper-dermal positioning is consistent with their role as progenitors for both dermal papilla cells (which associate with the base of developing hair follicles) and the interfollicular dermis-associated fibroblast populations. The same population is present in the SkO, where it serves as the starting point for fibroblast differentiation trajectories that parallel those in prenatal skin.

## Developmental Trajectories

### Hair fibroblast trajectory

From the HOXC5+ progenitor, one differentiation path produces the specialised mesenchymal cells of the hair follicle: pre-Dc cells, the dermal condensate (Dc), and the dermal papilla (Dp). Dc formation begins from 12 PCW in prenatal skin (Gopee et al., 2024). The Dc is the instructive mesenchymal signalling centre that induces overlying epithelial cells to form the hair placode; its fate specification has been shown to precede morphological formation and to depend on placode progenitor signals (Mok et al., 2019). Dermal condensation involves Fgf20-primed cell cycle exit, cell motility, and aggregation (Biggs et al., 2018).

### Dermal fibroblast trajectory

The second path from HOXC5+ progenitors gives rise to WNT2+ fibroblasts and PEAR1+ fibroblasts, constituting the interfollicular dermal fibroblast lineage. These populations correspond to the papillary and reticular fibroblast lineages documented in mouse dermis (Driskell et al., 2013; Joost et al., 2019).

## Organoid Fidelity and Gene Regulatory Programs

A key finding of Gopee et al. (2024) is that the gene regulatory programs active during hair follicle fibroblast differentiation in SkO closely mirror those in prenatal skin:

> "we observed a high number of matching TFs across pseudotime, which indicated that there are similar activated gene regulatory programs between prenatal skin and SkO during HF fibroblast differentiation"

— Gopee et al. (2024)

Mismatches between SkO and prenatal skin are attributable to the different developmental origins of dermal cells: prenatal skin fibroblasts derive from trunk and limb mesoderm, whereas SkO fibroblasts derive from neural crest differentiation in the pluripotent stem cell protocol. This results in differences in anteroposterior HOX identity:

> "TFs that were mismatched across pseudotime or drove misalignment in early and late pseudotime (for example, HOXA7 and BARX1) were largely attributable to the different origins of dermal cells between prenatal skin (trunk and limb) and SkO (neural crest differentiation)"

— Gopee et al. (2024)

Transcriptional signatures of hair follicle cell states, including dermal papilla, have been characterised at high resolution in human adult hair follicles (Heitman et al., 2022), providing a reference framework for the downstream products of the HOXC5+ trajectory.

No CL term exists for an early multipotent upper-dermal progenitor fibroblast. CL:0002551 (fibroblast of dermis) is the most appropriate available term. CL:0020011 (hair follicle associated fibroblast) was considered but rejected because HOXC5+ cells are an upstream progenitor for both hair and non-hair fibroblast trajectories. A new CL term for this progenitor state may be warranted with further cross-dataset validation.
