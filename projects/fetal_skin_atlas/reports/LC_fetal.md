# Langerhans Cells in Prenatal Human Skin
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Scope: fetal  
CL mapping: CL:0000453 (Langerhans cell) -- exact match

## Summary

Langerhans cells (LCs) are epidermal-resident antigen-presenting cells identified as a distinct immune cell lineage ("LC") in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024). In the atlas, LCs are grouped with dendritic cells among the myeloid immune compartment and are distinguished from MHCII+ macrophages by their expression of CD207 (langerin) and CD1A. LC precursors first colonize the developing human epidermis as early as 6-7 post-conception weeks (PCW), initially expressing only primitive markers (CD45, HLA-DR, CD1c), with the canonical LC markers CD207/langerin, CD1a, and Birbeck granules appearing progressively after 11 weeks estimated gestational age (EGA) (Schuster et al., 2014; Botting & Haniffa, 2020). In mice, LCs derive from a dual embryonic origin -- yolk sac macrophages followed by fetal liver monocytes -- and subsequently self-renew locally in the epidermis (Hoeffel et al., 2012). Whether the same dual origin applies in humans remains an active area of investigation. The early establishment of LCs during embryogenesis, rather than postnatal seeding, suggests they may play functional roles in the developing skin beyond classical immune surveillance.

## Markers

### CD207 (Langerin)
CD207 encodes Langerin, a C-type lectin receptor that is the defining marker of Langerhans cells and is responsible for the formation of Birbeck granules, a unique ultrastructural hallmark of LCs. In prenatal skin, CD207 expression is acquired progressively, becoming detectable only after 11 weeks EGA:

> "The distinguishing features of LCs in adult human epidermis, namely Birbeck granules, CD207/langerin, and CD1a are detectable only after 11 weeks EGA."
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2014)

### CD1A
CD1A is an MHC class I-like molecule involved in lipid antigen presentation. Together with CD207, it serves as a key identifier of mature LCs. Like langerin, CD1a expression is acquired late during fetal LC maturation:

> "LCs go through morphological and phenotypic changes within skin; they grow larger, more dendritic, gain expression of Langerin and CD1a, and form Birbeck granules in a stepwise manner."
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

### EPCAM
EPCAM (epithelial cell adhesion molecule) is highly expressed on LCs in the epidermis and mediates their close integration among keratinocytes. LCs also express the C-type lectin receptor Langerin alongside other typical DC markers:

> "LCs express the C-type lectin receptor Langerin, which induces the formation of Birbeck granules and other typical dendritic cell (DC) markers such as MHC class II molecules, CD11c, CD1a, and CD1c."
>
> -- Bellmann et al. (2020)

### Early Precursor Markers
Before acquiring the definitive LC phenotype, fetal LC precursors express CD45 (PTPRC), HLA-DR, CD1c, and ATPase. These early markers identify LC precursors in the embryonic epidermis from 7-9 weeks EGA:

> "During human skin development, their precursors are first identified in the epidermis at 7-9 weeks estimated gestational age (EGA), based on the expression of CD45 (PTPRC), HLA-DR, CD1c and ATPase."
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2014)

### Additional Markers
BMP7 and TGF-beta are key cytokines involved in LC differentiation and homeostasis, with BMP7 especially expressed in prenatal basal epidermal layers:

> "BMP7 is also involved in LC differentiation, especially expressed in the prenatal but also adult basal epidermal layers."
>
> -- Bellmann et al. (2020)

## Location

### Prenatal Epidermis
In the atlas, LCs are identified within the epidermal compartment of prenatal human skin spanning 7-17 PCW. LC precursors seed the epidermis early during the first trimester and progressively mature in situ:

> "LCs take up residence as early as 6 PCW."
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

> "Langerhans cells (LCs) are specialized antigen-presenting cells residing in the epidermis of the skin in close contact with neighboring keratinocytes."
>
> -- Bellmann et al. (2020)

### Stepwise Epidermal Maturation
LCs undergo substantial morphological changes within the developing epidermis. They become uniformly dendritic early in development but continue to mature phenotypically through the second trimester:

> "LCs are uniformly dendritic from 8 PCW, while surface expression and Birbeck granule formation continue to develop into the second trimester."
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

### Precursor Heterogeneity
The embryonic epidermis contains a heterogeneous LC precursor pool, contrasting with the phenotypic uniformity observed in adult skin:

> "Not all HLA-DR-positive epidermal cells in embryonic human skin express the LC markers CD1a, CD1c or CD207/langerin, prompted us to hypothesize that during ontogeny the LC phenotype is acquired in the epidermis."
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2014)

### Microenvironmental Context
In the atlas, LCs are grouped with DCs in the UMAP visualization of broad immune cell lineages. While macrophages were shown to co-locate with endothelial and neural cells in specific microenvironments, the atlas did not identify a specific LC-associated microenvironment, consistent with their dispersed distribution within the basal and suprabasal epidermis.

## Function

### Immune Surveillance and Antigen Presentation
Even during prenatal development, LC precursors and immature LCs possess functional antigen-presenting capacity. Embryonic APCs in human skin can phagocytose antigen, upregulate costimulatory molecules, and stimulate T cells. TGF-beta1 production in the developing skin precedes and promotes LC maturation:

> "transforming growth factor beta1 production precedes Langerin and CD1a expression on CD45+CD1c+ Langerhans cell (LC) precursors"
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2009)

> "CD45+HLA-DRhighCD1c+ dendritic cells (DCs) are already present in the epidermis and dermis at 9 wk estimated gestational age (EGA)"
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2009)

### Developmental Ontogeny
LCs have a unique embryonic origin that distinguishes them from most other dendritic cell subsets. In mice, they arise sequentially from yolk sac progenitors and then fetal liver monocytes, with the latter predominating in the adult:

> "Langerhans cell precursors initially arise from yolk sac progenitors, but are later superseded by fetal liver monocytes."
>
> -- Hoeffel et al. (2012)

> "During embryonic development, LCs arise from yolk sac macrophages and fetal liver monocytes and later restore their numbers by local self-renewal in the absence of inflammation."
>
> -- Bellmann et al. (2020)

This embryonic origin, combined with their capacity for local self-renewal independent of bone marrow input, makes LCs unique among DCs. Their early establishment during embryogenesis suggests non-immune developmental functions, consistent with the broader theme of the atlas that innate immune cells contribute to skin morphogenesis.

### TGF-beta and BMP7 Dependence
LC homeostasis and differentiation depend critically on TGF-beta, produced by keratinocytes and LCs in an autocrine loop:

> "LC homeostasis crucially depends on the cytokine TGF-b that is produced by keratinocytes and LCs, acting in an autocrine feedback loop."
>
> -- Bellmann et al. (2020)

This cytokine dependence connects LC maturation to the epidermal differentiation program that is a central feature of prenatal skin development.
