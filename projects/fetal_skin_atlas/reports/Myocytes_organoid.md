# Myocyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [muscle cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000187) (CL:0000187, broad match)

## Summary

Myocytes are differentiated muscle cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the skin, myocytes represent mature smooth muscle cells associated with the arrector pili muscle of hair follicles and vascular smooth muscle. The SkO model supports myocyte differentiation as part of the dermal mural cell compartment, which shows a low probability of correspondence to adult skin states even after extended culture.

## Markers

Myocytes in skin express smooth muscle markers including ACTA2 (smooth muscle actin), TAGLN (transgelin/SM22), MYH11, and CNN1 (calponin).

## Atlas Context

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

In prenatal skin and the SkO, myocytes are located in the dermal compartment, forming the arrector pili muscle associated with hair follicles and contributing to the vascular smooth muscle layer.

## Function

Myocytes in skin provide contractile function for the arrector pili muscle (enabling hair erection) and for vascular smooth muscle (regulating blood flow). Their immature differentiation state in the SkO suggests that full muscle maturation requires additional developmental time or signals.
