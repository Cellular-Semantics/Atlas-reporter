Scope: fetal

## CL Mapping

**CL:0000669** — pericyte
- Match type: exact
- Rationale: Pericytes in the fetal skin atlas are annotated as a fine-grained population under the broad "Mural cell" category. CL:0000669 is defined as an elongated, contractile cell wrapped about precapillary arterioles outside the basement membrane, present in capillaries where proper adventitia and muscle layer are absent. It is a subclass of mural cell (CL:0008034), directly matching the atlas broad category. Canonical markers include PDGFRB, RGS5, NG2, CD146, and ACTA2.

## Summary

Pericytes represent a fine-grained mural cell population in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), annotated under the broad "Mural cell" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). They co-locate with endothelial and neural cells in vascular microenvironments throughout gestation. LYVE1+ macrophages interact with vascular cells through PDGF signalling. A notable finding from skin organoid (SkO) comparison is that mural cells, including pericytes, had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states even after 19 weeks of culture (Extended Data Fig. 2d, Supplementary Table 5), indicating that their full developmental maturation in vivo requires signals absent from the organoid system.

## Markers

Marker genes for Pericytes are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. Canonical markers consistent with CL:0000669 include **PDGFRB** (PDGFRβ), **RGS5**, **ACTA2** (smooth muscle actin), **CSPG4** (NG2), **MCAM** (CD146), and **DESMIN**. PDGF receptor interactions (PDGFRα and PDGFRβ) are highlighted in the atlas as predicted signalling axes between macrophages and vascular cells in prenatal skin:

> "LYVE1+ macrophages co-located with WNT2+ fibroblasts and were predicted to interact through platelet-derived growth factors (PDGFs) and corresponding receptors (PDGFRα and PDGFRβ) expressed on fibroblasts"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

PDGFRβ is a key pericyte marker and the same PDGF signalling axis is implicated in macrophage-pericyte interactions in vascular microenvironments.

## Location

Pericytes are localised to the walls of small blood vessels (capillaries and postcapillary venules) in the prenatal dermis. They are present throughout the 7–17 PCW gestational window:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Spatial deconvolution placed mural cells in vascular microenvironments co-localising with endothelial cells:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Vascular support and microvascular regulation

As mural cells ensheathing microvessels, pericytes regulate capillary diameter, blood flow, and endothelial cell proliferation through direct physical contact and paracrine signalling.

### Interactions with macrophage subsets

Macrophage subsets were predicted to co-locate with vascular cells including mural cells in distinct tissue microenvironments:

> "macrophage subsets (Extended Data Fig. 7e,f) were predicted to co-locate with fibroblasts, neural cells and vascular cells in distinct tissue microenvironments in early gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

### Immune-dependent maturation

Full maturation of pericytes toward adult-equivalent cell states requires in vivo signals not replicated in organoid cultures:

> "immune cells are required to fully recapitulate in vivo endothelial cell development"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
