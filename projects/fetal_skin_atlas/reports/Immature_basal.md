# Immature Basal Keratinocyte (Immature basal)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [basal cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002187) (CL:0002187, broad match)

## Summary

Immature basal keratinocytes are an early fetal epidermal cell population identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas. These cells reside in the basal layer of the interfollicular epidermis (IFE) and are present from 7 PCW, decreasing after 11 PCW during the transition from embryonic to fetal skin (Gopee et al., 2024). They represent a developmentally early basal keratinocyte state that co-expresses canonical basal keratins (KRT5, KRT14) alongside embryonic-associated keratins (KRT18, KRT17, KRT19), distinguishing them from adult basal cells. Functionally, immature basal cells serve as the early progenitor pool of the developing epidermis before mature basal (DPYSL2+) cells emerge.

## Markers

### KRT5 and KRT14

KRT5 and KRT14 are the canonical basal keratinocyte markers, forming the type II/type I keratin pair expressed by proliferative progenitor cells in the stratum basale:

> "Proliferative progenitor cells in the epidermal basal layer and the outer root sheath are rich in dimerized KRT5 and KRT14, which provide mechanical support and cytoprotection in the basal cells."
>
> -- Rosendahl et al. (2021)

> "KRT5 and KRT14 demarking basal epithelial cells are associated with physiological keratinocyte cell proliferation"
>
> -- Yang et al. (2017)

### Embryonic keratins: KRT18, KRT17, KRT19

Fetal basal keratinocytes uniquely co-express keratins absent from adult interfollicular epidermis:

> "Expression of keratin 14 (K14, a marker for basal keratinocytes) and K15 (which is enriched in stable basal cells) was similar in fetal and adult skin. In contrast, expression of K18, K17, and K19 was seen in the basal layer of fetal epidermis, but not in adult interfollicular epidermal keratinocytes."
>
> -- Tan et al. (2014)

> "KRT5 and KRT14 are known to appear shortly before the onset of epithelial stratification, whereas KRT19 is expressed in basal lamina propria cells of fetal skin."
>
> -- Ma et al. (2025)

K17 expression in the fetal basal layer is transient, peaking during mid-gestation:

> "beginning at e14.5 d and firmly established by e16.5 d, the basal layer of embryonic epidermis expresses K17. Between e16.5 d and birth, the staining for K17 in the basal layer as well as the periderm is gradually lost."
>
> -- McGowan and Coulombe (1998)

## Location

### Fetal interfollicular epidermis

Immature basal cells are positioned in the basal layer of the early fetal epidermis:

> "Immature IFE cells, including periderm, immature basal and immature suprabasal cells, were present from 7 PCW and decreased after 11 PCW, during the transition from embryonic to fetal skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Supplementary Table 6 of the atlas shows immature basal cells detected in early gestation samples (e.g., 58 periderm and 9 immature basal cells at 7 PCW in donor F32).

## Function

### Epidermal progenitor pool

Immature basal keratinocytes serve as the early proliferative progenitor compartment for the developing epidermis. Fetal basal cells are already programmed for differentiation even before full stratification occurs:

> "fetal basal keratinocytes already are programmed to form the granular layer and incomplete stratum corneum, even when isolated from a stage prior to formation of these layers."
>
> -- Haake and Cooklis (1997)

These cells are replaced by mature basal populations (DPYSL2+ basal) as the epidermis stratifies after 11 PCW.

### Mesenchymal-epithelial signalling

The atlas identifies immature basal cells as the "early" epithelial partner in CellPhoneDB-predicted mesenchymal-epithelial interactions during prenatal hair follicle development (Gopee et al., 2024).
