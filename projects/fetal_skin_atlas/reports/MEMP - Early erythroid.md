# MEMP - Early Erythroid in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [megakaryocyte-erythroid progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000050) (CL:0000050, broad match -- MEMP includes mast cell potential not captured by CL MEP; this is an erythroid-biased substate)

## Summary

MEMP - Early erythroid is a cell state identified in the prenatal human skin atlas (Gopee et al., 2024) representing an erythroid-biased subpopulation within the megakaryocyte-erythroid-mast cell progenitor (MEMP) compartment. The MEMP was first characterized at the single-cell level in human fetal liver haematopoiesis by Popescu et al. (2019), who demonstrated that a shared MEMP population lies downstream of HSC/MPPs and gives rise to erythroid, megakaryocyte, and mast cell lineages. In the prenatal skin atlas, this population is subdivided into erythroid-biased (MEMP - Early erythroid) and megakaryocyte-biased (MEMP - Megak) substates, reflecting early lineage commitment. The presence of MEMPs in developing skin was independently confirmed by Suo et al. (2022), who detected megakaryocyte/erythroid progenitors in prenatal skin and spleen.

## Markers

Popescu et al. (2019) identified transcription factors dynamically modulated during erythroid specification from the MEMP:

> "Genes dynamically modulated in the specification of erythroid, megakaryocyte and mast cell lineages are distinct: TAL1 and KLF1 in erythroid; F11R, PBX1 and MEIS1 in megakaryocyte; and CPA3 and MS4A2 in mast cells."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

- **TAL1** (SCL): A basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor essential for erythroid specification from the MEMP.
- **KLF1** (EKLF): An erythroid Kruppel-like factor that drives erythroid lineage commitment and activates globin gene expression.

These markers distinguish the erythroid-biased MEMP from the megakaryocyte-biased and mast cell-biased states.

## Location

### Prenatal skin

MEMP - Early erythroid cells are present in prenatal human skin between 7-17 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). They belong to the haematopoietic progenitor compartment of the atlas. Spatial transcriptomics identified a "Myeloid/Megakaryocyte-erythroid enriched" microenvironment (ME2) in prenatal skin containing these progenitor populations.

> "Megakaryocytes, erythroid cells, mast cells and MEMP, are present in skin."
>
> -- Popescu et al. (2019)

### Fetal liver

The MEMP compartment is primarily studied in the fetal liver, where it is the main source of erythroid, megakaryocyte, and mast cell production during development (Popescu et al., 2019).

## Function

### Erythroid lineage commitment

The MEMP - Early erythroid state represents an early stage of commitment from the shared MEMP toward the erythroid lineage. These cells express the transcriptional programs (TAL1, KLF1) that drive differentiation into erythroid progenitor cells, which subsequently mature through erythroblast stages to produce red blood cells (Popescu et al., 2019). In the fetal context, this contributes to the production of erythrocytes bearing embryonic and fetal hemoglobins.

### Extramedullary haematopoiesis

The presence of erythroid-biased MEMPs in prenatal skin suggests that this organ can support early stages of erythroid commitment outside the primary haematopoietic organs (Suo et al., 2022).

## Structure / Morphology

No specific morphological descriptions of MEMP - Early erythroid cells in prenatal skin were found in the traversed literature.
