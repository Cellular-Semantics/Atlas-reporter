# POSTN+ Basal Keratinocytes in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid

## Summary

POSTN+ basal keratinocytes are a specialised epidermal population identified in both prenatal skin and the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model (Gopee et al., 2024). Defined by expression of periostin (POSTN), these cells represent a key progenitor state from which hair follicle differentiation proceeds along two distinct trajectories: the ORS/CL trajectory (producing outer root sheath and companion layer cells) and the IRS trajectory (producing placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex, and inner root sheath cells). POSTN+ basal cells are present throughout gestation in prenatal skin and are faithfully recapitulated in the SkO model, where their differentiation paths and gene expression dynamics are conserved.

## Markers

- **POSTN** (periostin): The defining marker of this basal keratinocyte subpopulation (Supplementary Table 3).
- **KRT14**: Basal keratinocyte marker, co-expressed in POSTN+ basal cells.
- **AGR2**: Expressed in POSTN+ basal cells; downregulated along the IRS trajectory (Extended Data Fig. 4b,c; Supplementary Table 7).

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### In the SkO model

POSTN+ basal cells reside in the basal epidermal layer of the SkO, positioned at the interface between the epidermis and dermis. They are particularly enriched at sites of hair follicle initiation.

### In prenatal skin

POSTN+ basal cells are present throughout gestation, unlike immature basal cells which decrease after 11 PCW or DPYSL2+ basal cells which increase after 11 PCW (Fig. 2b, Extended Data Fig. 3b).

## Function

### Hair follicle lineage specification

POSTN+ basal cells serve as the critical branch point for hair follicle epithelial differentiation. Their differentiation along the ORS/CL trajectory involves upregulation of genes such as BARX2 and SOX9 (associated with outer root sheath differentiation), while the IRS trajectory involves upregulation of matrix markers such as SHH and WNT10B (Gopee et al., 2024).

> "AGR2 was downregulated along the IRS trajectory, whereas known matrix markers such as SHH and WNT10B were upregulated"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

### Cell migration and placode formation

Loss of AGR2 expression along the IRS trajectory is functionally significant. AGR2 functions in the assembly of cysteine-rich receptors enriched in hair follicles, and its downregulation promotes cell migration (Martisova et al., 2022).

> "increased cellular migration in POSTN + basal cells may be involved in placode specification and dermal invagination"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
