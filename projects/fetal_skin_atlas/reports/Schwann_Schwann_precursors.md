# Schwann/Schwann Precursors (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [Schwann cell precursor](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002375) (CL:0002375, broad match -- annotation likely encompasses both precursors and immature Schwann cells)

## Summary

Schwann/Schwann precursors represent an early developmental stage in the Schwann cell lineage identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas. This annotation likely encompasses Schwann cell precursors (SCPs) and early immature Schwann cells, which are multipotent neural crest-derived glial progenitors. SCPs are distinguished from more mature Schwann cells by the absence of a basal lamina and their dependence on axon-derived neuregulin 1 type III for survival (Jessen and Mirsky, 2019). In the atlas, these cells appear in the fetal skin dataset (7-17 PCW) and are also represented in the organoid model. They form part of the neural cell compartment enriched in early gestation and contribute to the establishment of the peripheral nervous system in developing skin.

## Markers

- **SOX10**: Expressed from the neural crest stage onward, essential for Schwann cell specification.

  > "Specification of neural crest cells into the Schwann cell lineage is instructed by neuregulin 1 (Shah et al., 1994) and requires the transcription factor Sox10, which induces the expression of the neuregulin receptor ErbB3 (Britsch et al., 2001;Prasad et al., 2011) and of the early determinants of Schwann cell precursors fatty acid binding protein 7 (Fabp7) and myelin protein zero (P0)"
  >
  > -- Jacob et al. (2014)

- **FABP7 (BFABP/BLBP)**: An early glial marker whose expression begins at the Schwann cell precursor stage.

  > "Schwann cell precursors are specified from neural crest cells and represents the earliest glial marker . From the immature stage onwards, Schwann cells transiently express Sox2, an HMG domain transcription factor distantly related to Sox10"
  >
  > -- Finzsch et al. (2010)

- **MPZ (P0)**: Expressed at low levels from the SCP stage, increasing during maturation.

- **NGFR/p75NTR**: Neurotrophin receptor expressed in neural crest cells and SCPs.

- **ErbB3**: Neuregulin receptor required for SCP survival.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, neural cells are enriched in early gestation:

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm, which constitutes the first skin permeability barrier 2 , were enriched in early gestation, whereas suprabasal epidermal and HF cells were mainly observed in later gestation"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

These precursors reside within developing peripheral nerves in the dermal compartment and co-locate with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments.

## Function

### Glial Progenitor Function

Schwann cell precursors are multipotent progenitors that give rise to myelinating and non-myelinating Schwann cells, and can also generate melanocytes, parasympathetic neurons, and endoneurial fibroblasts.

> "The conversion of Schwann cell precursors to Schwann cells is controlled by transcription factors such as AP2α, Zeb2, and Notch, and extracellular signals including endothelin and axon-associated neuregulin, which is essential for precursor survival"
>
> -- Jessen and Mirsky (2019)

### Interaction with Macrophages

> "These findings suggest that prenatal skin macrophages may support the establishment of the skin peripheral nervous system during early gestation, as previously reported in mouse skin"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
