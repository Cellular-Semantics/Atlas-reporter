# Peridermal Cell (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [peridermal cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000078) (CL:0000078, exact match)

## Summary

Peridermal cells form the periderm, the first skin permeability barrier in embryonic development, identified in both prenatal skin and the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020). In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, periderm is classified as an immature IFE cell state enriched in early gestation and decreasing after 11 PCW. The periderm was observed sloughing from 11 PCW in prenatal skin, coinciding with the transition from embryonic to fetal skin and the onset of epidermal stratification.

## Markers

Peridermal cells are distinguished by expression of specific keratins (KRT17, KRT19) and lack of mature cornification markers.

## Location

The periderm forms the outermost layer of the embryonic epidermis, directly contacting the amniotic fluid. In the SkO, peridermal cells occupy the outer epithelial surface.

## Function

The periderm serves as a transient protective barrier in embryonic skin, preventing adhesion between developing epithelial surfaces and maintaining the integrity of the embryonic epidermis during development. It is shed as the stratified cornified epidermis matures.
