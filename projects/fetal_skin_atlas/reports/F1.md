# Fibroblast 1 (F1) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [skin fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002620) (CL:0002620, broad match — no exact CL term for this dominant homeostatic fibroblast subtype)

## Summary

F1 corresponds to Fb1 in Reynolds et al. (2021), the dominant fibroblast population in healthy adult human dermis. Reynolds et al. identify three fibroblast subsets in adult skin, all expressing extracellular matrix (ECM)-related genes including MMP2, COL1A1, COL1A2, and NT5E (encoding CD73). Fb1 is by far the most abundant, with Fb2 and Fb3 representing minor subpopulations in healthy skin. F1 therefore represents the predominant homeostatic dermal fibroblast state, responsible for maintaining the structural integrity and composition of the dermal ECM. Independent single-cell studies (Sole-Boldo et al., 2020) confirm the multi-subpopulation structure of human dermal fibroblasts with spatially distinct functional annotations, consistent with the Fb1/Fb2/Fb3 classification.

- **MMP2** — matrix metalloproteinase 2; ECM remodelling protease
- **COL1A1** — collagen type I alpha 1 chain; major structural ECM component
- **COL1A2** — collagen type I alpha 2 chain; major structural ECM component

## Location

F1 cells are the dominant fibroblast population distributed throughout the adult dermis. Dermal fibroblasts occupy both the papillary dermis (superficial, beneath the epidermis) and the reticular dermis (deep), with distinct subpopulation enrichments at each layer. F1, as the most abundant subtype in healthy skin, likely spans both compartments and represents the baseline structural fibroblast state of the adult dermis.

## Function

### 1. Structural ECM production and maintenance

F1/Fb1 fibroblasts are the primary producers of the structural dermis ECM. High expression of COL1A1 and COL1A2 drives the synthesis of type I collagen, the most abundant structural protein of the dermis, which provides tensile strength and scaffold for cellular organisation. MMP2 expression enables ECM remodelling through collagen and gelatin degradation, maintaining ECM homeostasis.

### 2. CD73-mediated immunomodulation

NT5E (CD73) catalyses the conversion of AMP to adenosine, a potent immunosuppressive molecule. Fibroblast-derived adenosine can suppress T cell activation and dampen local inflammatory responses, positioning F1 fibroblasts as participants in dermal immune homeostasis.

### 3. Homeostatic tissue maintenance

As the dominant fibroblast subset in healthy skin, F1 is the primary driver of steady-state dermal homeostasis — maintaining tissue architecture, ECM composition, and providing structural support to vascular and neural networks.

## Disease relevance

Reynolds et al. (2021) report that Fb2/F2 fibroblasts are significantly enriched in inflammatory skin disease (psoriasis and atopic dermatitis), with upregulation of chemokines CXCL12 and CCL19. By contrast, Fb1/F1 represents the dominant homeostatic state in healthy skin. The relative depletion of F1 in disease — through proportional shift toward F2 expansion — indicates a compositional remodelling of the dermal fibroblast compartment during skin inflammation, analogous to the VE1-to-VE3 shift in the vascular endothelium.
