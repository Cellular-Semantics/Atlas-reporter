# Neuron Progenitors SPP1+ (Organoid)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: organoid
Cell Ontology: [neural progenitor cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0011020) (CL:0011020, broad match)

## Summary

Neuron progenitors SPP1+ (organoid) are the skin organoid (SkO) counterpart to the fetal SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitors, identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas as a fine-grained neural cell annotation in SkO derived from human embryonic stem (ES) cells and induced pluripotent stem (iPS) cells. The SkO model closely recapitulates in vivo prenatal skin cell states more than adult cell states; neural cells including neuron progenitors are present in the organoid, though the absence of immune cells in SkO affects the full range of neural differentiation signals that macrophages provide in vivo. SPP1 (osteopontin) marks this proliferating neural progenitor state, which corresponds to the fetal SPP1+ proliferating neuron progenitor population identified in prenatal skin (7–17 PCW).

## Markers

- **SPP1 (osteopontin, secreted phosphoprotein 1)**: The defining marker of this organoid neural progenitor subtype, paralleling its expression in the fetal counterpart. Marks actively proliferating progenitors that contribute to the cutaneous peripheral nervous system.

- **Ki67 (MKI67)**: Proliferation marker expected in this actively dividing progenitor population.

- **SOX2**: Neural progenitor transcription factor; maintains progenitor identity.

- **NES (Nestin)**: Intermediate filament characteristic of neural progenitor cells.

## Location

### Skin Organoid

SPP1+ neuron progenitors are identified in SkO cultures derived from ES/iPS cells (Gopee et al., 2024). The SkO model recapitulates fetal skin cell states, including neural cells, more closely than adult skin:

> "Broadly, cell states were conserved among SkO, prenatal and adult skin, but SkO cell states matched prenatal skin more closely than adult skin across culture duration (Extended Data Fig. 2c,d and Supplementary Table 5). However, the tempo of differentiation varied across the distinct skin cell lineages. Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5). By contrast, accelerated differentiation was observed in keratinocytes and melanocytes, with alignment to adult cell states seen as early as 4 weeks of SkO culture (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

Neural cells were present in SkO even in the absence of immune cells:

> "Consistent with this hypothesis, we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Neural Progenitor Proliferation in Organoid

The organoid SPP1+ neuron progenitors expand the neural cell pool in the SkO model, recapitulating aspects of neural progenitor activity seen in fetal skin. In fetal skin, this progenitor pool is supported by macrophage-derived trophic signals via the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1); in the immunodeficient SkO model, these signals are absent.

### Correspondence to Fetal Skin

Neural cells including SPP1+ progenitors in SkO align with their prenatal skin counterparts rather than adult cell states, establishing their utility as a model for early neural development in skin:

> "Among ectoderm-derived cells, neural cells and the periderm, which constitutes the first skin permeability barrier 2 , were enriched in early gestation, whereas suprabasal epidermal and HF cells were mainly observed in later gestation (Fig. 1c, Extended Data Fig. 1d and Supplementary Table 4)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
