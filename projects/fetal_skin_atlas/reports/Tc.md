# Cytotoxic T cell (CD8+ T cell, Tc)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: adult

## Summary
Cytotoxic T cells (Tc), also known as CD8+ T cells, are a major effector lymphocyte population characterized by their cytolytic activity and expression of CD8A and CD8B. In adult human skin, Tc cells are enriched in the stroma and peripheral regions of tissue sections and play a key role in immune surveillance, pathogen response, and the local execution of cytotoxic and inflammatory effector programs. Marker genes defining this population include CD8A, CD8B, GZMA, GZMK, and PRF1, all reflecting the cell’s cytotoxic machinery and interferon-gamma secretion. Tc cells have critical roles both in tissue homeostasis and in disease, notably in inflammatory skin conditions and host defense (Supplementary Table S2–S5, Marella et al., 2025; Dhariwala et al., 2020; Szabo et al., 2019).

## Markers

Tc cells in adult skin are defined by the following markers:

- **CD8A and CD8B**: Core surface markers denoting the CD8+ lineage and supporting MHC class I recognition, enabling cytotoxic responses (Supplementary Table S2, Marella et al., 2025).
- **GZMA, GZMK, and PRF1**: Genes encoding granzyme A, granzyme K, and perforin 1, respectively. These are classic cytotoxic effector molecules—perforin forms pores in target cells, with granzymes mediating target cell apoptosis (Supplementary Table S3, Marella et al., 2025; Gibson et al., 2024; Papadimitriou et al., 2023). 
- **Additional context markers**: In inflamed or diseased skin, Tc cells may express CD103 (ITGAE), indicating tissue residency (Gibson et al., 2024), and CLA or CCR10, which promote skin homing (Gao et al., 2021; Torok et al., 2019).

## Location

### Adult Skin

- Tc cells are enriched in the stroma and periphery of adult tissue sections, consistent with their role in barrier surveillance (Supplementary Table S5, Marella et al., 2025).
- In health, CD8+ T cells are distributed across all skin regions, and their proportions are relatively uniform across various anatomical sites (Marella et al., 2025).

> "Tc cells are enriched in the stroma and periphery of adult tissue sections."
>
> — Marella et al. (2025)

- Histological analyses demonstrate CD8+ T cell infiltration in both the epidermis and dermis during disease states such as vitiligo, psoriasis, and scleroderma (Wang et al., 2019; Siemińska et al., 2024; Gao et al., 2021).

> "CD8 + T cell infiltration of the epidermis and dermis has been demonstrated histologically, in addition, higher numbers of cytotoxic CD8 + T cells in the blood were found in patients with vitiligo compared to healthy controls [49]."
>
> — Wang et al. (2019)

- In disease, distinct tissue-resident memory (Trm, CD103+) CD8+ T cell populations accumulate in specific skin locations, including lining the basement membrane (Gibson et al., 2024).

> "immunohistochemistry confirmed that cytotoxic (GNLY + ) CD8 + CD103 + T RM T cells line the basement membrane in SJS/ TEN-affected skin (Supplementary Fig. 7a)."
>
> — Gibson et al. (2024)

## Function

### Cytotoxicity and Effector Responses

Tc cells are primarily defined by cytolytic effector functions, which allow them to kill infected, stressed, or transformed cells in the skin (Marella et al., 2025; Gibson et al., 2024; Szabo et al., 2019).

> "Tc (CD8+ T cell) population is characterized by cytotoxic functions and IFN-gamma secretion."
>
> — Marella et al. (2025)

> "CD8 + T cells segregate into two major effector subsets based on expression of genes involved in cellular cytotoxicity (Cytotoxic module) and myriad cytokines and chemokines (Cytokine module)."
>
> — Szabo et al. (2019)

In skin diseases such as cutaneous leishmaniasis, psoriasis, and vitiligo, Tc cells contribute to both protective immunity and immunopathology:

> "Cytoxic CD8 + T cells has also been associated with tissue damage in mucocutaneous leishmaniasis (Brodskyn et al., 1997) as well as lesion progression in CL patients (Faria et al., 2009)."
>
> — Santos et al. (2013)

> "Melanocyte-specific, cytotoxic CD8 + T cells have been strongly implicated in the destruction of melanocytes in multiple studies."
>
> — Wang et al. (2019)

> "Moreover, epidermal psoriatic CD8 + Tc cells have increased expression of granzyme A, granzyme B, and perforin 1, key proteins responsible for the cytotoxic activity of CD8 + T cells [194,220]."
>
> — Siemińska et al. (2024)

- In leishmaniasis, cytotoxic CD8+ T cells accumulate in lesions and produce granzyme B, correlating with tissue injury (Santos et al., 2013; Covre et al., 2024).
- In vitiligo, skin-homing, type 1-polarized CD8+ T cells cluster around disappearing melanocytes and mediate antigen-specific cytotoxicity (Yang et al., 2015).

### Cytokine Production and Immunoregulation

Many skin CD8+ T cells produce IFN-gamma and additional pro-inflammatory cytokines in response to local antigen or inflammation (Marella et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2019).

> "Several cytokines, such as TNF-a and IFN-g that are primarily involved in melanocytes destruction, are produced by CD8 + T cells [52]."
>
> — Wang et al. (2019)

Some functional Tc subsets (Tc17, Tc22) may lack classical cytotoxicity but contribute to cytokine-mediated inflammation (Sabat et al., 2019; Torok et al., 2019):

> "While Tc1 and Tc17+1 CD8+ T cells display a classical cytotoxic phenotype, Tc2, Tc17, and Tc22 lack the capability to kill target cells and express the Th cell-typical molecule CD40L instead (Loyal et al., manuscript under review; [50])."
>
> — Sabat et al. (2019)

### Tissue Resident Memory and Clonal Expansion

Tc cells in the skin can become tissue-resident memory T cells (Trm), defined by CD103 and other retention markers, positioning them for rapid local responses (Gibson et al., 2024; Cao et al., 2025).

> "The cytotoxic CD8 + Tconv cluster expressed the same T RM -defining markers."
>
> — Gibson et al. (2024)

During acute inflammation, these Trm-like cytotoxic CD8+ T cells show clonal expansion and express high levels of cytotoxicity-related genes (Gibson et al., 2024):

> "Expanded oligoclonal clonotypes were significantly enriched for cytotoxicity-related genes, including GNLY, PRF1, and FCGR3A, but unexpanded polyclonal clonotypes for GZMA and GZMK (Fig. 4c)."
>
> — Gibson et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

- Tc cells are medium-sized lymphocytes, typically expressing CD3 and CD8, and lacking CD4 expression (Zheng et al., 2025).
- In the context of acral skin lymphomas, they may be present as medium-sized cells infiltrating the dermis, with variation in the loss of pan-T cell antigens (CD2, CD5, CD7); TIA-1 positivity is observed, but not all cases express granzyme B or perforin (Zheng et al., 2025).

> "T-cells is characterized by positivity for CD3 and CD8, absence of CD4 and CD30."
>
> — Zheng et al. (2025)
