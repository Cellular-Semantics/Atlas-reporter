# Cytotoxic T Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [cytotoxic T cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000910) (CL:0000910, exact match)

## Summary

Cytotoxic T cells (Tc) are one of three T cell subtypes identified in adult human skin by Reynolds et al. (2021). They express the canonical CD8 co-receptor (CD8A, CD8B) and exert cytolytic effector functions via granzymes and perforin. In healthy adult skin, Tc cells reside in both the epidermis and dermis, with epidermal cells displaying a tissue-resident memory phenotype. In inflammatory skin disease, disease-specific Tc subsets expand markedly: Tc IL13/IL22 cells predominate in atopic dermatitis (AD) and Tc17/Th17 cells in psoriasis, both exhibiting heightened clonality.

## Markers

> "cytotoxic (Tc) expressing CD8A/B"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

The core defining markers are CD8A and CD8B. Cytotoxic effector function is mediated by granzyme A (GZMA) and granzyme B (GZMB), perforin (PRF1), and granulysin (GNLY):

> "CD8 T cells exhibited cytotoxic signatures (CD8A, CD8B, GZMA, GNLY)"
>
> — Marella et al. (2024)

Disease-associated subsets carry additional cytokine markers:

> "cytotoxic T cells expressing IL13, IL22 and IFNG (Tc IL13/IL22)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "T cells expressing IL17A, IL17F, IFNG, IL22 and IL26 (Tc17/Th17) are found in both lesional and non-lesional skin"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

## Location

Cytotoxic T cells are found in both the epidermis and dermis of healthy adult human skin. Reynolds et al. (2021) compared epidermal versus dermal T cell transcriptomes:

> "Epidermal T cells upregulated genes associated with skin tissue residency (RGS1, PPP1R15A), effector memory (CD44, ID2), T cell activation (TNFRSF18) and inhibition of T cell response (CD96, TSC22D3 and DUSP4)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Dermal T cells, by contrast, express interferon-stimulated genes (IFITM1, IFI6, LY6E) and transcriptionally resemble blood T cells more closely than their epidermal counterparts (Reynolds et al., 2021).

## Function

### Homeostatic cytotoxicity and tissue residency

In healthy skin, epidermal Tc cells adopt a tissue-resident memory (TRM) programme, poised to mount rapid immune responses while co-expressing inhibitory molecules (CD96, DUSP4, TSC22D3) to prevent responses to non-pathogenic antigens (Reynolds et al., 2021). Granzyme A-expressing CD8 T cells are present in psoriasis lesions:

> "the majority of CD8 T cells from psoriasis lesions expressed GzmA. Confocal microscopy confirmed intracellular GzmA+ granules in epidermal T cells"
>
> — Cheuk et al. (2017)

### Disease-associated effector states

In AD, Tc IL13/IL22 cells dominate the lesional dermis and express amphiregulin and tissue-residency genes:

> "Tc IL13/IL22 cells are dominant in the dermis of AD lesional skin and express amphiregulin (AREG), a member of the epidermal growth factor family, skin tissue residency genes (RGS1, NR4A1, NR4A2), and effector and activated T cell genes (ID2, PRDM1, MAP3K8, DUSP2)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

In psoriasis, Tc17/Th17 cells dominate the lesional epidermis and express pathogenic Th17 cell genes:

> "Tc17/Th17 cells are dominant in the epidermis of lesional psoriasis skin, which was validated by flow cytometry. These cells express genes characteristic of activated and pathogenic Th17 cells (KLRB1, RBPJ and CXCL13)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### Clonal expansion in disease

Disease-associated Tc subsets show heightened clonality. Clonal T cells upregulate co-stimulatory and TCR-signalling genes:

> "Lesional clonal T cells had higher expression of co-stimulation genes (CD63, TNFRSF18, JAML) and T cell receptor signaling (EVL, LAT, LCK, JAK1) than non-clonal T cells"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)
