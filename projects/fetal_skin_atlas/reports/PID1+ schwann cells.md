# PID1+ Schwann Cells (Fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [Schwann cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002573) (CL:0002573, broad match)

## Summary

PID1+ Schwann cells are a fine-grained Schwann cell subtype identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas (7–17 PCW), defined by expression of PID1 (phosphotyrosine interaction domain containing 1). Schwann cells are peripheral nervous system glial cells that myelinate or ensheath axons; in prenatal skin they are enriched in early gestation as part of the neural cell population that establishes the cutaneous peripheral nervous system. The PID1+ subtype represents a transcriptionally distinct Schwann cell state within fetal skin, likely corresponding to either an immature or non-myelinating Schwann cell population given the early gestational context. The broader SOX10-expressing neural lineage in prenatal skin co-locates with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment (ME1), which support axon guidance and synaptogenesis. Like other Schwann cell subtypes in this atlas, PID1+ Schwann cells show slow maturation toward adult cell states.

## Markers

- **PID1 (phosphotyrosine interaction domain containing 1)**: The defining marker of this Schwann cell subtype. PID1 contains a phosphotyrosine-binding domain and has roles in signalling downstream of growth factor receptors; its expression in a Schwann cell subtype may reflect signalling states relevant to axon-glial communication during development.

- **SOX10**: The master transcription factor for the Schwann cell lineage, expressed throughout neural crest differentiation and Schwann cell development.

  > "Sox10 belongs to the group of HMG (high mobility group) domain-containing transcription factors (Wegner, 1999;Bowles et al., 2000). During vertebrate development, it is highly expressed in the emerging neural crest and later in the developing peripheral nervous system (PNS) and central nervous system, where its occurrence is restricted to glial cells"
  >
  > -- Finzsch et al. (2010)

- **S100B**: Pan-Schwann cell calcium-binding marker.

- **MPZ (P0)**: Expressed from Schwann cell precursor stage onward; marks commitment to the Schwann lineage.

## Location

### Prenatal Skin

PID1+ Schwann cells are identified in fetal skin (7–17 PCW) as a fine-grained annotation in Supplementary Table 3 of the atlas. Neural cells including Schwann cell subtypes are enriched in early gestation and co-locate with TML macrophages in the early neurovascular microenvironment:

> "TML macrophages were also predicted to co-locate with Schwann cells in prenatal skin ('early neurovascular microenvironment', ME1) (Figs. 1d and 3g) and expressed genes related to cell migration and"

## Function

### Peripheral Nervous System Development

PID1+ Schwann cells contribute to the cutaneous peripheral nervous system during fetal skin development, likely ensheatheing or myelinating sensory and autonomic axons innervating the developing dermis. TML macrophage interactions via VEGFA-NRP and SEMA3-NRP signalling support axon guidance and synapse formation in this context.

### Immature Differentiation State

Schwann cells in the prenatal skin atlas and SkO model show a prolonged immature state relative to adult skin:

> "Even after 19 weeks of culture, fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells had a low probability of correspondence to adult skin cell states (Extended Data Fig. 2d and Supplementary Table 5)."
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)
