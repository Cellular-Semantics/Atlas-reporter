# PEAR1+ Fibroblast in Fetal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Source dataset: Gopee et al. (2024), prenatal skin, 7–17 PCW
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast of dermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002551) (CL:0002551, broad match)

## Summary

The PEAR1+ fibroblast cluster in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas represents a PEAR1-expressing dermal fibroblast population that arises from HOXC5+ early fibroblast progenitors along the dermal fibroblast trajectory, becoming abundant after 11 PCW alongside WNT2+ fibroblasts. PEAR1+ fibroblasts carry the most adult-like pro-inflammatory transcriptional signature among fetal skin fibroblast subsets: genes associated with pro-inflammatory fibroblast phenotypes (APOE, IGFBP7, ITM2A) are upregulated during the HOXC5-to-PEAR1 transition. PEAR1 (platelet endothelial aggregation receptor 1) is a receptor tyrosine kinase-like receptor previously characterised in platelet aggregation and endothelial cell biology; its function in dermal fibroblasts is not yet established. The progressive acquisition of an adult-like gene expression profile in PEAR1+ fibroblasts during late gestation parallels the loss of the scarless healing capacity that characterises early prenatal skin.

## Markers

### Lineage-defining marker: PEAR1

PEAR1 expression defines this fibroblast subset within fetal dermis. PEAR1+ fibroblasts arise from HOXC5+ early fibroblast progenitors along the dermal fibroblast trajectory, following the same path as WNT2+ fibroblasts but representing a more differentiated state with a stronger pro-inflammatory signature:

> "the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts (abundant after 11 PCW)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Pro-inflammatory transition genes: APOE, IGFBP7, ITM2A

PEAR1+ fibroblasts are distinguished from WNT2+ fibroblasts by upregulation of genes associated with a pro-inflammatory fibroblast phenotype during the HOXC5-to-PEAR1 transition:

> "Genes associated with a pro-inflammatory fibroblast phenotype (APOE, IGFBP7 and ITM2A)...were also upregulated during the transition from HOXC5 + fibroblasts into PEAR1 + fibroblasts"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Shared late-gestation markers with WNT2+ fibroblasts: CDKN1A, IL1R1, POSTN

PEAR1+ and WNT2+ fibroblasts share a set of late-gestation markers associated with cellular senescence and pro-fibrotic pathways:

> "Notably, WNT2 + and PEAR1 + prenatal fibroblasts expressed several genes involved in cellular senescence (CDKN1A), cytokine pathways (for example, IL1R1) and collagen deposition (for example, POSTN)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Adult-like gene expression profile

> "The adult fibroblast gene expression profile was increased in WNT2 + and PEAR1 + prenatal fibroblasts, which were abundant in later gestation"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

### Fetal dermis, abundant from 11 PCW

PEAR1+ fibroblasts are a fine-grained cell type annotated in Supplementary Table 6 of Gopee et al. (2024). They reside in the fetal dermis and become abundant after 11 PCW, representing the more differentiated end of the HOXC5+ early fibroblast → WNT2+ → PEAR1+ differentiation continuum.

## Function

### Progressive acquisition of adult-type pro-inflammatory fibroblast identity

The primary functional significance of PEAR1+ fibroblasts in the context of this atlas is their role in the progressive maturation of the fetal dermis toward an adult-like, pro-inflammatory state. Their late-gestation abundance correlates with the loss of the scarless healing capacity that characterises early prenatal skin. The upregulation of CDKN1A (cellular senescence), collagen deposition genes (POSTN), and pro-inflammatory mediators in these cells contributes to the transition from regenerative to scar-forming wound healing as gestation advances.
