# Early LE — Early Lymphatic Endothelium (fetal)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis
Scope: fetal

## CL Mapping

**CL:2000011** — dermis lymphatic vessel endothelial cell
- Match type: broad
- Rationale: Early LE represents an early/progenitor lymphatic endothelial cell state in fetal skin, annotated under the broad "Lymphatic endothelium" category. CL:2000011 (dermis lymphatic vessel endothelial cell) is defined as "any endothelial cell of lymphatic vessel that is part of a dermis" and is a subclass of CL:0002138 (endothelial cell of lymphatic vessel). This is the best available match; no progenitor or early-state dermis lymphatic endothelial cell term exists in CL. A new term request may be warranted for an early/progenitor state.

## Summary

Early LE is a fine-grained annotation in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW), classified under the broad "Lymphatic endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). The atlas defines LE as:

> "LE, lymphatic endothelium"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Early LE represents an early or progenitor lymphatic endothelial cell state in fetal dermis, distinct from the more mature "LE" population within the same broad category. It is present throughout gestation and co-locates with macrophages in neurovascular microenvironments.

## Markers

Marker genes for Early LE are provided in Supplementary Table 3 as DEGs used for annotation. Canonical lymphatic endothelial cell markers established in the literature and consistent with the parent class CL:0002138 include PROX1, LYVE1, PDPN (podoplanin), and FLT4 (VEGFR3). The early/progenitor state is expected to share these markers at lower or intermediate expression levels compared to mature LE.

## Location

Early LE cells are localised to developing lymphatic vessels within the prenatal dermis. Spatial analysis placed endothelial cells including lymphatic endothelium in neurovascular microenvironments:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

These cells belong to the mesoderm-derived endothelial compartment present throughout gestation:

> "Mesoderm-derived cells, including skin fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and immune cells were present throughout gestation"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

## Function

### Early lymphangiogenesis

Early LE cells represent the initial lymphatic endothelial cell state in fetal skin, likely participating in the early phases of lymphatic vessel formation (lymphangiogenesis). Their maturation into the LE state involves progressive acquisition of the full lymphatic endothelial gene programme.

### Macrophage interactions and immune dependence

Macrophage-endothelial interactions support vascular development broadly in prenatal skin. Immunodeficient organoids lacking macrophages show reduced endothelial heterogeneity:

> "we observed fewer and less heterogeneous endothelial cells in the immunodeficient SkOs compared to prenatal skin, despite the formation of well-developed HFs, epidermis and neural cells"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x

Reciprocal communication is predicted through ligand-receptor interactions:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"

— Gopee et al. (2024), DOI:10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x
