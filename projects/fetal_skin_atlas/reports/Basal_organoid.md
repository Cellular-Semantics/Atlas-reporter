# Basal Keratinocytes in Hair-Bearing Skin Organoid (SkO)
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))  
Scope: organoid
Cell Ontology: [basal cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002187) (CL:0002187, exact match)

## Summary

Basal keratinocytes are a fundamental epidermal cell population identified in the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) model (Gopee et al., 2024). These cells form the proliferative basal layer of the stratified epidermis and represent the progenitor population from which other epidermal cell states arise. In the SkO, basal keratinocytes exhibit accelerated differentiation compared to other cell lineages, achieving alignment with adult skin cell states as early as 4 weeks of organoid culture (Gopee et al., 2024). The SkO model recapitulates the different components of prenatal skin including interfollicular epidermal cells, with cell states matching prenatal skin more closely than adult skin overall.

## Location

### In the SkO model

Basal keratinocytes form the innermost proliferative layer of the stratified epidermis in the SkO. The organoid generates a cyst-like structure with the epidermis on its outer surface, and basal cells line the epidermal-dermal interface.

### Comparison with prenatal and adult skin

>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

> "accelerated differentiation was observed in keratinocytes and melanocytes, with alignment to adult cell states seen as early as 4 weeks of SkO culture"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Function

### Epidermal progenitor

Basal keratinocytes in the SkO serve as the progenitor pool for the stratified interfollicular epidermis. They undergo asymmetric division to produce suprabasal daughter cells that terminally differentiate through the spinous, granular, and cornified layers.

### Hair follicle contribution

Within the SkO, basal cells also contribute to hair follicle formation. The immature basal state represents the early proliferative population that precedes the emergence of specialised hair follicle keratinocyte states such as POSTN+ basal cells, from which the outer root sheath, companion layer, and inner root sheath trajectories originate.
