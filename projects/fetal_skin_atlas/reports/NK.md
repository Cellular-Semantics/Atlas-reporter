# Natural Killer Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [natural killer cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000623) (CL:0000623, exact match)

## Summary

Natural killer (NK) cells in adult human skin are a distinct innate lymphocyte population identified by Reynolds et al. (2021). They are CD161(KLRB1)+, CD3-, and express a characteristic cytotoxic gene signature including KLRD1, GNLY, PRF1, GZMB, and FCGR3A. Reynolds et al. (2021) distinguish them from the transitional ILC1/NK population, which shares overlapping features of ILC1 and NK cells. Adult skin NK cells are closely related to fetal skin NK cells but differ in granzyme expression profile.

## Markers

> "NK (KLRD1+, GNLY+, PRF1+, GZMB+ and FCGR3A+) cells"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

> "We identified 4 clusters of innate lymphocytes that were CD161(KLRB1)+ CD3(CD3D/CD3G)-, consisting of ILC1/3, ILC2, ILC1/Natural Killer (NK) and NK (KLRD1+, GNLY+, PRF1+, GZMB+ and FCGR3A+) cells"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

The KLRD1 (CD94) gene product pairs with NKG2 subunits to form inhibitory or activating heterodimers; its expression in skin NK cells is consistent with mature NK cytotoxic function. PRF1 (perforin) and GNLY (granulysin) are effector molecules enabling target cell lysis.

## Location

NK cells are found in the lymphoid compartment of healthy adult dermis. They are identified in the adult skin UMAP alongside T cell subsets and ILCs (Reynolds et al., 2021). In the skin tumour context, NK cells are found in both healthy dermis and tumour stroma:

> "Natural killer cells play an important role in host immunity against pathogens and cancers. We identified one NK cell cluster based on its expression of Klr genes which encode molecules expressed on NK cells"
>
> — Fan et al. (2024)

## Function

### Cytotoxicity

NK cells in the skin exert cytotoxic function through the perforin-granzyme pathway. GZMB (granzyme B) and PRF1 are key effectors:

> "Granzyme B is a key serine protease expressed in cytotoxic T cells and NK cells by the GZMB gene; it enters the target cells through membrane pores created by perforin and induces apoptosis, eliminating infected cells"
>
> — Hasankhani et al. (2021)

### Relationship to fetal NK cells

Reynolds et al. (2021) found that fetal NK cells closely correlate with adult NK cells but differ in their granzyme profile:

> "Fetal NK cells correlate with adult NK cells, but express higher levels of GZMM and GZMK, suggesting that they may be functionally competent"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

### Distinction from ILC1/NK

Adult skin contains a transitional ILC1/NK population with overlapping features of ILC1 and NK cells:

> "ILC1/NK have overlapping features of ILC1 and NKs, as described. Plasticity within ILC1 and ILC3 is also recognized"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

The mature NK population (labelled "NK" in the Reynolds atlas) is distinguished from ILC1/NK by its higher expression of KLRD1, GNLY, PRF1, GZMB, and FCGR3A.
