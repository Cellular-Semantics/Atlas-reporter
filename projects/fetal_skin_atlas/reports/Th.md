# T helper cell (Th) / CD4+ T cell  
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Scope: adult

## Summary

T helper cells (Th), also referred to as CD4+ T cells, are a fundamental immune cell type crucial for adaptive immunity in adult human skin. They express characteristic helper and homing markers such as CD4, IL7R, LEF1, and CCR7 and reside predominantly within the dermal compartment. Th cells are highly heterogeneous, comprising various functional subsets (e.g., Th1, Th2, Th17, Th22, Tfh, Treg) that coordinate local immune surveillance, host defense, tissue homeostasis, and inflammation through varied cytokine production and cellular interactions. Their positioning in skin reveals spatial and anatomical adaptation, from clustering around hair follicles to forming functional tissue-resident memory pools.

## Markers

CD4+ T cells are identifiable by several canonical and functionally relevant markers as described in both the skin atlas and the wider literature:

- **CD4**: This classic surface molecule is the defining marker of T helper cells, used routinely to identify the population in single-cell and histological analyses (Supplementary Table S5; Schinner et al., 2023).
- **IL7R**: IL7R is upregulated in CD4+ T cell clusters in skin. Functionally, IL7R is essential for T cell survival and homeostatic maintenance, especially in tissue-resident memory T cells (Supplementary Table S5; Marella et al., 2025).
- **LEF1**: Found among differentially expressed genes in CD4+ T cell clusters; LEF1 is a transcription factor involved in T cell development (Supplementary Table S5).
- **CCR7**: Important for naive and central memory T cell trafficking, CCR7 is significantly upregulated in CD4+ T cells in human skin (Supplementary Table S5; Marella et al., 2025).
- **CD69, CD103 (ITGAE)**: Expression of these markers delineates tissue-resident memory (Trm) phenotypes. CD69 marks tissue retention, while CD103 is strongly associated with tissue residency and epithelial retention in the skin (Guomu Liu et al., 2024).
- **CXCR6, CD101, TWIST1**: These are enriched in skin-homing or resident CD4+ T cell populations, further supporting a role for specific migratory and tissue adaptation programs (A. A. K. Samat et al., 2021).
- **CCR4, CCR6, CCR10**: Chemokine receptor expression underlies skin tropism and recruitment (Jianguang Gong et al., 2021; A. A. K. Samat et al., 2021).
- **T-bet, GATA-3, RORγT, AHR, BCL6, PU.1**: Each subset-defining transcription factor (TF) marks canonical Th1, Th2, Th17, Th22, Tfh, and Th9 cells, respectively, correlating to functional specialization (Antonio Bensussen et al., 2024; Schinner et al., 2023).
- **FoxP3, CTLA4, CD25, ICOS**: Specifically highlight CD4+ regulatory T (Treg) cells in the skin (L. Cañedo-Dorantes et al., 2019; J. Khantakova et al., 2022).

Additional functionally relevant activation and regulatory markers, such as CD25, HLA-DR, PD-1, TIGIT, and CTLA-4, are variably expressed, particularly under inflammatory or antigen-stimulated conditions (Lambert et al., 2022; Prodger et al., 2015).

## Location

### Dermal Localization and Follicular Association

> "In the dermal compartment, helper T (TH) cells were predominant compared to CD8+T cells, and the expression of CD103 was also relatively low compared to that in the epidermis."
>
> — Guomu Liu et al. (2024)

> "we showed that CD4 + and CD8 + T cells can be detected both in the epidermis and dermis, with CD4 + T cells predominantly detected in the dermis (especially clustered around hair follicles)."
>
> — Weijie Du et al. (2021)

> "CD4 + T cells were localized exclusively around hair follicles ( Supplementary Fig. 1b)."
>
> — T. Adachi et al. (2015)

> "Most skin-homing T cells consist of CD4 memory T cells, and reside in the dermis."
>
> — Z. Chow et al. (2019)

CD4+ T cells are prevalent throughout the adult skin, with higher concentration in the dermis, and frequent association with hair follicles. This positioning facilitates interactions with skin-resident antigen-presenting cells and enables rapid response to tissue perturbation.

### Developmental Timing

During fetal skin development, CD4+ T cells are mostly naive and proliferative, in contrast to the predominant memory/activated phenotype seen in adult skin (M. Dhariwala et al., 2020):

> "the largest subsets of both CD4 + Tconv and CD8 + T cells in fetal skin demonstrated a largely naive, proliferative status, whereas those dominating in adult skin were enriched for markers of memory and activation."
>
> — M. Dhariwala et al. (2020)

### Regional Variability

Single-cell transcriptomic profiling reveals regional variation in abundance; the scalp shows the highest proportion of CD4+ T cells, while other regions have distinctive T cell subset distributions (Marella et al., 2025).

### Tissue Residency and Circulation

> "In the skin of healthy mice, CD4 + T cells establish a dynamic equilibrium between migration and circulation with the circulating T-cell pool (59). Nonetheless, heightened skin inflammation or infection leads to augmented recruitment of CD4 + T cells to the skin and their retention in the dermis."
>
> — Guomu Liu et al. (2024)

> "70% to 85% of the total pool of tissueresident memory T cells consists of CD4 + T cells [4]."
>
> — J. Khantakova et al. (2022)

Tissue-resident memory (Trm) CD4+ T cells form a major proportion of the skin’s memory T cell pool, regulated by interactions with the local microenvironment and cytokines (e.g., IL-7 from hair follicle keratinocytes).

## Function

### Adaptive Defense and Immune Surveillance

CD4+ T cells in the skin are essential immune orchestrators. They mediate host defense against pathogens, tissue homeostasis, wound healing, and regulate various inflammatory and autoimmune responses (L. Cañedo-Dorantes et al., 2019; R. Sabat et al., 2019):

> "CD4+ T cells exert helper/inducer functions."
>
> — L. Loyal et al. (2020)

### Subset Diversification and Specialization

> "CD4 + T cells can be further subdivided into different subsets on the basis of distinct molecular and functional characteristics. Th1, Th2, Th17, regulatory T (Treg), Th9, Th22, and follicular Th (Tfh) cells are the major subsets of fully differentiated CD4 + T cells."
>
> — Z. Jadali (2019)

> "canonical effector lineages, they are divided in Th1, Th2, Th9, Th17, Th22 and TFH (T follicular lymphocytes) subsets, which can be identified as T-bet + IFN-g + (Th1), GATA3 + IL-4 + (Th2), PU.1 + IL-9 + (Th9), RORgT + IL-17 + (Th17), AHR + IL-22 + (Th22) and BCL6 + IL-21 + (TFH) (Figure 1A)."
>
> — Antonio Bensussen et al. (2024)

Each subset is defined by unique transcription factors and cytokine profiles, thus promoting tailored immune responses: Th1 (IFN-γ, intracellular pathogen defense), Th2 (IL-4, IL-13, humoral response and allergy), Th9 (IL-9, tissue immunity), Th17 (IL-17, barrier protection and neutrophil recruitment), Th22 (IL-22, wound healing and barrier function), Tfh (help for B cells), and Treg (immune suppression).

### Cytokine Production and Tissue Interactions

> "They produce IL-9 that is required for maximal production of IL-9 itself as well as for the production of additional inflammatory cytokines including IFN-γ, IL-17, and IL-13 in skin tropic Th cells."
>
> — Z. Jadali (2019)

> "CD4 + T lymphocytes secrete a set of specific cytokines to coordinate immune responses."
>
> — Antonio Bensussen et al. (2024)

These cytokine signatures drive direct effector functions (microbial killing, inflammation) and orchestrate responses from other immune and stromal cell types.

### Memory, Tissue Retention, and Wound Response

> "This results in the development of a CD69 + CD103 + CD4 + T-cell phenotype embedded within the skin, which possesses potent effector functions (15)."
>
> — Guomu Liu et al. (2024)

> "These induced CD4 + and CD8 + T RM cells had tissue-resident memory characteristics in terms of cell surface phenotypes, transcriptional profiles, and potential self-renewal ability."
>
> — Qianchuan Tian et al. (2022)

Discovery of tissue-resident and memory CD4+ T cells underlines their critical role in rapid, localized immune defense upon reinfection or injury, often independent of recirculating lymphocyte recruitment.

### Regulation of Tolerance and Homeostasis

> "Skin homeostasis and peripheral tolerance to commensals and self-antigens are controlled by skin immunosuppressive CD4+Foxp3+ regulatory T cells (T regs ) that suppress the abnormal effects of self-reactive immune cells' responses [102,103]."
>
> — L. Cañedo-Dorantes et al. (2019)

> "Treg cells are predominantly found in lymphoid tissues and represent 2-5% of CD4 + T cells [63]."
>
> — J. Khantakova et al. (2022)

FoxP3+ regulatory T cells represent a specialized, skin-adapted Treg Trm population crucial for maintaining immune homeostasis and preventing excessive inflammation.

### Subsets in Disease and Inflammation

> "Skin is protected by several subsets of CD4 + Th cells. Interestingly, these different subsets are found among the CLA + skin-homing T cell population and participate in cutaneous defense responses against bacterial, viral, and fungal pathogens as well as in immune responses toward allergens and autoantigens."
>
> — Z. Jadali (2019)

Distinct Th subset imbalances drive dermatological diseases: Th1 and Th17 dominate autoimmune and inflammatory conditions (e.g., psoriasis, vitiligo), Th2 in atopic dermatitis, Treg suppression in autoimmunity and cancer (R. Sabat et al., 2019).

### Antigen-Specific Responses and Activation

> "Th17 and Th1 cells had substantially higher levels of both activation markers (87.9 and 86.0% CD69+, respectively; 43.4 and 45.9% HLA-DR+, respectively); Th22 cells were also more activated than other CD4 cells (60.5% CD69+ and 35.8% HLA-DR+, p<0.001), albeit less so that Th1 or Th17 cells (both p<0.001)."
>
> — J. Prodger et al. (2015)

Such activation marker expression reflects inflammatory and/or antigen-specific activation within the skin, with direct relevance to their effector and regulatory roles.

## Structure / Morphology

> "Phenotypic analysis suggests most of the CD4 + and CD8 + T cells are effector memory T cells, as assessed by CD44 and CD62L expressions, and are resident cells, by CD103 and CD69 co-expression (Fig. 1d)."
>
> — T. Adachi et al. (2015)

> "CD4 + T cells in the epidermis expressed CD103 at slightly lower levels than those in the dermis (Fig. 1e,f)."
>
> — T. Adachi et al. (2015)

Skin Th cells are typically small lymphocytes, with morphology indicative of resident memory and effector memory states, displaying co-expression of markers associated with residency, memory, and variable activation across compartments (epidermis/dermis, around follicles).

No further ultrastructural or cytoplasmic feature descriptions were found in the traversed literature.
