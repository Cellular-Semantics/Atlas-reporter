# T Helper Cell in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [helper T cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000912) (CL:0000912, exact match)

## Summary

T helper (Th) cells are one of three T cell subtypes identified in adult human skin by Reynolds et al. (2021). They express CD4 and CD40LG as defining markers and are found in both the epidermis and dermis. In healthy skin, they coordinate adaptive immune responses by providing help to B cells and cytotoxic T cells, and by directing macrophage activation. In inflammatory skin disease, Th2 cells (producing IL-4 and IL-13) dominate in atopic dermatitis, while Th17 cells (producing IL-17A, IL-17F) are central to psoriasis pathogenesis.

## Markers

> "helper (Th) expressing CD4, CD40LG"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

In healthy human skin, CD4 T cells additionally express homing and homeostatic markers:

> "CD4 T cells expressed helper and homing markers (CD4, IL7R, CCR7)"
>
> — Marella et al. (2024)

## Location

T helper cells are distributed across both the epidermal and dermal compartments of adult human skin. Reynolds et al. (2021) compared T cell transcriptomes between compartments, finding that dermal T cells transcriptionally resemble blood T cells more closely, while epidermal T cells carry tissue-residency signatures. CD4+ T cells are predominant in the upper dermis of inflammatory skin conditions.

## Function

### Homeostatic immune coordination

In healthy skin, Th cells provide help to other immune cells. Dermal CD14+ monocytes, in turn, can provide direct B cell help and induce follicular helper T cell differentiation (Reynolds and Haniffa, 2015). Th cells are essential for coordinating both innate and adaptive skin immune responses.

### Th2-mediated atopic dermatitis

In atopic dermatitis, Th2-polarized CD4+ cells drive IL-4 and IL-13 production, with pathological consequences for the skin barrier:

> "Th2 polarization facilitates S. aureus binding and colonization, and IL-4 and IL-13 inhibit skin production of AMPs"
>
> — Chatzigeorgiou et al. (2024)

> "T-helper 2 (Th2) and T-helper 22 (Th22) responses are intensified in chronic atopic dermatitis lesions, with parallel activation of the Th1 axis"
>
> — Chatzigeorgiou et al. (2024)

### Th17-mediated psoriasis

In psoriasis, the Tc17/Th17 disease-associated population (shared between cytotoxic and helper T cell lineages) dominates the lesional epidermis:

> "Tc17/Th17 cells are dominant in the epidermis of lesional psoriasis skin, which was validated by flow cytometry. These cells express genes characteristic of activated and pathogenic Th17 cells (KLRB1, RBPJ and CXCL13)"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)
