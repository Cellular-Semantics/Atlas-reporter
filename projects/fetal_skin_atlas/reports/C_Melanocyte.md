# Melanocyte in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [melanocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000148) (CL:0000148, exact match)

## Summary

Melanocytes (C_Melanocyte, labelled with the C_ prefix to disambiguate from macrophages in atlas file ordering) are neural crest-derived pigment-producing cells that reside in the basal layer of the epidermis and in hair follicles of adult human skin. They are identified in Reynolds et al. (2021) as part of the stromal cell compartment alongside fibroblasts, endothelial cells, pericytes, and Schwann cells. Melanocytes are defined by co-expression of TYRP1, DCT, MLANA, PMEL, and the master transcription factor MITF. Their primary function is the synthesis of melanin pigment within specialised organelles called melanosomes, which are transferred to surrounding keratinocytes to provide photoprotection against UV radiation.

Key markers: **TYRP1** (tyrosinase-related protein 1, melanin synthesis enzyme), **DCT** (dopachrome tautomerase, melanin synthesis enzyme), **MLANA** (MART-1, melanosome-associated antigen), **PMEL** (gp100/Pmel17, melanosome structural protein), **MITF** (microphthalmia-associated transcription factor, master regulator).

## Location

Melanocytes are located primarily in the basal layer (stratum basale) of the interfollicular epidermis and in the bulb and outer root sheath of hair follicles. In the epidermis, each melanocyte forms a dendritic melanin unit with approximately 36 surrounding keratinocytes, to which it transfers melanosomes via its dendrites.

## Function

### Melanin synthesis and UV photoprotection

Melanocytes produce melanin through the enzymatic melanogenesis pathway. TYR (tyrosinase), TYRP1, and DCT are the three key enzymes that convert tyrosine to eumelanin (brown/black) or phaeomelanin (yellow/red). Melanosomes loaded with melanin are transferred to keratinocytes where they form supranuclear melanin caps that shield the keratinocyte nucleus from UV-induced DNA damage.

### Neural crest origin and skin identity

Melanocytes derive from neural crest cells that migrate from the dorsal neural tube to the skin during embryonic development. MITF is the master transcription factor controlling melanocyte identity, survival, and proliferation throughout life. MITF directly activates the promoters of TYRP1, DCT, and PMEL, establishing and maintaining the melanocyte transcriptional programme.

### Role in inflammatory skin disease

In inflammatory skin conditions including vitiligo (autoimmune melanocyte destruction) and psoriasis, the melanocyte population is altered. Reynolds et al. (2021) show that developmental stromal cell programmes are co-opted in inflammatory disease, and the stromal compartment including melanocytes is part of the broader skin cell ecosystem affected by inflammation.
