## Summary

Pre B cell is a late B-lymphocyte precursor stage identified in fetal human skin (7–17 PCW) by the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas. Single-cell haematopoietic profiling of fetal tissues has established that pre-B cells appear in the fetal liver and non-lymphoid tissues (NLT), including skin, during the first trimester:

> "Pre-B cells are detected between 7-8 PCW, but mature B cells only after 9 PCW"
> — Popescu et al. (2019), *Nature*

Skin-resident pre-B cells derive from haematopoietic precursors seeding peripheral tissues during a period when the lymphoid differentiation hierarchy is establishing itself across multiple organs:

> "Comparing liver with yolk sac and NLT, pro-B, pre-B and B cells are present in NLT but HSC/MPPs and pre pro-B cells are absent"
> — Popescu et al. (2019), *Nature*

The detection of pre-B cells in prenatal skin alongside more mature B cell stages indicates that B lymphopoiesis in non-lymphoid fetal tissue proceeds from committed precursors, rather than from the earliest multipotent progenitors.

## Markers

Pre-B cells are characterised by expression of the pre-B cell receptor (pre-BCR), comprising the mu (IgM) heavy chain paired with surrogate light chain components (VpreB and lambda5). Key markers include:

- **CD19** — B-lineage commitment marker, present from pro-B cell stage
- **Cytoplasmic IgM (mu heavy chain)** — marks completion of heavy chain rearrangement
- **Pre-BCR / surrogate light chain** — defines the pre-B cell stage
- **B220 (PTPRC/CD45R)** — B cell surface marker
- **RAG1/RAG2** — active during light chain rearrangement at the pre-B cell stage

Marker genes distinguishing fetal pre-B cells from other B cell precursor stages are provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

## Location

Pre B cells are located within the immune compartment of fetal skin (dermis) during prenatal development from approximately 7–8 PCW onwards. They were isolated as CD45+ immune cells by FACS prior to scRNA-seq. In parallel fetal haematopoiesis studies, pre-B cells are found in fetal liver, yolk sac, and non-lymphoid tissues including skin; notably, HSC/MPPs and pre-pro-B cells are absent from these non-lymphoid tissue sites.

## Function

Pre-B cells represent the developmental stage at which immunoglobulin light chain V(D)J recombination occurs, following successful heavy chain rearrangement at the pro-B cell stage. Signal transduction through the pre-BCR drives clonal expansion and licenses light chain rearrangement, ultimately generating the mature B cell antigen receptor.

In fetal tissues, the presence of pre-B cells alongside immature and mature B cells (but absence of the earliest progenitors) suggests that locally seeded precursors undergo B cell commitment and maturation steps within peripheral fetal tissues. The temporal pattern — pre-B cells at 7–8 PCW, mature B cells only after 9 PCW — reflects the progressive nature of B lymphopoiesis.

## Structure / Morphology

Pre-B cells are small-to-medium lymphoid cells with a large nucleus and scant cytoplasm. At the large pre-B-II stage, transient clonal expansion produces a population of cycling cells expressing the pre-BCR on the cell surface. At the subsequent small pre-B-II stage, cells become quiescent and initiate light chain rearrangement. No specific ultrastructural data for fetal skin pre-B cells are reported in the atlas text.
