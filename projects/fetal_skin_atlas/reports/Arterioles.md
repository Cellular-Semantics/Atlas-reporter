# Arterioles in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [CL:1000412](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_1000412) endothelial cell of arteriole

## Summary

Arterioles are the most terminally differentiated vascular endothelial cell state along the arteriolar trajectory in prenatal human skin (7–17 PCW). They are annotated as a fine-grained subtype within the broad "Vascular endothelium" category (Supplementary Table 3) and are distinguished by expression of the gap junction gene GJA5. Trajectory analysis revealed that early endothelial progenitors differentiate along either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries → capillary arterioles → arterioles) or a venular pathway (postcapillary venules → venules). Arteriole cells also show elevated sprouting angiogenesis ("tip" cell) potential. Comparison with skin organoids (SkO) lacking immune cells demonstrated that immune cell-derived signals are required for full arteriolar differentiation, implicating macrophage-endothelial crosstalk in vascular maturation.

## Markers

- **GJA5** (gap junction protein alpha-5): defining arteriolar marker; highest expression in arterioles, absent in lymphatic vessels (Gopee et al., 2024; He et al., 2022)
- **HEY1**: co-expressed with GJA5 in arterioles in adult human skin (He et al., 2022)

## Location

Arterioles are present throughout the dermal vasculature of prenatal human skin across the full gestational window studied (7–17 PCW). Endothelial cells including arterioles are detected from the earliest time points sampled (Extended Data Fig. 1d, Supplementary Table 4). Macrophage subsets interact with different vascular endothelial subtypes including arterioles (Extended Data Fig. 9a, Supplementary Table 28).

## Function

Arteriole endothelial cells represent the terminal differentiation state of the arteriolar vascular tree and are responsible for regulating blood flow distribution in the developing dermis. Their differentiation from capillary arterioles requires immune cell-derived signals absent from SkO:

> "Unlike SkO capillary arteriole cells, prenatal skin capillary arteriole cells could further differentiate into arterioles"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

> "Inferred trajectory analysis showed that early endothelial cells in prenatal skin differentiated into either an arteriolar pathway (capillaries, capillary arterioles and arterioles) or venular pathway (postcapillary venules and venules), with expression of characteristic genes (for example, GJA5 for arteriolar and PLVAP for venular)"

(Gopee et al., 2024)

In adult human skin, GJA5-expressing arterioles form a discrete subtype with defined markers distinct from post-arterial capillaries, pre-venular capillaries, and venular compartments:

> "the gap junction protein alpha 5 (GJA5) was most abundantly expressed in arterioles, but absent in LVs"

(He et al., 2022)
