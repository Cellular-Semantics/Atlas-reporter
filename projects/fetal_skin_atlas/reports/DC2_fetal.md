# DC2 (cDC2) in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [CD1c-positive myeloid dendritic cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002399) (CL:0002399, exact match)

## Summary

DC2, also known as conventional dendritic cell type 2 (cDC2), is a major myeloid dendritic cell subset identified in prenatal human skin in the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas. DC2 is present from the first trimester of human development, appearing in the dermis prior to the onset of bone marrow haematopoiesis, and closely resembles its adult counterpart (Botting & Haniffa, 2020). Characterized by expression of CD1C, FCER1A, and CLEC10A, fetal DC2 co-locates spatially with pre-dermal condensate cells in Visium spatial transcriptomics data, alongside DC1, lymphoid tissue inducer cells, and ILC3. This spatial association suggests a role in the microenvironment supporting early hair follicle formation. Cross-organ prenatal atlas analysis confirms that DC subset transcriptional profiles are conserved between prenatal and adult life (Suo et al., 2022). Although fetal DCs possess a relatively immature phenotype, they retain the capacity to induce T cell responses and are thought to contribute to tolerogenic responses to self and maternal antigens during early development (Miah et al., 2021).

## Markers

The following markers define the DC2/cDC2 population in prenatal skin, supported by atlas annotations and the broader literature:

- **CD1C**: The canonical marker for cDC2. In the prenatal skin atlas, CD1C is used for cluster annotation of DC2 (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024). CD1c-positive dermal dendritic cells are distinguishable from macrophages by 9 weeks gestational age in prenatal human dermis (Schuster et al., 2012).
- **FCER1A**: Encoding the alpha chain of the high-affinity IgE receptor, FCER1A is a co-annotating marker for DC2 in the atlas (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024) and a widely recognized cDC2 surface marker (Collin & Bigley, 2018).
- **CLEC10A (CD301)**: Identified as a differentially expressed gene in the DC2 cluster. CLEC10A has been established as a specific marker for human CD1c+ dendritic cells across multiple tissues (Heger et al., 2018) and is a consistent cDC2 marker in transcriptional profiling (Collin & Bigley, 2018).
- **CD163**: A differentially expressed gene in DC2, typically associated with anti-inflammatory functions and monocyte-like cDC2 subsets (Supplementary Table 3, Gopee et al., 2024).

Additional markers from the literature include CD11c, CD11b, CD2, CD172a (SIRPA), CD13, and CD33 (Collin & Bigley, 2018). Two transcriptionally distinct subsets of cDC2 have been described: cDC2A (CLEC10A-negative, CLEC4A-high) and cDC2B (CD1c+, CLEC10A+) (Brown et al., 2019). In neonatal skin, approximately two-thirds of DC2 express CD301b (MGL2), marking a functionally specialized tolerogenic subset (Weckel et al., 2022).

## Location

### Prenatal Skin (Dermis)

DC2 is a constituent of the dermal immune cell network in prenatal human skin. It is present from the first trimester, before bone marrow haematopoiesis commences:

> "Mature adult skin contains the dendritic cell (DC) subsets DC1 and DC2, as well as macrophages, all of which have been identified in first-trimester human skin and closely resemble their adult counterparts."
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

> "DC1 and DC2 have been identified in fetal skin prior to bone marrow production"
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

### Spatial Co-location with Pre-Dermal Condensate

In the prenatal skin atlas, Visium spatial transcriptomics revealed that DC2 co-locates with pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells:

> "pre-dermal condensate (pre-Dc) cells co-located with dendritic and lymphoid cells based on correlation analyses"
>
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

The Extended Data Fig. 1f legend specifies the immune cell types co-locating with pre-Dc: "DC1, DC2, LTi and ILC3" (Gopee et al., 2024).

### Developmental Origin

DC2 in fetal skin may originate from both fetal liver and, later, bone marrow-derived precursors:

> "as such they may originate from both fetal liver- and bone marrow-derived precursors"
>
> -- Botting & Haniffa (2020)

CD1c-positive dermal dendritic cells can be distinguished from skin macrophages as early as 9 weeks gestational age:

> "we found a distinction between CD206+CD1c+CD11c+ DDCs and CD206+CD209+CD1c- skin macrophages by 9 weeks estimated gestational age (EGA)"
>
> -- Schuster et al. (2012)

## Function

### Tolerogenic Immune Regulation

Fetal DCs, including cDC2, are thought to contribute to maintaining tolerance to self and maternal antigens during prenatal development:

> "Their presence during early development is attributed to ensuring tolerogenic responses to self and maternal antigens"
>
> -- Miah et al. (2021)

In neonatal skin, CD301b+ DC2 are specifically equipped for uptake of commensal bacteria and generation of regulatory T cells:

> "CD301b+ type 2 conventional dendritic cells (DC) as a subset in neonatal skin specifically capable of uptake, presentation and generation of regulatory T cells (Tregs) to commensal antigens"
>
> -- Weckel et al. (2022)

### Antigen Presentation

Despite a relatively immature phenotype, fetal cDC2 retain functional capacity:

> "Compared to adult DCs, fetal DCs possess an immature phenotype but can induce allogeneic T-cell proliferation upon culture"
>
> -- Miah et al. (2021)

### Functional Competence in Early Life

Studies in mice demonstrate that early life cDC2 are functionally competent, with environmental signals rather than ontogeny shaping their function:

> "cDC2 in early life, despite being distinct from cDC2 in adult life, are functionally competent and can induce T cell responses"
>
> -- Papaioannou et al. (2021)

> "cDC2 in early and adult life, however, are exposed to distinct cytokine environments that shape their transcriptional profile and alter their ability to sense pathogens, secrete cytokines and polarize T cells"
>
> -- Papaioannou et al. (2021)

### Transcriptional Conservation Across Development

Cross-organ prenatal atlas analysis confirms that DC subset transcriptional profiles are conserved between prenatal and adult life:

> "we found the transcriptional profiles of DC subsets were conserved between adult and prenatal counterparts"
>
> -- Suo et al. (2022)

### Potential Role in Hair Follicle Microenvironment

The spatial co-location of DC2 with pre-dermal condensate cells in Visium data suggests a potential non-immune role in supporting the niche for early hair follicle formation, alongside DC1, LTi, and ILC3 (Gopee et al., 2024). However, direct functional validation of this interaction remains to be performed.
