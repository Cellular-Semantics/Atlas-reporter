# Early Fibroblasts FRZB+ in Skin Organoid

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Tissue context: hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO)
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast of dermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002551) (CL:0002551, broad match)

## Summary

The Early fibroblasts FRZB+ population represents a rare early dermal fibroblast state marked by expression of the secreted Wnt antagonist FRZB (frizzled-related protein). In the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal skin atlas, this population is observed in both the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) and prenatal skin, though in prenatal skin it is primarily confined to a single sample from the earliest gestation stage sampled (7 post-conception weeks, PCW). Because of this marked rarity and single-sample predominance, FRZB+ fibroblasts were excluded from the fibroblast trajectory analyses used to characterise the broader dermal fibroblast lineage hierarchy. Despite their scarcity in prenatal skin, FRZB-expressing fibroblasts are present in several other developing organs, suggesting that FRZB expression marks a conserved early fibroblast state across embryonic tissues. The SkO system recapitulates this population alongside other dermal fibroblast subtypes, enabling study of this rare state outside the constraints of limited prenatal tissue availability.

## Markers

### Defining marker: FRZB

FRZB is the sole defining marker for this population. It encodes a secreted inhibitor of the Wnt signalling pathway and is used in the atlas to identify and distinguish this early fibroblast state from other dermal fibroblast populations. Gopee et al. (2024) note its rarity in prenatal skin and its exclusion from trajectory analysis:

> "We excluded FRZB + fibroblasts, which were primarily observed in one sample from the earliest gestation stage (7 PCW)"

> "Although rare in prenatal skin, FRZB-expressing fibroblasts were present in several other developing organs"

— Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location and Developmental Context

FRZB+ early fibroblasts are identified in the dermal compartment of the hair-bearing skin organoid (SkO) as well as in prenatal skin, with their prenatal skin presence restricted to the earliest gestation stage examined (7 PCW). Their presence across multiple developing organs suggests that FRZB marks a broadly conserved early fibroblast state rather than one specific to skin. Extended Data Fig. 4e in Gopee et al. (2024) documents the cross-organ distribution of FRZB-expressing fibroblasts.

The SkO model generated from pluripotent stem cells recapitulates multiple dermal fibroblast subtypes present in prenatal skin (Lee et al., 2020), providing access to this rare population in a controlled experimental system. However, SkO fibroblasts retain a prenatal rather than adult identity: even after extended culture, they show low probability of correspondence to adult skin fibroblast states (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Functional Significance

FRZB functions as a secreted frizzled-related protein that sequesters Wnt ligands extracellularly, inhibiting canonical Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. In the context of dermal development, Wnt signalling plays a central role in fibroblast differentiation toward hair follicle-associated fates including dermal condensate and dermal papilla formation (Mok et al., 2019; Biggs et al., 2018). The expression of a Wnt antagonist such as FRZB in early fibroblasts may therefore indicate a state of Wnt pathway restraint at the earliest stages of dermal progenitor identity, prior to commitment along hair follicle or interfollicular dermal trajectories.

The role of distinct fibroblast lineages in determining dermal architecture has been characterised in mouse models (Driskell et al., 2013), and the prenatal human skin atlas extends this framework to human development. FRZB+ fibroblasts, by virtue of their exclusion from the main trajectory analysis, remain the least characterised of the early fibroblast states identified in the atlas.

No specific CL term exists for an FRZB-expressing or early-stage dermal fibroblast. CL:0002551 (fibroblast of dermis) is the most appropriate available term. A new CL term is not currently recommended given the extreme rarity of this population and its exclusion from trajectory analyses in the atlas.
