# Outer root sheath
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Cell Ontology: [outer root sheath cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002561) (CL:0002561, exact match)  
Scope: prenatal (fetal)

## Summary

Outer root sheath (ORS) cells form the outermost epithelial layer of the hair follicle, contiguous with the basal layer of the interfollicular epidermis. In the prenatal skin atlas, ORS cells are identified by SLC26A7 expression and are present from 14 PCW (Gopee et al., 2024). ORS cells differentiate along the ORS/CL trajectory from DPYSL2+ basal cells, which involves upregulation of BARX2 and SOX9. The ORS harbours the hair follicle stem cell niche (bulge region) that is responsible for hair follicle regeneration throughout postnatal life.

## Markers

- **SLC26A7**: Defining marker of ORS in the atlas.
  > "we identified HF cells from 14 PCW in our scRNA-seq data, which comprised placode, matrix (SHH + ), outer root sheath (ORS) (SLC26A7 + ), companion layer (CL), inner root sheath (IRS) and cuticle and cortex cells (cuticle/cortex; part of the inner layers of the HF)"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **BARX2, SOX9**: Previously reported genes related to ORS differentiation, upregulated along the ORS/CL trajectory.
  > "Along the ORS/CL trajectory, we identified new genes upregulated by DPYSL2 + basal cells, such as AGR2, and previously reported genes related to ORS differentiation (BARX2 and SOX9)"
  > -- Gopee et al. (2024)

- **AGR2**: A new gene identified as upregulated by DPYSL2+ basal cells along the ORS/CL trajectory.

## Developmental Trajectory

ORS cells differentiate along the ORS/CL trajectory from POSTN+ basal cells via DPYSL2+ basal cells:

> "Inferred trajectory and pseudotime analysis of epidermal cells in the integrated prenatal skin and SkO data predicted the differentiation of POSTN + basal cells into two paths: ORS/CL trajectory, comprising DPYSL2 + basal cells, ORS and CL; and IRS trajectory, involving placode, matrix, cuticle/cortex and IRS"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Location

The ORS forms the outermost epithelial sheath of the hair follicle, separating the follicle from the surrounding dermis. It is contiguous with the basal layer of the interfollicular epidermis and contains the bulge stem cell niche.

## Function

The ORS provides structural support to the hair follicle, separates the follicle from the dermis, and contains epithelial stem cells at its bulge region. These stem cells replenish the hair follicle during cycling and can also contribute to wound repair.

## Disease Relevance

> "Genes harbouring mutations known to cause reduced hair growth (hypotrichosis) or abnormally shaped hair (for example, pili torti) (Supplementary Table 12) were expressed along the ORS/CL trajectory, IRS trajectory and hair fibroblast trajectory pseudotimes"
> -- Gopee et al. (2024)

## Structure / Morphology

The ORS is composed of multiple layers of keratinocytes forming a cylinder that envelopes the inner root sheath and hair shaft. It expresses basal-type keratins (K5, K14) and is biochemically similar to the epidermal basal layer.
