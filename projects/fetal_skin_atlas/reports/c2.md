# Proliferating Keratinocyte (c2) in Adult Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: adult
Source dataset: Reynolds et al. (2021), integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [basal cell of epidermis](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0002187) (CL:0002187, broad match — no exact CL term)

## Summary

The c2 cluster represents proliferating keratinocytes in adult human skin, as defined by Reynolds et al. (2021). The Figure 4d legend provides the complete cluster mapping: "undifferentiated KC (clusters 1, 5), proliferating (cluster 2), differentiated KC (clusters 3, 4, 6, 7), differentiated KC\* (cluster 8)." Proliferating keratinocytes are characterised by expression of cell cycle genes CDK1 and PCNA, with intermediate levels of basal keratins and lower expression of suprabasal differentiation markers. They correspond to the "mitotic" cluster identified in the earlier Cheng et al. (2018) epidermal scRNA-seq and the "proliferation" cluster in Negri and Watt's (2022) re-analysis of the Reynolds dataset. These cells are located in the basal compartment and represent the actively dividing fraction of the epidermis — likely transit-amplifying cells that amplify the keratinocyte pool before terminal differentiation.

## Markers

### Cell cycle genes: CDK1, PCNA, MKI67

Reynolds et al. (2021) define the proliferating group by two markers:

> "Proliferating keratinocytes (CDK1+,PCNA+) have lower expression of suprabasal cell transcripts e.g. KRT1, KRT10 that characterize differentiated keratinocytes"
>
> — Reynolds et al. (2021)

Cheng et al. (2018) identified the equivalent cluster and confirmed the broader cell cycle signature:

> "One showed coordinate elevation of more than a dozen well-recognized DNA synthesis and cell division transcripts, such as PCNA and KI67. We therefore termed this cluster 'mitotic.'"
>
> — Cheng et al. (2018)

Negri and Watt (2022) corroborated this in their re-analysis:

> "The proliferation cluster expresses high levels of cycling cells, including CDK1 and MKI67"
>
> — Negri and Watt (2022)

### Intermediate keratin levels

Proliferating keratinocytes show intermediate levels of basal keratins KRT5/KRT14, distinguishing them from the strongly KRT5/KRT14-high undifferentiated (c1, c5) clusters:

> "The four remaining keratinocyte clusters all showed intermediate levels of KRT5 and KRT14"
>
> — Cheng et al. (2018)

## Location

### Basal compartment of the epidermis

Negri and Watt (2022) place the proliferation cluster within the basal layer:

> "On the basis of the expression of K5 and K14, we could assign four clusters to the basal layerdbasal I, II, and III, and proliferationdin good agreement with scRNA-seq of neonatal foreskin keratinocytes"
>
> — Negri and Watt (2022)

However, Cheng et al. (2018) note that cell cycle activity is not strictly confined to the basal layer:

> "charting these cell cycle-related transcripts against KRT10 expression in our data suggests that they remain active well into suprabasal layers"
>
> — Cheng et al. (2018)

## Function

### Epidermal renewal via transit amplification

Proliferating keratinocytes are the actively dividing cells that sustain epidermal homeostasis. In the classical model, they correspond to transit-amplifying cells:

> "slowly cycling stem cell that produces daughterdtransit-amplifyingdcells that undergo a few rounds of divisions before moving into the suprabasal layers and undergoing terminal differentiation"
>
> — Negri and Watt (2022)

### Disease relevance: expansion in psoriasis

The proliferating fraction is markedly expanded in psoriatic epidermis, reflecting the hyperproliferative nature of the disease:

> "psoriatic epidermis is remarkable for expansion of the mitotic subfraction"
>
> — Cheng et al. (2018)

Reynolds et al. (2021) also showed significantly altered proliferating keratinocyte proportions in both lesional AD (p = 6.5×10⁻¹⁰) and lesional psoriasis (p = 8.1×10⁻¹⁴) compared to healthy skin.
