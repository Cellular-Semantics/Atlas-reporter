# Melanocyte (Organoid)

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: organoid
Source dataset: Lee et al. (2020) SkO model, integrated into Gopee et al. (2024)
Cell Ontology: [melanocyte](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000148) (CL:0000148, exact match)

## Summary

Melanocytes are neural crest-derived pigment cells identified in the SkO model (Lee et al., 2020) and prenatal human skin. In the Gopee et al. (2024) atlas, melanocytes are part of the ectoderm-derived compartment. The SkO showed notably accelerated melanocyte differentiation compared to other lineages, with alignment to adult cell states as early as 4 weeks of culture. This rapid maturation contrasts with fibroblasts, mural and Schwann cells, which maintained a prenatal-like state even after 19 weeks of SkO culture.

## Markers

Melanocytes express melanin biosynthesis enzymes (TYR, TYRP1, DCT), melanocyte lineage transcription factors (MITF, SOX10), and melanosome-associated proteins (PMEL, MLANA).

## Location

In prenatal skin, melanocytes reside in the basal epidermis and developing hair follicles. In the SkO, they occupy equivalent positions within the organoid epithelium.

## Function

Melanocytes synthesise melanin in specialised organelles (melanosomes) and transfer it to surrounding keratinocytes, providing pigmentation and UV protection. Their rapid differentiation in the SkO suggests that the organoid microenvironment efficiently supports melanocyte maturation.
