# CCL19+ Fibroblasts in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000057) (CL:0000057, broad match)

## Summary

CCL19+ fibroblasts are a stromal cell subtype identified in the Gopee et al. (2024) prenatal human skin atlas as one of several transcriptionally distinct dermal fibroblast populations annotated from single-cell RNA sequencing data (7–17 post-conception weeks, PCW). The defining feature of this subtype is high expression of CCL19, a CC-chemokine that binds its cognate receptor CCR7 on naive and effector T cells, dendritic cells, and other CCR7-expressing leucocytes. The atlas catalogues multiple prenatal dermal fibroblast subsets — HOXC5+, WNT2+, PEAR1+, FRZB+, and CCL19+, among others — that arise from a common fibroblast progenitor and exhibit distinct temporal enrichment across gestation. Overall, prenatal skin fibroblasts display an immune-suppressive, pro-regenerative gene expression programme, consistent with the fetal capacity for scarless healing. Contextual evidence from adult inflammatory skin diseases and cancer biology indicates that CCL19-producing stromal fibroblasts recruit CCR7+ T cells to sites of immune activity and occupy perivascular niches, suggesting the CCL19+ fibroblast may play analogous roles in establishing the immune microenvironment of developing skin.

## Markers

The CCL19+ fibroblast is defined by expression of CCL19 (C-C motif chemokine ligand 19). Within the Gopee 2024 atlas the prenatal skin dermal fibroblasts form a developmental hierarchy:

> "Inferred trajectory analysis predicted that HOXC5 + early fibroblasts (located in the upper dermis (Fig. 2f) and abundant before 11 PCW (Extended Data Fig. 3c)) differentiated along two paths: the first (hair fibroblast trajectory) formed hair-specialized fibroblasts (pre-Dc cells, the Dc and Dp) and the second (dermal fibroblast trajectory) formed WNT2 + fibroblasts and PEAR1 + fibroblasts (abundant after 11 PCW)"

CCL19+ fibroblasts are annotated as a distinct dermal subtype in this atlas (Extended Data Fig. 3c). The broader prenatal fibroblast transcriptional programme, within which this subset sits, is characterised by upregulation of immune-suppressive and regenerative genes:

> "prenatal skin fibroblasts had upregulated genes involved in immune suppression (CD200), regulation of inflammation (for example, RAMP2) and tissue regeneration (MDK)"

> "Genes associated with a pro-inflammatory fibroblast phenotype (APOE, IGFBP7 and ITM2A) 43,52 were also upregulated during the transition from HOXC5 + fibroblasts into PEAR1 + fibroblasts"

## Location

CCL19+ fibroblasts reside within the dermal compartment of prenatal human skin profiled from 7 to 17 PCW. The HOXC5+ progenitor from which all dermal fibroblasts derive is located in the upper dermis early in gestation. In inflammatory adult skin, CCL19-expressing fibroblasts localise specifically to leukocyte-rich areas:

> "CCL19-expressing fibroblasts have been identified in atopic dermatitis and localize to leukocyte-rich areas of the skin"

In cancer biology, CCL19-producing fibroblastic stromal cells occupy perivascular positions:

> "Ccl19-expressing FSCs reside in peri-vascular niches within LLC tumors and T cell recruitment is impaired upon Ccl19 gene deletion suggesting an early role of FSCs in forming immune-stimulating stromal niches"

## Function

### Immune cell recruitment via CCR7 signalling

The primary functional role attributed to CCL19+ fibroblasts across tissues is the chemokine-mediated recruitment of CCR7-expressing immune cells. Fibroblast-derived CCL19 signals through CCR7 on a wide range of T cell populations:

> "CCL19 expressed by fibroblasts and basal keratinocytes was received by CCR7 expressed on CD8 + Pro T cells, naive T cells, CD8 + T1 cells, CD8 + T2 cells, Tregs, and Texh cells"

CCL19 produced by fibroblasts acts synergistically with co-expressed chemokines to amplify T cell recruitment:

> "LP keratinocytes and fibroblasts specifically secrete a combination of CXCL9, CXCL10, and CCL19 cytokines"

### Lymphoid niche formation

In secondary lymphoid organs, CCL19 is produced by fibroblastic reticular cell (FRC) equivalents — the lymphoid tissue organiser (LTo) cells — as part of the programme that recruits lymphoid tissue inducer cells and establishes T cell zones:

> "Mature LTi express lymphotoxin alpha 1 beta 2 (LTa 1 β 2 ) which binds LTbR in activated LTo, resulting in further LTo maturation and expression of intercellular adhesion molecule 1 (ICAM1), vascular cell adhesion molecule 1 (VCAM1), chemokine (C-C motif) ligand 19 (Ccl19) and 21 (Ccl21), and Cxcl13 which recruit more LTi and promote interactions between LTi and LTo"

In cancer, CCL19-producing fibroblastic stromal cells similarly promote immune-stimulating stromal niches:

> "In human and mouse lung cancer, Ccl19 producing fibroblastic stromal cells (FSC) correlate with increased CD8 + T cell infiltration and tumor growth control"

### Potential contribution to prenatal immune surveillance and scarless healing

Prenatal skin fibroblasts collectively support the remarkable capacity of fetal skin to heal without scarring through a downregulated inflammatory programme:

> "A combination of fibroblast and macrophage-associated molecular features potentially contribute to the ability of prenatal skin to heal without scarring, including the presence of fibroblast progenitors, a downregulated immune milieu and reduced collagen expression"

The CCL19+ fibroblast subset's expression of a canonical lymphoid-homing chemokine — in a tissue context with nascent T cell populations and active hair follicle morphogenesis — suggests it may participate in immune surveillance and niche organisation during normal skin development, potentially modulating the balance between immune tolerance and controlled immune recruitment that characterises the fetal skin environment.
