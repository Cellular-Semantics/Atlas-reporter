# CCL19+ Fibroblasts in Prenatal Human Skin

Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: [10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x))
Scope: fetal
Cell Ontology: [fibroblast](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000057) (CL:0000057, broad match — no exact CL term)

## Summary

CCL19+ fibroblasts are a dermal fibroblast subset identified in prenatal human skin (7-17 PCW) by Gopee et al. (2024). This population is defined by expression of CCL19, a chemokine classically associated with immune cell recruitment and lymphoid tissue organization. CCL19+ fibroblasts are annotated as a distinct cluster within the fetal skin stromal compartment. In other tissues, CCL19-expressing fibroblasts function as fibroblastic reticular cells that organize immune niches, recruit T cells and dendritic cells via the CCR7 receptor, and support formation of tertiary lymphoid structures (Johansson-Percival and Ganss, 2021; Kersh et al., 2024). Their presence in prenatal skin suggests a potential role in establishing early immune microenvironments during skin development.

## Markers

The defining marker for this subset is **CCL19** (C-C motif chemokine ligand 19), which encodes a chemokine that signals through the CCR7 receptor. The full list of differentially expressed genes for this cluster is provided in Supplementary Table 3 of Gopee et al. (2024).

CCL19 is a ligand for CCR7 and is involved in the recruitment of naive T cells and dendritic cells. In skin disease contexts, CCL19-expressing fibroblasts have been identified in lesional skin of atopic dermatitis and lichen planus, where they localize to leukocyte-rich areas (Kersh et al., 2024).

## Location

### In prenatal skin

CCL19+ fibroblasts are present within the dermal compartment of prenatal human skin between 7 and 17 PCW. Broad cell labels and fine-grained annotations of cell states were assigned on the basis of differentially expressed genes (Gopee et al., 2024).

## Function

### 1. Potential immune cell recruitment

CCL19 is one of the primary chemokines for the recruitment of T cells and dendritic cells via the CCR7 receptor. In other tissues, CCL19+ fibroblasts play a crucial role in forming tertiary lymphoid structures and establishing local immune microenvironments (Johansson-Percival and Ganss, 2021).

### 2. Potential lymphoid niche formation

In tumour settings, CCL19-producing fibroblastic stromal cells correlate with increased CD8+ T cell infiltration and reside in peri-vascular niches. CCL19-expressing fibroblastic reticular cells have been shown to organize functional lymphoid structures in diverse tissues (Johansson-Percival and Ganss, 2021).
