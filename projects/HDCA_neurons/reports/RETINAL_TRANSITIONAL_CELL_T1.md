## Summary

Transitional retinal cells are the bridge populations between cycling neural progenitors and post-mitotic terminal cell types. HDCA's T1 cluster is marked by SPP1, RORB, RGS16, MAB21L1 and CRABP1 — a signature consistent with early-born (RGC- and cone-biased) neurogenic intermediates.

## HDCA atlas evidence

**Cell count:** 1,633 cells. HDCA classifies this cluster as `broad_celltype = AUDIOVISUAL_NEURONAL`, `systems = AUDIOVISUAL_SYSTEM`.

### HDCA paper text


HDCA's retina characterisation includes early neurogenic intermediates:

> "embryonic retina was characterised by expression of ATOH7, required for retinal ganglion cell (RGC) development71; PRDM13, which specifies amacrine cell fate in mouse72; GADD45A, which characterises early neurogenic cells in mouse retina73; and TRH, which encodes thyrotropin releasing hormone"
>
> — Webb et al. (2026), HDCA
