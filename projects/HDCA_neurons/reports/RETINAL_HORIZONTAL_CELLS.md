# RETINAL_HORIZONTAL_CELLS — Retinal horizontal cell

Cell Ontology: [retina horizontal cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000745) (CL:0000745, **exact match** — ONECUT2+/PAX6+/SIX3+ horizontal cell signature — CL exact match.)

## Summary

Horizontal cells are early-born retinal interneurons that mediate lateral interactions in the outer plexiform layer. The HDCA horizontal cell cluster expresses ONECUT2 (the canonical horizontal-cell determinant), PAX6, SIX3, RORB, MAB21L1 and CRABP1 — exactly matching the ONECUT-family horizontal-cell signature in mouse and human retina.

## HDCA atlas evidence

**Cell count:** 68 cells. HDCA classifies this cluster as `broad_celltype = AUDIOVISUAL_NEURONAL`, `systems = AUDIOVISUAL_SYSTEM`.

### HDCA paper text


HDCA places horizontal cells within the embryonic retina:

> "embryonic retina was characterised by expression of ATOH7, required for retinal ganglion cell (RGC) development71; PRDM13, which specifies amacrine cell fate in mouse72; GADD45A, which characterises early neurogenic cells in mouse retina73; and TRH, which encodes thyrotropin releasing hormone"
>
> — Webb et al. (2026), HDCA
