# SENSORY_NEURONAL_OTIC_PLACODE — Sensory neuron, otic placode-derived (statoacoustic/cochleovestibular neuron)

Cell Ontology: [neuroplacodal cell](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000032) (CL:0000032, **broad match** — HDCA-assigned. CL:0000032 describes the placodal *ectoderm*, not the differentiated sensory neuron derived from it. HDCA cells are POU4F1+/EYA1+/SIX1+ delaminated post-placodal sensory neurons. Better...)

## Summary

The otic placode is a cranial ectodermal thickening that gives rise to the inner ear and the cochleovestibular (VIIIth cranial) sensory neurons. The HDCA otic-placode-derived sensory neuron cluster expresses SIX1, EYA1, EYA2, ISL1, POU4F1 (BRN3A) and FOXG1 — the canonical otic-placode neurogenic signature — together with FGF10 and pan-neuronal markers (DCX, GAP43, ELAVL4, STMN2, NEFL, NEFM). This is a non-NCC origin for sensory neurons; HDCA highlights this dual origin (NCC vs placode) as a paper-level finding.

## HDCA atlas evidence

**Cell count:** 1,485 cells. HDCA classifies this cluster as `broad_celltype = PNS_PLACODE`, `systems = NERVOUS_SYSTEM`.

### HDCA paper text


HDCA highlights placodal vs NCC origin of sensory neurons as a paper-level finding:

> "the full repertoire of NCC-derivatives (SOX10+ glial and PRPH+ neuronal) across the embryo and placode-derived sensory neurons in cranial regions (SIX1, EYA1, PRPH)"
>
> — Webb et al. (2026), HDCA

HDCA also notes that placode-derived sensory neurons have been studied mainly in model organisms:

> "Placode- and NCC-derived states have been characterised largely in model organisms"
>
> — Webb et al. (2026), HDCA
