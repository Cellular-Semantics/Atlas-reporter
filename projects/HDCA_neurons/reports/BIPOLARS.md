# BIPOLARS — Retinal bipolar cell

Cell Ontology: [retinal bipolar neuron](http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/CL_0000748) (CL:0000748, **exact match** — HDCA assigned CL:0000103 (generic bipolar neuron); CL:0000748 is the specific retinal subterm with definition matching RORB+/NEUROD4+/RCVRN+ retinal bipolar profile. Refined from HDCA assignment.)

## Summary

Bipolar cells are the late-born vertical interneurons connecting photoreceptors to retinal ganglion cells. HDCA's bipolar cluster expresses RORB (the bipolar-cell-class determinant), RCVRN, NEUROD4, GNB3, UNC119, CA10, SYT1, SNAP25, SYP — a signature matching the canonical post-mitotic bipolar transcriptional programme.

## HDCA atlas evidence

**Cell count:** 1,334 cells. HDCA classifies this cluster as `broad_celltype = AUDIOVISUAL_NEURONAL`, `systems = AUDIOVISUAL_SYSTEM`.

### HDCA paper text


HDCA places bipolar cells within the embryonic retina lineage:

> "embryonic retina was characterised by expression of ATOH7, required for retinal ganglion cell (RGC) development71; PRDM13, which specifies amacrine cell fate in mouse72; GADD45A, which characterises early neurogenic cells in mouse retina73; and TRH, which encodes thyrotropin releasing hormone"
>
> — Webb et al. (2026), HDCA
